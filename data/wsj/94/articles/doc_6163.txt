Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms rose in the latest week, although most changes were moderate.
The average yield on six-month small denomination, or "savings," CDs at major banks rose to 2.77% from 2.71% in the week ended yesterday, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The Wilmingtom, Del., information-service concern said the average yield on five-year savings CDs increased to 4.49% from 4.42%.
The average yield on six-month CDs sold by major brokerage firms rose to 3.17% from 3.02%, Banxquote reported.
On five-year, broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 4.64% from 4.48%, the survey said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on six-month certificates at major banks rose to 2.88% from 2.77%, according to Banxquote.
The average yield on five-year jumbos rose to 4.75% from 4.61%.

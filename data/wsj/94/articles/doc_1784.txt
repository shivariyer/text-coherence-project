A strong bond market carried most stocks higher, but blue chips were hit by a late burst of program selling.
Traders said large sell orders for many big cyclical stocks hit the New York Stock Exchange floor in the final minutes of trading.
A 15-point gain in the Dow Jones Industrial Average was reversed to a loss of 1.44 to 3848.15.
The selling knocked Minnesota Mining & Manufacturing for a loss of 1/2 to 102 1/2 at the close; it was at 103 5/8 just a few minutes earlier.
International Paper ended with a drop of 7/8 to 68 1/4; it had been at 69 1/8 ahead of the close.
Broad-based indexes were barely affected by the late selling spree, whose sudden nature left some traders momentarily convinced that their quote machines had malfunctioned.
Advancing issues on the New York Stock Exchange led decliners about 1,346 to 803.
Observers said the sudden drop could have been related to a single institution bailing out of positions, or to trading strategies tied to tomorrow's "triple-witching" expiration of futures and options.
The market was affected by program trading several times during the day.
Program selling in the morning had initially helped depress stocks, despite gains in the bond market.
Stocks recovered from the early losses as the rally in the bond market mounted.
The 30-year Treasury bond gained nearly a point, pushing its yield down to 6.79%.
Investors in both stocks and bonds were reassured by inflation data.
After a positive reading on February producer prices Tuesday, the Labor Department yesterday said the consumer price index edged up 0.3%, matching expectations.
The Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index closed with a gain of 2.41 to 469.42; the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index advanced 1.35 to 260.34; and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index jumped 2.29 to 445.00.
The Nasdaq Composite Index rose 5.47, to 798.99.
That is less than 1.5 points from its high set Jan. 31.
The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index rose 1.69 to 469.82.
Northrop jumped 1 7/8 to 43 3/4 amid rumors that Martin Marietta or another large aerospace concern could make a bid for the Los Angeles company.
Northrop and Martin Marietta have both offered to acquire Grumman.
Northrop's bid of $60 a share for Grumman topped a $55-a-share offer from Martin Marietta, but traders have speculated that new, higher offers for Grumman will arise.
Spokesmen for Northrop, Martin Marietta and Grumman all declined to comment on the rumors.
Shares of Grumman eased 5/8 to 65 1/4.
Martin Marietta lost 3/8 to 44 1/8.
Nike climbed 3 to 57 1/2.
The Beaverton, Ore., footwear company had earnings of 85 cents a share for the third quarter ended last month, down from $1.16 a year earlier, but in line with analysts' projections.
Nike said it expects its business to resume growth in the next fiscal year.
U.S. Surgical jumped 2 1/4 to 18 3/8 after the company projected that its first-quarter loss will be narrower than the 26-cent-ashare operating loss it expects to report for the fourth quarter.
The Norwalk, Conn., medical-device maker said its restructuring and cost-cutting initiatives will be completed this month.
MFS Communications jumped 4 3/8 to 35 1/8 on the Nasdaq Stock Market after the Omaha, Neb., company launched a $9-a-share tender offer to acquire Centex Telemanagement of San Francisco.
Centex, a small capitalization stock, rocketed 5 5/8, or 115%, to 10 1/2 on Nasdaq.
Adobe Systems lost 3 to 29 1/2 following news it plans to acquire Aldus through a stock-swap transaction.
Adobe, of Mountain View, Calif., will pay 1.15 shares of its common stock for each share of Seattle-based Aldus.
Aldus, also a small-cap issue, surged 6 to 32 1/4 on Nasdaq.
Amoco advanced 2 1/2 to 54 5/8.
Dean Witter Reynolds analyst Eugene Nowak upgraded his rating on the stock to "accumulate" from "neutral," calling the stock "attractively priced."
Mr. Nowak also cited a planned restructuring at the Chicago oil and gas company.
Trading in Cia. de Telefonos de Chile's American depositary receipts remained halted all day on the Big Board, but they traded at between 100 and 105 in late action off the exchange floor.
The issue tumbled 15 3/4 to 100 1/2 Tuesday before trading was stopped to allow for dissemination of news.
Chilean regulators had proposed new rate tariffs that the telephone-services company said would hurt its business.
The Big Board said exchange trading in the ADR was suspended pending release of additional information.
Telefonos de Mexico's American depositary receipts added 7/8 to 61 3/4 on Big Board-leading volume of 10 million.
The Mexican stock market rebounded somewhat from a bruising tumble Tuesday, triggered by political uncertainty in Mexico and worries about the kidnapping of a prominent Mexican banking and brokerage firm executive.
3Com rallied 2 7/8 to 62 7/8 on Nasdaq.
Late Tuesday, the Santa Clara, Calif., company reported earnings from operations of 72 cents a share for the third quarter ended Feb. 28, up from 31 cents a share in the year-earlier period, which included a charge.
International Game Technology added 1 5/8 to 32.
Smith Barney Shearson added shares of the Reno, Nev., gambling equipment company to a recommended list.

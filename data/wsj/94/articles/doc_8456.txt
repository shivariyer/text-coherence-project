Bombardier Corp. -- $400 million of asset-backed certificates priced in two parts by underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co. and Scotia McLeod (USA) Inc.
The certificates were backed by lines of credit to nearly 3,900 U.S. dealers of equipment ranging from recreational ski equipment to consumer electronics and lawnmowers.
The issue was sold through special-purpose trust Bombardier Receivables Master Trust I. The $376.5 million of floating-rate Class A asset-backed certificates, Series 1994-1, was priced to float 20 basis points above the one-month London Interbank Offered Rate.
The certificates have an average life of three years.
The $23.5 million of fixed-rate Class B asset-backed certificates, Series 1994-1, was priced as 5.2s at 99.8125 to yield 5.32%, a spread of 73 basis points above the Treasury's 6.875% notes of April 1997.
The certificates have an average life of 3.25 years.
Additional credit support was provided by a 7.6% subordinated seller's interest and a reserve fund of 75 basis points.
Bombardier Corp. is a unit Bombardier Inc.

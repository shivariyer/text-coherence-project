Westmoreland Energy Inc. and LG&E Power Inc. said they have completed financing for a $93.3 million joint-venture independent power project in Roanoke, Va.
The joint venture will construct a 44-megawatt coal-fired cogeneration facility and sell electricity to North Carolina Power, a unit of Dominion Resources Inc., and steam to Patch Rubber Co., a unit of Myers Industries Inc., Akron, Ohio.
Westmoreland Coal Co., parent of Westmoreland Energy, will provide 79.5% of the project's coal requirements.
LG&E Power, a unit of LG&E Energy Corp., Louisville, Ky., specializes in developing cogeneration power projects.
LG&E Energy is a utility holding company.

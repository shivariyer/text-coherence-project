Consolidated Freightways Inc. said that while its long-haul trucking business continues to lag, a surge in air freight and regional trucking drove fourth-quarter earnings into the black.
Consolidated in Palo Alto, Calif., reported fourth-quarter net income of $12.1 million, or 33 cents a share, compared with a loss of $18.6 million, or 53 cents a share, a year earlier.
The company's year-ago results included $17.3 million, or 51 cents a share, for nonrecurring charges.
Fourth-quarter revenue was $1.1 billion, up 8.5% from $1 billion a year ago.
For all of 1993 Consolidated reported net income of $31.6 million, or 87 cents a share, compared with a 1992 loss of $97.7 million, or $2.78 a share.
The 1992 results included one-time charges and the cumulative effect of an accounting change.
Revenue for 1993 was $4.2 billion, up 3.4% from $4.1 billion in 1992.
The stock closed at $26.25 a share, up 75 cents, in composite trading on the New York Stock Echange.

Adolph Coors Co. reported a net loss of $75.1 million, or $1.97 a share, for the 12-week fourth quarter, including a one-time pretax charge of about $109.5 million, for voluntary severance packages for 679 white-collar employees and for work-place restructuring.
Additional one-time charges raised the total charges for the quarter to $122.5 million before taxes, or $1.98 a share.
A net loss for the year-earlier quarter totaled $22.7 million, or 60 cents a share.
Net sales declined to $337.3 million from $339.2 million.
The Golden, Colo., company said operating profit from continuing operations was $300,000 compared with a loss of $3.5 million from continuing operations the year-earlier quarter, in spite of particularly competitive market conditions in 1993.
For the year, the company reported pretax operating profit of $77.8 million, up from $73.3 million in 1992.

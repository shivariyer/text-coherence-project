The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
CIT Group Holdings Inc. -- $150 million debt in two parts priced by underwriters led by UBS Securities Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first tranche, $100 million of medium-term notes due Feb. 28, 1997, was priced initially as 5.5s at par to yield 5.591%.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 40 basis points above Treasurys.
The second tranche, $50 million of floating-rate notes due Feb. 28, 1997, was priced initially at par.
The notes will float and pay monthly at 10 basis points above one-month the London Interbank Offered Rate.
The coupon will be set Monday.
Both parts are rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
CIT is owned by Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank Ltd. and Chemical Banking Corp.
Cidco Inc. -- initial offering of 3.3 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Alex. Brown & Sons Inc. and Montgomery Securities.
Electronic Fab Technology Corp. -- initial offering of 1.5 million common shares priced at $7.50 each.
Managing underwriters were Dain Bosworth Inc. and Stephens Inc.
Rock-Tenn Co. -- initial offering of 4.3 million common shares priced at $16.50 each through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
Santander Finance Ltd. -- $250 million of noncumulative, perpetual, preferred stock priced with a 7.9% dividend yield.
The 10,000 shares were priced at $25 each, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The shares are noncallable for five years and rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Santander Finance is a unit of Banco Santander SA.
Connecticut -- $150 million of Series 1994A bonds for transportation-infrastructure purposes apparently won by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. group.
Yields range from 3% in 1995 to 5.8% in 2014.
Bonds due in 1995-2008 have a single-A-1 rating from Moody's and a double-A-minus rating from S&P.
The Financial Guaranty Insurance Co. is insuring bonds due in 2009-14, and those securities carry triple-A ratings from Moody's and S&P.
Fulton County (Georgia) School District -- $175 million of improvement bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
Yields range from 3.1% in 1995 to 5.85% in 2021.
The bonds have double-A ratings from Moody's and S&P.
Westchester County (New York) Industrial Development Agency -- $113 million of 1994 Series A refunding bonds priced by a Smith Barney Shearson Inc. group.
Yields range from 4.45% in 1998 to 5.75% in 2009.
The bonds are insured by Ambac Indemnity Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from Moody's and S&P.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Prudential Securities Inc.
The offering, Trust 1994-56, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 6.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.15% and weighted average maturity of 358 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of Remic securities offered by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-53, is backed by the principle-only classes of Strips Trust 251, Strips Trust 237 and Strips Trust 203.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $150 million of Remic offered by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.
The offering, Trust 1994-57, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.925% and weighted average maturity of 349 months.
Lehman Home Equity Loan Trust -- $208.5 million of assetbacked certificates priced to yield 25 basis points above the one-month Libor by Lehman Brothers Inc., the parent company.
The issue, Lehman Home Equity Loan Trust 1994-1, is backed by subordinate classes from three Household Finance Corp. home-equity transactions: 1988-1, 1989-1 and 1989-2.
The certificates in the issue have an average life of 4.25 years based on a 29% conditional prepayment rate.
They were rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P on a 100% insurance wrap provided by Capital Markets Assurance Corp.

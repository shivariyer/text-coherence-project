WESTINGHOUSE ELECTRIC Corp., Pittsburgh, said it issued about $520 million of preferred equity securities in a private placement.
Proceeds will be used for debt reduction and rebuilding "financial strength," Chairman Michael Jordan said in a statement.
The issue is mandatorily convertible into common stock June 1, 1997.
Thirty-six million depositary shares were sold, with each share representing one-tenth of a share of the company's Series C conversion preferred stock.
The price was $14.44 a depositary share, with an annual dividend of $1.30 a share until conversion.
The underwriters were Morgan Stanley & Co.; Goldman, Sachs & Co.; and Lehman Brothers Inc.

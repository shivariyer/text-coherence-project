The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
ANZ Banking Group Ltd. -- $250 million of subordinated notes due Feb. 1, 2004, priced as 6.25s at 99.705 to yield 6.29%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 65 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Bell Atlantic-New Jersey -- $250 million of debentures priced through a competitive bid won by Morgan Stanley & Co.
The debentures, due Feb. 1, 2004, were priced as 5.875s at 99.81 to yield 5.9%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 25 basis points above Treasurys.
The debentures are rated triple-A by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P. Bell Atlantic-New Jersey is a unit of Bell Atlantic Corp.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $115 million of notes due Feb. 7, 2001, priced at par to yield 5.625%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for three years, were priced at a spread of 46 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
USX Corp. -- $300 million of notes due Feb. 15, 2004, priced as 7.2s at 99.786 to yield 7.23%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 160 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Baa3 by Moody's and double-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Digital Link Corp. -- initial offering of 2.6 million common shares priced at $14 apiece through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co. and Wessels Arnold & Henderson.
Post Properties Inc. -- three million common shares priced at $29.50 apiece through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Western Digital Corp. -- 6.6 million common shares priced at $11.75 apiece through underwriters led by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Quebec -- $1 billion of debentures due Feb. 9, 2024, priced as 7.125s at 99.485 to yield 7.167%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 82 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Salomon Brothers Inc.
The offering, Series G027, is backed by Government National Mortgage Association 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 8% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 348 months.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $158 million of realestate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Merrill Lynch & Co.
The offering, Series 1690, is backed by Class PA from the agency's Remic Tranche 1611.
Compagnie Bancaire (France) -- two billion French francs of 6.5% Eurobonds due Dec. 3, 2004, via Credit Commercial de France.
Bonds will trade interchangeably with two billion francs of identical bonds outstanding.
Issue and reoffer price 101.5 to yield 6.3%, a margin of 60 basis points above new 10-year French Treasury.
Fees 0.375.
Council of Europe Resettlement Fund (supranational) -- $100 million of corridor floating-rate Euronotes due February 1995 at issue and reoffer price par via Salomon Brothers Ltd.
Payable quarterly at 60 basis points over three-month London Interbank Offered Rate when Libor is within 0% to 4% in first six months and 0% to 4.25% in second six months.
Fees undisclosed.
Daimler-Benz North America Corp. (German parent) -- $60 million of zero-coupon Eurobonds due Feb. 17, 1995, at issue price 99 via J.P. Morgan Securities Ltd.
Redemption amount linked to value of J.P. Morgan Emerging Markets Bond Index.
Guarantor Daimler-Benz AG.
Fees 0.2 point.
Deutsche Pfandbrief- und Hypothekenbank AG (Germany) -- one billion marks of floating-rate notes due February 2003 at issue price 99.97 via Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale.
Payable semiannually at six-month Frankfurt Interbank Offered Rate flat.
Fees 0.2 point, less 0.02 point for lead manager.
Goldman Sachs Group Ltd. (U.S.) -- 150 million Canadian dollars of floating-rate Euronotes due Feb. 18, 2004, at issue and reoffer price 99.8 via Goldman Sachs International Ltd.
Payable quarterly flat at three-month Banker's Acceptance Rate for Canadian dollars.
Minimum coupon 5%.
Fees 0.375.
Rabobank Nederland (Netherlands) -- 500 million Dutch guilders of 5.875% Eurobonds due Mar. 4, 2004, at issue price 101.
Reoffered 100.25 to yield 5.841%, a margin of 19 basis points above comparable Dutch state loan.
Fees one.
Rabobank Nederland (Netherlands) -- $100 million of foreign exchange rate-linked range floating-rate Euronotes due Feb. 24, 1995, at issue price par via Samuel Montagu & Co.
Payable quarterly at three-month dollar Libor plus 0.25 point, multiplied by number of interest accrual days divided by total number of London business days in coupon period.
Interest accrues on days when mark-dollar exchange rate on Telerate page 3,760 falls between or is equal to 1.62 and 1.88 marks a dollar.
For last two days of each coupon period, foreign exchange rate will be spot rate prevailing on third last day of coupon period.
Fees 0.1.
SNS Groep NV (Netherlands) -- 500 million Dutch guilders of 5.5% Eurobonds due Mar. 1, 1999, at issue price 100.75 via SNS Bank Nederland NV.
Reoffered at 100.05 to yield 5.49%, a margin of 35 basis points above comparable Dutch state loan.
Fees one.
Toronto-Dominion Australia Ltd. (Canadian parent) -- 100 million Australian dollars of 5.75% Eurobonds due March 28, 1997, at issue price 100.9 via Hambros Bank Ltd.
Bonds yield 5.93% at 99.525 co-managers' break-even price, a margin of 28 basis points over comparable Australian government bond.
Guarantor Toronto-Dominion Bank.

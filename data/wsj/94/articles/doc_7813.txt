Stratus Computer Inc., meeting analysts' expectations, reported a fourth-quarter loss of $17.1 million, or 72 cents a share, including a one-time charge of $36.2 million, or $1.54 a share, in connection with recent acquisitions.
A year earlier, the Marlboro, Mass., maker of fault-tolerant computers had earnings of $17.2 million, or 73 cents a share.
Stratus said revenue for latest quarter, ended Jan. 2, rose 10% to $148.1 million, as its core hardware business increased 5%.
Core business direct sales rose 13% on the strength of sales to the U.S. telecommunications industry.
International direct sales declined 26%.
In late New York Stock Exchange trading, Stratus was quoted at $29.625., off 12.5 cents.

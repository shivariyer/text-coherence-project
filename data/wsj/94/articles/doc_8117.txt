Brinker International Inc. said it agreed to acquire Dallas-based On the Border Cafes Inc. for about $32.7 million in Brinker common shares.
Steven Fenstermacher, chief executive officer of On the Border, will continue to direct the Tex-Mex restaurant chain's operations as a vice president of Brinker, according to Debra Smithart, Brinker's chief financial officer.
She added that Brinker plans to expand the 21-unit chain.
Brinker operates more than 400 restaurants, including the Chili's, Grady's American Grill, Romano's Macaroni Grill and Spaggeddies Italian chains.
Brinker closed yesterday at $43.625 a share, down 12.5 cents, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
On the Border closed at $7.25 a share, up 50 cents, in trading on the Nasdaq Stock Market.
Brinker said it expects to complete the acquisition in May, subject to certain conditions and to the approval of On the Border shareholders.

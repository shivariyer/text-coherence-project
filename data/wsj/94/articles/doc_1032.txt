Pep Boys-Manny, Moe & Jack said net income for its fiscal fourth quarter rose 26%, slightly bettering analysts' estimates.
For the quarter ended Jan. 29, net rose to $15.5 million, or 25 cents a share, from $12.3 million, or 20 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose to $296.8 million, up 9% from $272 million.
The Philadelphia-based company said fiscal 1994 is off to a "solid start," with comparable-store sales and labor revenue running ahead of plan.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, the stock closed at $28.25, up $1.25.
For the year, the auto-parts retailer said earnings climbed to $65.5 million, or $1.06 a share, from $54.6 million, or 90 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose 7% to $1.24 billion.
Pep Boys, which operates 386 stores in 28 states, said it expects to open as many as 50 supercenters in fiscal 1994.

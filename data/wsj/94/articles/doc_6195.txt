Stock prices were mixed as the bond market's troubles weighed heavily on trading.
The dollar rose.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average edged down 0.29 point to 3906.03.
Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index slipped 0.71 to 471.05, but the Nasdaq Composite Index rose 3.50 to 782.70.
Economically sensitive stocks continued to outperform with cyclicals such as General Motors, AlliedSignal and Caterpillar leading the Dow Jones industrials.
Offsetting those gains were losses among consumer stocks such as Sears, Procter & Gamble and Philip Morris.
Joseph R. Liro, chief economist at S.G. Warburg, said the bond market's woes won't beleaguer the stock market for long.
He pointed to yesterday's report of further gains in productivity in the fourth quarter.
"Stocks are going to get some reinforcement from earnings news over the next several quarters," Mr. Liro said.
"I don't think the stock market will take its direction from rates."
In the short run, though, some analysts said stocks could get hammered if the bond market, facing a semiannual auction of 30-year Treasury bonds tomorrow, continues to slump.
World-wide, stock prices gain in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index rose 0.36 to 116.19.
Stock prices were mixed.
Volume totaled 318 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,172 issues rose and 962 fell.
The dollar rose.
In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.7640 marks and 108.75 yen, compared with 1.7590 marks and 108.70 yen Monday.

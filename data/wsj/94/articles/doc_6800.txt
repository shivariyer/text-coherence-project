The company's IBM Personal Computer Co. unit named Mark Loughridge as assistant general manager for finance and planning.
Mr. Loughridge, 40 years old, most recently served as assistant controller for corporate finance at IBM.
He succeeds Edward Rogers, 56, chief financial officer of IBM PC, who will retire.
Mr. Rogers, who has spent 31 years at IBM and is credited with helping manage IBM PC's recent turnaround, says he will seek another job.

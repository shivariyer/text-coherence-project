The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
ANR Pipeline Co. -- $125 million of debentures due Feb. 15, 2024, priced as 7.375s at 99.28 to yield 7.435%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 90 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Baa2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Citicorp Securities Inc.
ANR Pipeline is a unit of Coastal Corp.
Banque Paribas (New York branch) -- $300 million of notes due March 1, 2009, priced as 6.875s at 99.215 to yield 6.96%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 102 basis points above the 5.75% Treasury maturing in August 2003.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through Merrill Lynch & Co.
Federal Farm Credit Bank System -- $100 million of medium-term notes due Feb. 22, 1996, priced initially at par to yield 4.44%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of four basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Nortek Inc. -- $190 million of senior, subordinated notes due March 1, 2004, priced as 9.875s at 99.214 to yield 10%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for five years, are rated Caa by Moody's and triple-C by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.
Breed Technologies Inc. -- 2.8 million common shares priced at $28.50 each through underwriters led by Lazard Freres.
India Fund Inc. -- initial offering of 35 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Oppenheimer & Co.
Healthcare Realty Trust Inc. -- 6.2 million common shares priced at $21.375 each, according to the company.
Co-managers of the underwriting group are Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.; Smith Barney Shearson Inc.; and Equitable Securities Corp.

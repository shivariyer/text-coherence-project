Oracle Corp., Redwood City, Calif., said profit more than doubled to $70 million, or 24 cents a share, in the third quarter ended Feb. 28, citing strong sales of newer versions of its database software.
A year earlier, net income was $29 million, or 10 cents a share.
Excluding a nonrecurring litigation charge in the 1993 period of $24 million, or five cents a share, earnings increased 56%.
Sales rose 30% to $483 million from $370 million.
The results were at the upper end of analysts' expectations.
Zack's Investment Research Inc. said the average estimate was 23 cents a share, with all projections ranging from 21 cents to 26 cents.
Oracle said its strongest growth came in newer versions of its database software.
The company said sales of its database software for desktop computers increased 67% over the year-earlier period, while sales of software for the Unix operating system grew 37%.
The company's results were reported after stock markets closed.
Oracle's shares closed at $33.25, down 87.5 cents.

South Korea's Hyundai Motor Co. said it will start operation of its recently expanded production lines on April 1, which will bring the company's annual passenger-car capacity to one million units from the current 900,000.
Hyundai officials said the company recently completed the expansion of its third plant, in Ulsan, south of Seoul, which was producing its compact passenger car Elantra.
With the expansion, the company's total vehicle capacity including trucks and buses will be 1.26 million units a year.
The latest expansion is part of the company's long-term project to increase its annual auto capacity to two million units by the end of the year 2000.

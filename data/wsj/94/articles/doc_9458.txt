Coltec Industries Inc. said fourth-quarter earnings fell 72% on extroardinary charges from early retirement and refinancing of debt.
Profit fell to $6.5 million, or nine cents a share, including charges of $16.8 million, or 24 cents a share.
That compares with net income of $22.6 million, or 33 cents a share, including debt-related charges of $1.6 million, or two cents a share, a year earlier.
Sales rose to $344.2 million from $340.5 million.
Coltec, which makes aerospace, automotive and other industrial products, said it arranged a $415 million revolving credit facility with a group of banks, which will reduce interest costs and has been used to prepay borrowings under a 1992 credit agreement.
For the year, net totaled $47.4 million, or 68 cents a share, including extraordinary charges of $17.8 million, or 26 cents a share.
For the previous year, the company had a net loss of $42 million, or 72 cents a share, including extraordinary charges of $107 million, or $1.83 a share.
Sales fell 2.4% to $1.33 billion from $1.37 billion.

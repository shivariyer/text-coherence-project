Clear Channel Communications Inc. said fourth-quarter revenue surged 50% and net income nearly tripled, surpassing analysts' estimates and sending the broadcasting company's stock higher.
Clear Channel, based in San Antonio, Texas, had net income of $4.8 million, or 35 cents a share, on revenue of $47.9 million.
Last year, the company posted fourth-quarter earnings of $1.6 million, or 14 cents a share, on revenue of $31.9 million.
According to the Institutional Brokers Estimate System, analysts had expected earnings of 21 cents a share in the quarter.
As a result, Clear Channel shares rose $2.125, or 5.3%, to $42.50 in American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
Clear Channel raised $58 million in a secondary stock offering during the quarter, and completed the acquisition of a Minneapolis television station.
L. Lowry Mays, president and chief executive, said the results reflected both the company's acquisitions and improved operations at existing stations.

Delta Air Lines said it and Varig Brazilian Airlines received Department of Transportation approval to begin service between the U.S. and Brazil.
A Delta spokesman said flights will operate from Atlanta, Chicago, Los Angeles, Miami and New York to Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.
Northbound service from Rio de Janeiro to Atlanta, with a stop in Sao Paulo, will begin June 15, and southbound service from Atlanta will start June 16, Delta said.
The spokesman said Varig will provide aircraft for the service between the U.S. and Brazil, and both airlines will provide crews for the service.

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Ametek Inc. -- $150 million of senior notes due March 15, 2004, priced as 9.75s at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for five years.
Rated Ba3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Chartwell Re Corp. -- $75 million of senior notes due March 1, 2004, priced initially at par to yield 10.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for five years.
Rated Ba3 by by Moody's and double-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $200 million of debentures due March 18, 1999, priced at par to yield 6.14%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures, noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 23 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of debentures due March 21, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.73%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for one year and was priced at a spread of 47 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of step-up notes due March 24, 1999, priced at par to yield 4.9%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes have a one time-call option March 24, 1995.
The coupon then rises to 6.61%.
The notes were priced at a spread of 29 basis points above Treasurys and will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Drypers Corp. -- initial offering of 2.9 million common shares priced at $14.50 each through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Prophet 21 Inc. -- initial public offering of 1.5 million common shares priced at $10 each, according to the company.
William Blair & Co. is manager of the underwriting group.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Lehman Brothers Inc.
The offering, Remic Trust 1994-62, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.5% and weighted average maturity of 358 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic securities offered by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
The offering, Remic Trust 1994-61, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 8.1% and weighted average maturity of 351 months.
New Zealand Dairy Board Finance Ltd. (New Zealand) -- $150 million of floating-rate Euronotes due April 5, 1999, at issue price 99.82 via Lehman Brothers International.
Payable quarterly at three-month dollar London Interbank Offered Rate plus 0.875 point.
Guarantor New Zealand Dairy Board.
Fees 0.15.
South West Water PLC (U.K.) -- #50 million of 8.375% bonds due Feb. 8, 1998, at issue price 104.12 via Baring Brothers and Co.
Bonds will trade interchangeably with #35 million of identical notes outstanding.
Other terms undisclosed.

Iwerks Entertainment Inc., Burbank, Calif., said it signed an agreement with Blockbuster Entertainment Corp. to design and build motion-simulation theaters for Blockbuster's planned chain of family entertainment complexes.
Terms weren't disclosed.
Iwerks, which specializes in making specialty theaters and simulator rides for amusement venues, also said it will develop and produce a library of films exclusively for the Blockbuster venture announced last year.
Blockbuster, Fort Lauderdale, Fla., is a video-rental chain.
The sites and opening dates for the company's first entertainment complexes haven't been disclosed.
Iwerks's stock rose $2.25, or 9.9%, to close at $25 on the Nasdaq Stock Market.
Blockbuster's stock fell 87.5 cents to close at $24.875 in composite New York Stock Exchange trading.

General Electric Co.'s GE Investments unit said it agreed to sell a line of mutual funds through CrossLand Savings Services Corp., a unit of CrossLand Savings.
The agreement calls for the bank to make GE Funds available to customers at its 35 branches in the New York metropolitan area.
The unit will offer U.S. equity, global equity, tax-exempt, fixed-income, strategic investment and money market mutual funds.
GE funds are also available through Chemical Bank of New York and Founders National Trust Bank, Naples, Fla.

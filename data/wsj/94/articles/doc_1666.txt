Stock prices climbed, with the Nasdaq Composite index hitting a record even as the bond market retreated.
The dollar was mixed.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average rallied 16.99 points to 3865.14 after an optimistic outlook from Caterpillar, a machinery maker, prompted investors to load up on some of the economically sensitive stocks that make up the industrial average.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index gained 1.48 to 470.90 and the Nasdaq Composite jumped 4.86 to 803.85, shattering its old record of 800.47 set on Jan. 31.
Strength in technology and telecommunications shares helped boost the index of small- to medium-capitalization stocks.
Caterpillar said that as a result of growing strength in the U.S. and European economies, sales of its machines and engines will exceed expectations in the first quarter.
"In the tug-of-war between interest rates and earnings, earnings are winning," said Stefan Abrams, director of equity strategy at Trust Co. of the West.
Mr. Abrams said that in the coming months "suprisingly good earnings will conquer moderately higher interest rates" and help propel the stock market higher.
"For industrial and technology companies, the surprises are on the upside," he said.
Meanwhile, jitters continued to plague the bond market.
Bond prices were buoyed in the morning by a report from the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia that said that fewer manufacturers this month are paying higher prices for supplies than last month.
That news assuaged the inflation hawks on Wall Street, but couldn't keep prices afloat for the rest of the day.
"We got a lift on the Philadelphia Fed survey but there was very little follow-through by retail investors," said Joseph Liro, economist at S.G. Warburg.
World-wide, stock prices rose in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index gained 0.02 to 116.48.
Stock prices rose.
Volume totaled 303.9 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,149 issues advanced and 1,015 declined.
Bond prices eased.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue fell 3/8 point, or $3.75 for each $1,000 face amount.
The dollar was mixed.
In late New York trading the currency was at 1.6890 marks and 105.85 yen compared with 1.6885 marks and 106.07 yen Wednesday.

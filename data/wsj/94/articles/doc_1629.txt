The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Canadian National Railway Co. -- $300 million of notes due March 15, 2004, priced as 7s at 99.24 to yield 7.108%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 72 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brother Inc.
Columbia/HCA Healthcare Corp. -- $150 million issue of notes due March 30, 2004, priced as 7.15s at 99.744 to yield 7.186%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 80 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $150 million of notes due March 28, 1996, priced at par to yield 5.15%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for one year and was priced at a spread of 16 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by CM&M Corporate Securities Co.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of multi step-up notes due March 12, 2001, priced at par to yield 5.65% for the first two years, according to MCM CorporateWatch.

The notes, which are noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 95 basis points above the 7.75 Treasury maturing in February 2001.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Tennessee NA.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of medium-term notes due March 26, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.6%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.

The issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Hartmarx Corp. -- $100 million of senior subordinated notes due Jan. 15, 2002, priced as 10.875s at 99.322 to yield 11%.
The issue is noncallable for five years.
Rated single-B-3 by Moody's and single-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
HealthSouth Rehabilitation Corp. -- $250 million of senior subordinated notes due April 1, 2001, priced at par to yield 9.5%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for four years.
Rated Ba3 by Moody's and single-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.

Rated single-B-1 by Moody's and single-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.

The company said the 10.25% notes will be redeemed immediately following the current offering, which is to close Wednesday.

Michigan -- $150 million Series 1994A State Trunk Line Fund bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 5.74688%.
Yields for reoffered bonds range from 3% in 1995 to 5.85% in 2024.
Bonds due in 2020 and 2024 are insured by the Financial Guaranty Insurance Co., and those securities carry triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
The balance of the bonds have a single-A-1 rating from Moody's and a double-A-minus rating from S&P.
New Jersey Wastewater Treatment Trust -- $98 million various refunding revenue bonds sold competitively apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
The $81.3 million Series A bonds are believed to have been won with a bid setting the combined true interest cost at 5.28%.
Yields range from 2.75% in 1994 to 5.65% in 2007.
Goldman Sachs apparently won the $12.4 million Series B bonds with a bid setting the true interest cost at 5.08%.
Yields for those bonds range from 3.6% in 1995 to 5.4% in 2007.
The Series A and B bonds are tax-exempt.
The $4.26 million taxable Series C bonds were apparently won by Goldman with a bid setting the true interest cost at 6.783%.
Yields for those bonds range from 4.85% in 1995 to 7% in 2007.
All of the bonds have triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
New York State Urban Development Corp. -- $89 million Series 1994 youth-facilities revenue bonds priced by a Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. group.
Included in the issue are $66 million tax-exempt bonds, which are priced to yield from 5.4% in 2001 to 6% in 2008 and 2009.
Most of the bonds have received a triple-B rating from S&P and a Baa1 rating from Moody's.
Bonds due in 2014 are insured by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
The offering also includes $23 million taxable bonds, which are priced to yield from 5.15% in 1995 to 7.55% in 2003.
These bonds have also received a triple-B rating from S&P and a Baa1 rating from Moody's.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $1.61 billion of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Bear, Stearns & Co.
The offering, Series G029, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 8% and weighted average maturity of 348 months.
Yamaha Motor Corp.
USA -- $150 million of senior asset-backed certificates priced as 6.25s at 99 31/32 to yield 6.34%.
According to MCM CorporateWatch, the certificates were offered through Yamaha Motor Master Trust 1994-1, a special-purpose vehicle.
The issue was structured as a five-year "soft-bullet" security and was priced at a spread of 45 basis points above the Treasurys's when-issued five-year note.
The issue is backed by the floor-plan receivables from domestic dealers of Yamaha motor products.
Rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Chase Securities Inc.
Credit support was provided by an 18% subordinated class of certificates that was retained by the issuer.
The issuer is a unit of Yamaha Motor Co.

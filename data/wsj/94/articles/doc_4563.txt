The Texas Economic Index, a weekly leading indicator, remained flat at 111.5 in the latest week, as both initial unemployment claims and Texas refinery output stayed relatively unchanged during the week.
On a year-over-year basis, the growth rate of the index fell slightly to 2.9% from 3%.
Over the past 52 weeks, consumer confidence and help-wanted advertising have risen sharply.
Note: All figures are statewide, except for the U.S. Short Leading Index, which is national, and the Consumer Confidence Index, which tracks Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana and Arkansas
Sources: U.S. Labor Dept., Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, American Petroleum Institute, The Conference Board, CIBCR at Columbia University

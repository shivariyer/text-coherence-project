The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $250 million of notes due March 4, 1999, priced at par to yield 5.8%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 18 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Student Loan Marketing Association -- $500 million of floating-rate notes due March 7, 2001, priced at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for two years, will pay quarterly and float weekly 26 basis points above three-month Treasury bills.
The first coupon will be set March 7.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
AES China Generating Co. -- initial offering of 10 million Class A common shares priced at $16 each through underwriters led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.; Merrill Lynch & Co.; and J.P. Morgan Securities Inc.

Concord Holding Corp. -- initial offering of 2.6 million common shares priced at $12.50 each through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co. and Montgomery Securities.

Results Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. said it expects 1993 net income in the range of $385 million to $390 million, or $2.60 to $2.65 a share, including a previously announced accounting charge of $86.3 million related to post-employment benefits.
Results will be reported Feb. 9.
In 1992, the tire maker posted a loss of $658.6 million, or $4.61 a share, reflecting a May 1993 2-for-1 stock split, after extraordinary charges that included a noncash charge of more than $1 billion for retiree nonpension benefits.
World-wide sales for 1993 are expected to be about $11.6 billion, up from $11.4 billion, excluding sales of about $300 million from divested noncore businesses.
Goodyear said corporate debt, which in mid-1991 climbed to $3.7 billion, has been reduced to just over $1.4 billion and interest expense has been cut by almost half.

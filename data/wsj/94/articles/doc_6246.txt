DNA Plant Technology Corp said it expanded distribution of its specially bred tomatoes, carrots and sweet mini-pepper.
The company, which develops and markets fruits and vegetables through biotechnological breeding and genetic engineering, said its longer-life tomatoes are sold by eight supermarket chains in five states, and its carrots are sold in 22 states and Canada.
The sweet pepper, developed by crossing a bell pepper with a jalapeno, is now sold in seven states.

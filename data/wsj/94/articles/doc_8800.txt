Canadian newsprint production fell 3.2% in November from a year earlier, while shipments rose 1.8%, the Canadian Pulp and Paper Association said.
Canadian mills operated at 97% of capacity in November, unchanged from a year earlier, the industry group said.
Output for November was 769,000 metric tons, down from 794,000 metric tons a year earlier.
Total shipments rose to 799,000 metric tons, from 786,000 metric tons a year earlier.
A metric ton is equal to about 2,204.6 pounds.
Shipments to the U.S. fell 8.8% from a year earlier, to 489,000 metric tons.
Domestic shipments rose 4.7%, to 99,000 metric tons.
Shipments to offshore markets rose 36.5%, to 211,000 metric tons.
Canadian mills' stocks totaled 468,000 metric tons at the end of November, compared with 498,000 metric tons at the end of October and 405,000 metric tons at the end of November 1992.

Stock prices slid as investors raked in profits from high-flying cyclical stocks.
Bond prices and the dollar fell.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average sank 17.45 points to 3895.34.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index slipped 1.05 to 470.92 and the Nasdaq Composite Index fell 4.26 to 786.39.
The industrial average, which plunged more than 25 points during the day, finished below the 3900 level only two trading days after breaking through the psychological barrier.
International Business Machines fought back from a 3 1/4 point-deficit to finish down 3/8 at 58 1/4.
Investors were disappointed by IBM's fourth-quarter earnings, which came in slightly below analysts' forecasts.
Investors took profits and ran from hot economically sensitive stocks.
The Morgan Stanley Cyclical Index sank 0.7%, but has a 3.6% gain so far this year.
By contrast, the Consumer Index, which inched up yesterday, is off 2% on the year.
The index tracks growth stocks.
Thomas McManus, a U.S. market strategist at Morgan Stanley, said the retreat among cyclicals was understandable in light of their rally this year.
"But for 1994, people will still want to tilt their portfolios toward companies that benefit from the economic recovery," Mr. McManus said.
The market may be headed for a few more down days, said Alfred Goldman, director of market analysis at A.G. Edwards & Sons in St. Louis.
He noted that declining stocks far outnumbered advancers and that the ratio of new stock price highs to new lows was poor.
But he added that investors should remember that the industrial average has climbed a healthy 3.8% since the start of the year.
"This is a very normal time out, not a top out," Mr. Goldman said.
Bond prices fell amid a pickup in January consumer confidence and a sharp jump in sales of previously owned homes in December.
Analysts said the government's auction of $17 billion in two-year notes went pretty much as expected.
World-wide, stock prices rose in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index gained 0.42 to 113.41.
Stock prices declined.
Volume totaled 326.1 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,202 issues fell and 883 rose.
Bond prices fell.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue fell more than 1/2 point, or more than $5 for each $1,000 face amount, to yield 6.34%.
The dollar weakened.
In late New York trading the currency was quoted at 1.7496 marks and 110.90 yen compared with 1.7508 marks and 111.70 yen Monday.

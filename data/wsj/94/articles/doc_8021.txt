The Russell 2000 Index's eight days of back-to-back records came to a halt as investors took profits.
The barometer, which tracks the performance of small-capitalization stocks traded on the Nasdaq Stock Market and the major exchanges, declined 0.91 point, or 0.34%, to 262.89 after spending part of yesterday afternoon in record territory.
Some networking issues followed DSC Communications lower after the stock skidded 3 1/8 to 62 1/2, following a downgrade by Salomon Brothers.
The Plano, Texas, company posted improved fourth-quarter results of 51 cents a share, up from 28 cents, after the close.
Dynatech, Burlington, Mass., fell 1 1/8 to 20 5/8, after reporting fiscal third-quarter net income of 14 cents a share, below last year's 57 cents and analysts' forecasts of 26 cents.
Also in the technology group, Altron, Wilmington, Mass., declined 7/8 to 14 7/8, and Network Equipment Technologies (NYSE), Redwood City, Calif., gave up 1/4 to 8 3/4.
Cyrix, based in Richardson, Texas, surged 3 3/4 to 24 1/2.
Cyrix won the first round in another legal battle with rival Intel over its right to sell computer chips that are Intel clones.
Intel, Santa Clara, Calif., added 3/8 to 65 1/2.
Among stocks sensitive to interest rates, Provident Bancorp, Cincinnati, lost 1 to 31 1/2; United Wisconsin Services, Milwaukee, fell 1 1/4 to 30 and Selective Insurance Group, Branchville, N.J., retreated 7/8 to 28 1/4.
IEC Electronics rose 3/4 to 13 1/2 following its release of improved fiscal first-quarter earnings.
The Newark, N.Y., maker of complex circuit-board assemblies said it earned 37 cents a share on more shares in the latest quarter, up from 22 cents a year earlier.
Huntingdon International Holdings (NYSE) of the U.K., plummeted 2 1/2 to 8 5/8.
The provider of consulting engineering and chemistry services said its estimated operating profit for the fiscal first quarter ended Dec. 31 is "significantly below" net income of 23 cents an American depositary receipt reported a year earlier.
Telios Pharmaceuticals, San Diego, plunged 1 3/8 to 4 7/8.
The company said the Food and Drug Administration "is seeking further information" about certain aspects of previously submitted clinical data regarding Telios' Argidene Gel for treatment of diabetic foot ulcers.
Greenwich Pharmaceuticals, Fort Washington, Pa., jumped 5/8 to 3 7/8 in heavy trading.
Analysts said the shares rose ahead of Thursday's meeting with the FDA's Arthritis Drugs Advisory Committee.
National Steel (NYSE) added 1 3/4 to 15 7/8 following news that it will receive a $110 million cash injection from a lawsuit the company and other steelmakers brought against a USX subsidiary.
The U.S. Supreme Court said it will let stand a $210 million antitrust award against Bessemer & Lake Erie Railroad, the USX unit.
National Steel, based in Mishawaka, Ind., was awarded $87.5 million plus interest.
Casino Magic gained 1 1/8 to 17.
The Bay St. Louis, Mo., company said its Gateway Casino Co. joint venture with Caesars World has won the right to negotiate for the third of three riverboat gambling sites pegged for St. Louis.
First Team Sports, Mounds View, Minn., rose 3/4 to 7 3/4.
The company said it was selected as a supplier to Wal-Mart Stores for a number of its in-line skate products.
First Team said it thinks sales to Wal-Mart will have a positive impact on its fiscal 1995 sales results.
Traders, such as Larry Kinney, head of over-the-counter trading at SoundView Financial Group, said sharp swings on the New York Stock Exchange, due to programs, interfered with the ability of small stocks to maintain their record levels.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average jumped around, sailing up as many as 19 points from Friday's record of 3914.48.
By the close, the Big Board indicator had eased about 1 3/4 points.
"It was just very sloppy all around," Mr. Kinney said.
"It was the combination of profit-taking and that the breadth didn't look too good."
The Nasdaq Stock Market's advance-decline line showed losers beating out gainers, 1,706 to 1,360.
Nasdaq National Market volume was at 257,160,801 shares.
Total volume registered 302,445,101 shares.
On Friday, National Market turnover stood at 293,710,600 and overall volume was 349,432,300.
The Nasdaq Composite Index slipped 3.63 points, or 0.46%, to end at 790.65, after rising briefly above Friday's record early yesterday afternoon.
Nasdaq National Market stocks that set highs for the year to date totaled 99, while 22 hit lows.

Buoyed by investment gains, Cigna Corp.'s profit soared in the fourth quarter.
The Philadelphia-based insurance company earned $194 million, or $2.70 a share, compared with $50 million, or 70 cents a share, in the year-before period.
The latest results include capital gains of $102 million, compared with $19 million in the prior year.
Individual businesses posted mixed operating results in the quarter.
The company's group life and health insurance business earned $155 million, compared with $115 million a year earlier.
Pension profits were flat at $44 million.
Individual life insurance and annuity profits rose to $33 million from $24 million.
The property and casualty segment had a $124 million loss, compared with a $115 million loss the year before.
Cigna said it expects to lose $25 million on the Los Angeles earthquake in the current quarter.

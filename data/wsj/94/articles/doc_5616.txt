Brazil's industrial production grew 9.6% in 1993 over the previous year.
December production advanced 10.1% above the level in December 1992 and was 4.4% higher than in November, according to the National Institute of Geography and Statistics.
Last year's industrial production was the best in seven years, the institute said.
December was the third consecutive month in which production grew over the previous month, according to the data.
The most dramatic growth was in consumer durable goods, which increased 41% in 1993 over the previous year.
In the same period, capital goods grew 14.9% and nondurable consumer goods rose 3.9%.

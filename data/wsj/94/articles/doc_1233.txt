Price/Costco Inc. in Kirkland, Wash., citing a higher tax rate and one-time items, reported a 10% drop in second-quarter net income on an 8% increase in sales.
For the quarter ended Feb. 13, net slipped to $62.3 million, or 28 cents a share, after a $1.5 million charge relating to damage from the January earthquake in Los Angeles.
In the year-earlier period, net was $69 million, or 31 cents a share, after a gain of $4 million from sale of property.
Sales for the recent period were $4.1 billion, up from $3.8 billion a year earlier.
The tax rate for the current year was 41%, compared with 40% last year.
The company's stock closed at $19.50 a share, down 50 cents in Nasdaq Stock Market trading.

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Cincinnati Gas & Electric Co. -- $220 million of first-mortgage bonds bonds priced by underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first tranche, $110 million of first-mortgage bonds, due Feb. 15, 1999, was priced as 5.8s at 99.79 to yield 5.849%.
The bonds were priced at a spread of 50 basis points above Treasurys.
The second tranche, $110 million of first-mortgage bonds due Feb. 15, 2004, was priced as 6.45s at 99.818 to yield 6.475%.
The bonds were priced at a spread of 59 basis points above the when-issued Treasury.
Both parts are noncallable and rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of floating-rate notes due Feb. 14, 1997, priced at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The coupon on the noncallable issue will float and pay quarterly at 15 basis points above three-month Treasury bills.
The first coupon will be set May 14.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Intercel Inc. -- initial offering of 1.6 million common shares priced at $8.25 each through underwriters led by Robinson-Humphrey Co. and Equitable Securities Corp.
Trism Inc. -- initial offering of 2.2 million common shares priced at $14 each through underwriters led by Alex. Brown & Sons Inc. and Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Webco Industries Inc. -- initial offering of 2.5 million common shares priced at $14 each through underwriters led by Kidder, Peabody & Co. and Dean Witter Reynolds.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $360 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities is being offered by Bear, Stearns & Co.
The offering, Series 1674, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.65% and weighted average maturity of 356 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $900 million of Remic securities offered by Bear, Stearns & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-40, is backed by Fannie Mae 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.625% and weighted average maturity of 356 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $250 million of Remic securities offered by Utendahl Capital Partners.
The offering, Series 1994-38, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.65% and weighted average maturity of 356 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $162.5 million of Remic securities offered by Prudential Securities Inc.
The offering, Series 1994-35, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.65% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 358 months.
Bangkok Bank Ltd. (Thailand) -- $400 million of 10-year subordinated convertible Eurobonds at issue price par via Morgan Stanley & Co.
Indicated coupon 3% to 3.25%.
Convertible into ordinary company shares at indicated premium 23% to 26%.
Mandatorily convertible after three years if share price exceeds 140% of conversion price.
Fees 2.5, less 0.33 for lead manager.
Cesar Corp. (Japan) -- 100 million Swiss francs of privately placed convertible 0.75% notes due Mar. 31, 1998, at par via Nomura Bank (Switzerland).
Notes convertible from March 7 to March 17, 1998, at expected 2.5% premium.
Terms fixed Feb. 14.
Fees 1.625.
Credit Foncier de France (French agency) -- 730 million French francs of 7.6% Eurobonds due March 7, 1997, at issue price par via Bankers Trust France SA.
Redemption at 75 multiplied by sum of one plus following: CACF minus CACI, all divided by CACI, to maximum zero.
CACF is value of CAC 40 index four weeks before maturity.
CACI is average of CAC 40 index from three readings Feb. 8.
Fees undisclosed.
Halifax Building Society (U.K.) -- #500 million of floating-rate Euronotes due March 1999 at issue price par via Samuel Montagu & Co. and S.G. Warburg Securities Ltd.
Reoffered at 99.72.
Interest payable quarterly flat at three-month London Interbank Offered Rate.
Callable quarterly at par from March 1997.
Fees 0.43.
Maeda Corp. (Japan) -- $200 million of 1.25% Eurobonds with equity warrants attached due March 3, 1998, at issue price par via Daiwa Europe Ltd.
Warrants exercisable to purchase company shares from March 17 to Feb. 17, 1998.
Terms fixed Feb. 14.
Indicated premium 2.5%.
Monday's closing share price 1,270 yen.
Fees 2.25, less 0.15 for lead manager.

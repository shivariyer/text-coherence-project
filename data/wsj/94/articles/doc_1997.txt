The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $75 million of multiple step-up notes due April 8, 1999, priced initially at par to yield 5.5%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The coupon rises to 5.7% April 8, 1995, and then to 6%, 7% and 8% in each consecutive year.
The notes are noncallable for one year.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $250 million of notes due March 24, 2004, priced initially at par to yield 7.05%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for three years and was priced at a spread of 58 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
GTE California Inc. -- $250 million of debentures priced through a competitive bid won by Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Smith Barney Shearson Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures, due March 15, 2004, were priced as 6.75s at 98.808 to yield 6.917%.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 44 basis points above Treasurys and is rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
GTE California is a unit of GTE Corp.
Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $200 million of notes due March 30, 1999, priced as 6.375s at 99.52 to yield 6.489%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 60 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch.
Norwest Financial Inc. -- $150 million of senior notes due March 15, 1999, priced as 6.25s at 99.875 to yield 6.279%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 37.5 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Norwest Financial is a unit of Norwest Corp.
Sun Co. -- $100 million of notes due March 15, 2004, priced as 7.125s at 99.498 to yield 7.196%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 75 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Baa1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
California Federal Bank FSB -- $150 million of noncumulative, perpetual, preferred stock priced with a 10.625% dividend yield.
The 1.5 million shares were priced at $100 each.
The issue, noncallable for five years, is rated Caa by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-C by Standard & Poor's Corp.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Executive Risk Inc. -- initial offering of three million common shares priced at $12 each through underwriters led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.
Jalate Ltd. -- initial offering of 900,000 common shares priced at $6.50 each through underwriters led by H.J. Meyers & Co. and Sanders Morris Mundy Inc.
Prime Retail Inc. -- initial public offering of 2.5 million common shares priced at $19 each, according to the company, which said the underwriting firm of Friedman, Billings, Ramsey & Co. also is offering two million 10.5% Series A senior cumulative, preferred shares at $25 each and 6.1 million 8.5% Series B cumulative, participating, convertible, preferred shares at $25 each.
Both issues priced at par.
ThermoTrex Corp. -- 1.6 million common shares priced at $15.375 each, according to the company.
NatWest Securities Ltd. and Lehman Brothers Inc. co-managed the offering.
ThermoTrex is a public unit of Thermo Electron Corp.
Three-Five Systems Inc. -- 1.1 million common shares priced at $50 each through underwriters led by Chicago Corp. and Kemper Securities.
LTC Properties Inc. -- 4.2 million common shares priced at $13.25 each through underwriters led by NatWest Securities Ltd. and Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Connecticut -- $205 million of 1994 Series A general-obligation bonds apparently won by a Bear, Stearns & Co. group.
The bonds, due 1995-2010, are rated double-A by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P. Bonds yields range from 3.8% in 1996 to 5.1% in 2003.
Bonds due 1995 and 2004-10 aren't reoffered.
Mecklenburg County, N.C. -- $197.2 million of Series 1994 public-improvement bonds apparently won by a CS First Boston Inc. group.
Yields range from 4.2% in 1997 to 5.65% in 2012 and 2013.
Moody's and S&P rated the bonds triple-A.
South Carolina -- $75 million of Series 1994A general-obligation bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
Yields range from 3.2% in 1995 to 5.6% in 2009.
Moody's has rated the bonds triple-A, and S&P has assigned a double-A-plus rating.
Tennessee -- $161.2 million of 1994 general-obligation bonds apparently won by a CS First Boston Inc. group.
Yields range from 3.85% in 1996 to 5.7% in 2013 and 2014.
Moody's has rated the bonds triple-A, and S&P has assigned a double-A-plus rating.
Virginia Housing Development Authority -- $95 million of Series C mortgage-revenue bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
Yields for $40 million of the bonds range from 4.45% in 1996 to 6.3% in 2015.
Yields for the remainder range from 4.4% in 1996 to 6.25% in 2015.
Moody's Investors Service Inc. has rated the bonds double-A, and S&P has assigned a double-A-plus rating.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $2.33 billion of stripped, mortgage-backed securities.
Strips Trust 259 is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.5% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 357 months.
Underwriters are Kidder, Peabody & Co., and Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $1.1 billion of stripped, mortgage-backed securities underwritten by seven co-managers.
The offering, Strips Trust 260, is backed by Fannie Mae's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.95%, plus or minus eight basis points, and a weighted average maturity of 354 months, plus or minus six months.
The co-managers are Greenwich Capital Markets with $300 million; Morgan Stanley & Co. with $250 million; Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. with $200 million; J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. with $100 million; Prudential Securities Inc. with $100 million; Salomon Brothers Inc. with $100 million; and Lehman Brothers Inc. with $50 million.
Credit Local de France (French agency) -- $200 million of 6.25% Eurobonds at issue and reoffer price 99.738 due April 26, 1999, via Fuji International Finance PLC.
Bonds yield 6.312%, a margin of 26 basis points over comparable U.S. Treasury.
Fees 0.25.
Ebara Corp. (Japan) -- 160 million marks of 1% Eurobonds with equity warrants attached due March 31, 1998, at issue price par via Dresdner Bank AG.
Terms fixed March 24.
Warrants exercisable to purchase company shares at indicated premium 2.5% from April 19 to March 27, 1998.
Fees 2.25.
New Zealand (sovereign) -- $1 billion of floating-rate Euronotes due April 1999 at issue and reoffer price 99.78 via Morgan Stanley & Co.
International Ltd. and UBS Ltd.
Payable quarterly at 0.125 point below three-month dollar London Interbank Offered Rate.
Callable quarterly at par from April 1996.
Fees 0.15.
Petroleos Mexicanos (Mexico) -- $400 million of floating-rate Euronotes due April 7, 1997, in two tranches at issue and reoffer price par via Chase Investment Bank and Swiss Bank Corp.
First $200 million tranche at coupon of three-month dollar Libor plus 205 basis points, with interest accruing only on days when three-month Libor is within 0.25 point below to 0.5 point above quarterly coupon fixing.
Second $200 million tranche at coupon of three-month dollar Libor plus 85 basis points.
Guarantors Pemex-Exploracion y Produccion, PemexRefinancion, Pemex-Gas y Petroquimica Basica and PemexPetroquimica.
Fees 0.375.
Payable quarterly.
Israel (sovereign) -- $1.96 billion of debt through underwriters Lehman Brothers Inc. and Bear, Stearns & Co.
The state sold $121.5 million of notes maturing Feb. 15, 1999, as 6s at 99.9226 to yield 6.02%.
The debt was priced at a spread of seven basis points above Treasurys.
The second tranche, $117 million of notes maturing in Feb. 15, 2004, was priced as 6.625s at 99.4712 to yield 6.7%.
The debt was priced at a spread of 18 basis points above Treasurys.
The third tranche, $225.97 million of notes maturing in 2014, was priced as 6.6s at 97.1208 to yield 6.955%.
The debt has a 12.24-year average life and was priced at a spread of 43.5 basis points above Treasurys.
The fourth tranche, $333.06 million of notes maturing in 2018, was priced as 6.8s at 96.4043 to yield 7.18%.
The debt has a 16.25-year average life and was priced at 66 basis points above Treasurys.
Israel also sold $1.158 billion of strips in 24 tranches maturing between Aug. 15, 2012, and Feb. 15, 2024.
The debt yields between 11.44% and 25.23% and was priced 18 to 22 basis points above Treasurys.
The debt is rated triple-A by Moody's and is backed by the U.S. acting through the Agency for International Development.
Lehman Brothers Inc. lead-managed the notes maturing in 1999 and 2004 while Bear, Stearns & Co. lead-managed the remaining tranches.

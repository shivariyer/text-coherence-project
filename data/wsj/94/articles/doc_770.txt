General Electric Corp.'s GE Transportation Systems unit will jointly develop and build a new diesel locomotive engine with Deutz MWM of Mannheim, Germany.
In a statement, the company said the 6000 horsepower GE 7HDL diesel engine is part of a series of engines that will be used for locomotive, marine and industrial applications.
A prototype engine will be built later this year and commercial production is scheduled for 1995, the company said.
Deutz MWM is a unit of Klockner-Humboldt-Deutz AG of Cologne, Germany.

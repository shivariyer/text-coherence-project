Major Canadian collective bargaining settlements reached in 1993 provided for wage increases averaging 0.7%, the federal labor department said.
The 1993 figure was the lowest since the department started compiling wage data from collective agreements in 1967.
Settlements in 1992 provided for average wage increases of 2.1%.
The department also reported that in 1993 time not worked as a result of major work stoppages fell to its lowest rate in almost half a century.
The time loss was 568,910 working days, or 0.02% of total working time.

Small-cap stocks broke a three-session string of record closes as fear over rising interest rates again reared its head.
Jitters about a possible Federal Reserve move to tighten credit this week sent bond and then stock investors diving for cover.
The Fed's policy-making committee meets today.
A Feb. 4 decision by the Fed to raise short-term rates touched off a stock market shellacking.
Traders also said a normal pullback after last week's strong rise dragged prices down a bit.
Weakness in overseas markets was another depressant.
The Russell 2000 Index of small-capitalization issues lost 2.09, or 0.77%, to 268.99, after hitting a record high Friday for the third straight session.
Nasdaq issues were clobbered on profit-taking following two straight record-setting days.
The Nasdaq Stock Market's Composite Index sagged 6.63, or 0.82%, to 797.30.
Advancing Nasdaq issues trailed decliners, 1,205 to 1,822, on Nasdaq National Market volume of 222,974,050 shares and total turnover of 261,551,000.
On Friday, 261,244,200 National Market shares changed hands, while a total of 311,448,000 shares were traded.
Gaming issues fell as The Wall Street Journal reported that President Clinton's welfare-reform working group will recommend a gambling tax as part of a proposal to the president at a cabinet meeting today.
Sodak Gaming, Rapid City, S.D., fell 2 1/4 to 23; Casino America, Biloxi, Miss., dropped 1 5/8 to 33 1/2 and Sands Regents, Reno, Nev., retreated 1/2 to 13.
But the outlook for small-company stocks remains bright over the longer term, said Rao Chalasani, chief investment strategist at Kemper Securities Inc.
"Small stocks started doing well in the fourth quarter of 1990, and I think they've got a few more quarters before the trend of them outperforming larger stocks is over," Mr. Chalasani said.
Over the short term, however, he thinks small-cap issues could correct 5% more from current levels, as profit-taking continues to eat away at the strong gains made over the past month.
The day's most active Nasdaq stock, Amtech, plummeted 11 1/8, or 34%, to 21 3/8 on 6.2 million shares, more than 26 times average daily turnover.
The Interagency Group, which represents toll authorities in New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania, recommended Mark IV Industries' technology rather than Amtech's for its electronic toll collection system.
Mark IV gained 5/8 to 20 on the Big Board.
Another actively traded issue, Imatron, gained 3/16 to 1 3/4 on 5.2 million shares, compared with average daily volume of 305,300.
The South San Francisco, Calif., designer of medical diagnostic computer scanners said Friday that recent studies indicate its Electron Beam Ultrafast CT technology can detect certain indicators of heart disease.
Brazilian funds sank as investors fretted that a dispute between Brazil's congress and executive branch could damage the economy.
Brazil Fund slipped 2 1/8 to 24 3/4, and Brazilian Equity Fund gave up 1 7/8 to 21.
On the New York Stock Exchange, Baltimore Bancorp gained 1 1/4 to 19 1/4.
First Fidelity Bancorp, which eased 1/4 to 45 1/4, said it reached a definitive merger agreement to acquire the smaller company for $346 million.
In other merger news, ACC, a Rochester, N.Y., provider of long-distance telephone service, rose 3 1/2 to 25 1/4.
It agreed to merge with LDDS Communications in a stock swap.
LDDS lost 1 3/8 to 27 1/8.
Mark Controls Corp. rose 1 3/8 to 19 3/8.
The Skokie, Ill., valve maker said it received a letter of intent to be acquired by Tyco International Ltd. for $19 a share.
Mark Controls already has signed a definitive agreement to be acquired for $17.75 a share by Danaher, which rose 1 3/8 to 40 on the Big Board.
Tyco rose 1/4 to 54 1/4.
Market watchers often say stocks reverse direction on the Monday following a Friday session of expirations.
The stock market ignored bond weakness Friday and got a boost from "triple witching" when quarterly futures and options expired.
"There were a combination of things working today," said Barry Berman, head of equity trading at Robert W. Baird & Co.
"A reversal of Friday, rate worries, concerns about Whitewater all show there's a lot of things up in the air right now.
The market at times takes bad news and uses it as an excuse for selling."

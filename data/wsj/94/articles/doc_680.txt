The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
AK Steel Holding Corp. -- planned initial public offering increased in size and expected offering price to 17.6 million common shares at $21 to $24 a share, according to a preliminary prospectus.
The IPO was initially planned to be 15.8 million shares priced at $20 a share.
KLM Royal Dutch Airlines -- 18.5 million new shares priced at $23.375 each for the U.S. market, based on Thursday's closing price on the New York Stock Exchange.
Omega Healthcare Investors Inc. -- three million common shares priced at $24.25 each through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.
Anne Arundel County, Md. -- $75 million of county pollution-control revenue refunding bonds sold with Baltimore Gas & Electric Co. to underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
The company said the bonds, due April 1, 2024, were priced for reoffering at 98.628 to yield 6.1%.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300.3 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
The offering, Series 1720, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.95% and weighted average maturity of 350 months.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $200 million of Remic securities offered by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
The offering, Series 1719, is backed by the agency's five-year balloon mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 6.05% and weighted average maturity of 58 months.
Citicorp -- $1 billion of Citibank NA credit-card asset-backed certificates priced in two parts, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The certificates were sold globally through Standard Credit Card Master Trust 1994-2, a special-purpose vehicle, and were backed by Visa and MasterCard receivables.
The $940 million senior Class A certificates were priced as 7.25s at 99.681 to yield 7.29%, a 70-basis-point spread above the Treasury's 5.875s of February 2004.
The certificates, with an average life of 12 years, were rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc., Standard & Poor's Corp., Duff & Phelps Credit Rating Co. and Fitch Investors Service Inc.
Underwriters were led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
The $60 million subordinate Class B certificates were priced as 7.5s at 99.829 to yield 7.52%, a 93-basis-point spread above the Treasury's 5.875% notes of February 2004.
The certificates, with an average life of 12 years, were rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P. Underwriters were led by Citicorp Securities Markets Inc.
Credit support in the deal consisted of the 6% subordinate class and a 7% cash-collateral account, of which 5% was shared between the two classes and 2% was dedicated solely to the subordinate class.
Abbey National Treasury Services PLC (U.K. parent) -- 100 million Eurpean Currency Units of 6.25% Eurobonds due April 26, 1999, at issue price 100.12 via Societe Generale.
Reoffered at 98.545 to yield 6.60%, a margin of 25 basis points over comparable ECU French Treasury.
Guarantor Abbey National PLC.
Fees 1.875.
Landesbank Schleswig-Holstein Girozentrale (Germany) -- 100 million marks of bonds due April 26, 2004, at issue price 99.9 for own account.
Bonds known as Kommunalobligationen (communal bonds) backed by mortgages.
Coupon 6.25% first year.
Years two to six, coupon flat at six-month mark London Interbank Offered Rate.
Thereafter coupon 7%.
Fees undisclosed.
Nissho Iwai American Corp. (Japanese parent) -- 10 billion yen of floating-rate Euronotes due June 30, 1996, at issue price 100.2 via Sanwa International PLC.
Payable quarterly at three-month yen Libor plus 27.5 basis points.
Coupon collared from June 30 to June 30, 1995, at 2.7% and 3%.
Thereafter, no minimum coupon, maximum 3.5%.
Keep-well agreement with Nissho Iwai Corp.
Fees 0.2.
Swedish Export Credit Ltd. (Swedish agency) -- $75 million of zero-coupon commodity-linked Eurobonds due April 14, 1997, at issue price 100.875 via Goldman Sachs International Ltd.
Reoffered at 99.75.
Minimum redemption at par.
Additional payment based on performance and excess returns of Goldman Sachs Commodity Index.
Fees 1.375.

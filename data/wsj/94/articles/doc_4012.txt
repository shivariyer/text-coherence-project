The communications-technology concern named James W. Eyster president and chief executive officer, effective today.
Mr. Eyster, 50 years old, joins the company after serving as executive vice president of M&I Data Services Inc., a software developer.
He succeeds Edward D. Markham, whose employment with ARI "ended" Friday, according to a weekend release.
Mr. Markham and company officials could not be reached for comment.
ARI had 1994 fiscal first-quarter losses of $1.2 million, or 11 cents a share, on revenue of $1.6 million.

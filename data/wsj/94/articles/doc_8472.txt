Mr. Coffee Inc. said it received an offer to enter into discussions with an unidentified potential buyer.
"Discussions are in a very preliminary stage and no decision concerning an appropriate course of action has been reached," the Cleveland company said.
The announcement was made after the stock market had closed for the day.
Mr. Coffee finished at $11.75, up 25 cents, in Nasdaq Stock Market trading.
Gary C. Chin, an Oppenheimer & Co. analyst, said Mr. Coffee would make an attractive acquisition for a large consumer housewares company selling through the mass retail market.
Mr. Coffee has about 34% of the U.S. automatic drip coffee maker market, the leading position.
Mr. Chin said he conservatively figures Mr. Coffee could fetch about $18 a share.
Last spring, Mr. Coffee was the subject of takeover rumors, although nothing came of them at the time.
In the 39 weeks ended Sept. 26, Mr. Coffee had net income of $1.7 million, or 20 cents a share, which included a charge of $1.2 million for the early extinguishment of debt.
Sales in the period totaled $113 million.
For the fiscal year ended Dec. 27, 1992, Mr. Coffee earned $3.7 million, or 45 cents a share, on $169 million in sales.

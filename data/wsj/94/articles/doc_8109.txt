Federal Paper Board Co., citing lower prices and sales in its market pulp business, said fourth-quarter net income fell 47%.
Earnings for the 16-week period ended Jan. 1 were $7.9 million, or 14 cents a share, compared with $14.9 million, or 31 cents a share, for the 17-week period a year earlier.
Sales fell 9% to $413.7 million from $454.6 million.
The latest quarter includes a charge of $3.4 million related to mill modernization.
John R. Kennedy, president, said results were about as expected, considering general economic and market conditions for the Montvale, N.J., paper concern's major products.
For the year, the company said net fell 77% to $20.8 million, or 34 cents a share, from $91.6 million, or $2.04 cents a share, for the 53 weeks ended Jan. 2, 1993.
Sales fell 5% to $1.39 billion from $1.46 billion.

Evans & Sutherland Computer Corp., Salt Lake City, announced plans to spin off its Tripos Inc. subsidiary through a special dividend of Tripos stock to shareholders of the parent company.
In a Securities and Exchange Commission filing, the companies said that each Evans & Sutherland shareholder will receive, in a tax-free distribution, one share of Tripos common stock for every three shares of Evans & Sutherland common stock.
The proposed spinoff is expected to be completed this spring.
Evans & Sutherland is a computer systems and software concern.
Tripos, a 92-employee company based in St. Louis, sells software used in the pharmaceutical industry.
It earned $444,000 on sales of $17.9 million in 1993, out of the parent company's total profits of $1.8 million, or 22 cents a share, on sales of $142.2 million, the companies said.
On the Nasdaq Stock Market, Evans & Sutherland closed at $18.375, up 37.5 cents.

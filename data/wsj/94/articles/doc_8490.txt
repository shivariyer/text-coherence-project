Stride Rite Corp. reported net income of $7.4 million, or 15 cents a share, for its fourth quarter ended Dec. 3, a substantial increase over year-ago results, which were depressed by a big one-time charge.
In the fiscal fourth quarter of 1992, the Cambridge, Mass., footwear manufacturer had net of $2 million, or four cents a share, after a $11.1 million charge stemming from the relocation of its distribution center from Kentucky to Massachusetts.
Revenue climbed 3.3% to $111.1 million from $107.5 million a year ago.
The company said sales of its Keds line were down 3% in 1993, the result of a shift in consumer preference toward lower-priced canvas shoes.
In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, shares of Stride Rite closed at $16.875, up 25 cents.
For the full year in 1993, Stride Rite had net of $58.3 million, or $1.15 a share, down 5.2% from $61.5 million, or $1.19 a share in 1992.
Full-year sales slipped slightly to $582.9 million from $585.9 million in 1992.

Nielsen Marketing Research, a unit of Dun & Bradstreet Corp., said it agreed in principle to take a minority stake in efficient market service inc. and commit $60 million to ems over a three-year period.
The accord also calls for the two market-research companies to jointly develop and market services for the consumer packaged goods industry.
The services are in a growing area called Efficient Consumer Response, which is designed to take hundreds of millions of dollars out of the costs of merchandising, food and other consumer packaged goods.
Deerfield, Ill.-based ems specializes in gathering and reporting supermarket scanner data on a daily basis.

General Signal Corp. reported fourth-quarter net income of $4.9 million, driven by continued cost-cutting and a pickup in orders and sales.
A year-earlier, the company had a loss of $37.5 million.
Fourth-quarter net income was 10 cents a share, compared with last year's loss of 89 cents a share.
The fourth quarter included a number of one-time charges.
The company said earnings on a continuing basis after excluding special charges, climbed 14% to 57 cents a share.
"Overall the quarter was our best from an ongoing business basis in over two years, reflecting continued discipline in cost containment and asset management," said Edmund M. Carpenter, chairman and chief executive officer.
The company in Stamford, Conn., makes equipment and instruments for process control, electrical and other industries.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, shares of General Signal closed at $36.50, up 25 cents.

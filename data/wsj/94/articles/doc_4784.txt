Mr. Coffee Inc. said cost-cutting and expense control led to a 68% increase in fourth-quarter net income despite a slight dip in sales.
For the quarter ended Dec. 26, 1993, the maker of automatic drip coffee machines reported net income of $3.8 million, or 46 cents a share, vs. net of $2.3 million, or 27 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue declined 4.2% to $62.0 million from $64.8 million.
The Cleveland company added that it has retired the last of its high-rate debt and gained market share in its automatic drip coffee-maker segment.
For the year, Mr. Coffee reported net income of $5.5 million, or 66 cents a share, up 46% from $3.7 million, or 45 cents a share, in 1992.
Revenue increased 3.6% to $175 million.
The full-year 1993 earnings included a $1.2 million, or 15-cent-a-share, extraordinary charge related to early extinguishment of debt.
On the Nasdaq Stock Market Friday, Mr. Coffee closed at $14.125, up 25 cents.

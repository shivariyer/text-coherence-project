Nashua Corp. restated its 1993 fourth-quarter and year results to reflect a revision of its estimate of the value of the assets of its computer-products group.
It said restated results for the 1993 fourth quarter and year include pretax restructuring charges totaling $48.5 million, up from previously reported charges of $42.5 million.
As a result, the Nashua, N.H., fax paper and supplies company reported a 1993 fourth-quarter net loss of $30 million, or $4.74 a share, and a yearly loss of $19.2 million, or $3.02 a share.
Losses were previously reported of $26.2 million, or $4.12 a share, and $15.3 million, or $2.40 a share, respectively.
Nashua also said it has signed a letter of intent to sell its thin-film disk operations to an investor group.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Nashua stock closed at $27.50 a share, unchanged.

Yields on taxable money-market funds rose a bit in the week ended Tuesday.
IBC/Donoghue's average seven-day compound yield on taxable funds crept up to 2.87% from 2.85%, according to Money Fund Report, an Ashland, Mass., newsletter.
Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.
The seven-day simple yield on taxable funds rose to 2.83% from 2.81%, while the 30-day simple yield inched up to 2.80% from 2.78%.
The 30-day compound yield rose to 2.84% from 2.82%.
Assets of 645 taxable funds decreased by $128.4 million to $478.80 billion, the newsletter said.
Institutional investors took out $408.7 million, while individual investors put in $280.3 million.
Average maturity of the taxable funds' investments, which include commercial paper, or short-term corporate IOUs, and Treasury bills, shortened by one day, to 50 days.
The average tax-exempt seven-day compound yield slipped to 1.89% from 1.95% in the latest week.
The latest tax-free yield is equivalent to a taxable 2.95% for someone paying 36% in taxes and to 3.13% for those who pay 39.6%.
Assets of 346 tax-exempt funds rose by $375.4 million to $116.48 billion, according to the newsletter.
The tax-free funds' average portfolio maturity shortened by one day to 54 days.
Money Fund Report is published by IBC/Donoghue Inc., a subsidiary of IBC USA (Publications) Inc.

Oshkosh Truck Corp. said it is cutting its work force at Oshkosh, Wis., by 25%, or 400 employees, resulting in a charge of $2 million, or 23 cents a share, in its fiscal second quarter, ending March 31.
In the quarter ended March 31, 1993, the company earned $2.1 million, or 25 cents a share, on sales of $154.3 million.
The trucking concern said the downsizing relates to the reduction of its defense business and includes early retirements, reductions in work force, and layoffs.
Oshkosh said it is reducing its staff to a level "consistent" with expected future business.

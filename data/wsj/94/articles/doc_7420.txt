Bird Medical Technologies Inc., Palm Springs, Calif., expects to report fourth-quarter net income of $550,000, or eight to 10 cents a share, Felix T. Troilo, president and chief executive, said.
The results compare with net of $234,000, or four cents a share, a year earlier.
Sales rose to about $16.5 million from $12.8 million, Mr. Troilo said.
The maker of respiratory care and infection-control products forecasts a 20% growth in sales "and possibly more" for 1994, he said.
For the year, the company expects to report net of $1.2 million to $1.3 million, or 19 cents to 21 cents a share.
Sales climbed 20% to $52.4 million from $43.8 million.
Bird Medical had a 1992 net loss of $1.4 million, or 22 cents a share, after a onetime, pretax charge of $1.6 million related to restructuring.

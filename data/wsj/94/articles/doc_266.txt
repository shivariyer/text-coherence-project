Williams-Sonoma Inc., boosted by continued growth in retail sales, said net income for its fourth quarter jumped 48% to $11.4 million, or 67 cents a share, from $7.7 million, or 46 cents a share, a year earlier.
For the period ending Jan. 30, the San Francisco-based kitchenware retailer said sales rose 26% to $162.7 million from $129 million a year earlier.
William-Sonoma shares closed at $33.75, down 25 cents, in the Nasdaq National Market yesterday.
Net for the year soared to $11.2 million, or 66 cents a share, from $1.8 million, or 11 cents a share, in the previous year.
Sales increased 18.9% to $410.1 million from $344.9 million.

Transamerica Corp. said its fourth-quarter net income rose 92%, paced by strong showings in its life insurance and real-estate services segments.
San Francisco-based Transamerica reported net of $22.8 million, or 24 cents a share, compared with year-earlier earnings of $11.9 million, or six cents a share.
Per-share earnings were increased by a stock buyback and the diversion of preferred dividends.
Excluding investment gains and losses, Transamerica said profit from continuing operations rose 17% to $101.1 million, or $1.24 a share, from $86.5 million, or $1.02 a share.
For all of 1993, Transamerica's net climbed 55% to $377.4 million, or $4.51 a share, from $243.2 million, or $2.83 a share, in 1992.
Profit from continuing operations rose 26% to $425.2 million, or $5.12 a share, from $337.8 million, or $4.05 a share.

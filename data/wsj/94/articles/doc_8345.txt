Lee Enterprises Inc., citing strengthened advertising at its newspaper and broadcast holdings, said earnings in its fiscal first quarter rose 16%, on a 6% rise in revenue.
For the quarter, ended Dec. 31, the Davenport, Iowa, media concern reported net income of $13.3 million, or 57 cents a share, up from the year-before $11.5 million, or 49 cents a share.
Revenue rose to $102.1 million from $96.3 million.
The company owns eight television stations, 19 daily newspapers and a stable of weekly or specialty papers, primarily in smaller cities and towns.
Lee said classified advertising increased 11% from the year-earlier period, reflecting "strength in the automotive and real estate segments and more advertising by individual customers."
Overall newspaper advertising revenue grew 3.7%; broadcasting revenue rose 2%.

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Lear Seating Corp. -- $145 million of subordinated notes, due Feb. 1, 2002, were priced at par to yield 8 1/4%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes are noncallable for four years and are rated single-B-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Illinois Bell Telephone Co. -- $100 million of debentures, due Feb. 1, 2004, were priced as 5.8s at 99.495 to yield 5.867%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The non-callable debentures were priced at a spread of 25 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P, the issue was sold through a competitive bid won by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Illinois Bell is a unit of Ameritech.
Illinois Bell Telephone Co. -- $100 million of debentures, due Feb. 1, 2025, were priced as 6 5/8s at 97.183 to yield 6.845%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures, which are noncallable for 10 years, were priced at a spread of 52 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated triple-A by Moody's and double-A-1 by S&P, the issue was sold through a competitive bid won by Lehman Brothers.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $700 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit mortgage securities were offered by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.
The offering, Series 1689, is backed by the agency's 30-year 7% mortgage securities.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit mortgage securities were offered by Kidder Peabody & Co.
The offering, Series 1691, is backed by the agency's 30-year 7.5% mortgage securities.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit mortgage securities were offered by Bear Stearns & Co.
The offering, Series 1688, is backed by $240 million of the agency's 20-year 7 1/2% mortgage securities and $60 million of the agency's 20-year 6% securities.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real estate mortgage investment conduit mortgage securities were offered by Nikko Securities Co.
The offering, Series 1692, is backed by the agency's 30-year 6.5% mortgage securities.
Federal Farm Bank Credit System -- $150 million of medium-term notes, due Feb. 5, 1996, were priced at par to yield 4.09%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes are noncallable for one year and will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
Canada -- The Canadian government said it intends to offer up to 600 million Canadian dollars (US$455.2 million) of inflation-indexed bonds maturing Dec. 1, 2021.
The bonds carry a coupon rate of 4.25%.
The semi-annual coupon payments and the final redemption value of the bonds will be adjusted for inflation.
The Canadian government has sold C$2.13 billion of such bonds.
Proceeds from he planned issue will be used for general government purposes.
The offering price and other details of the planned issue will be announced later.
A group of 13 securities firms has been appointed to market the issue.
Statens Bostadsfinansierings (Sweden;) -- 30 billion yen of floating-rate Euronotes due Feb. 22, 1999, in two-tranches via Daiwa Europe Ltd. and Fuji International Finance PLC.
The first tranche, totaling 15 billion yen, pays interest quarterly flat at three-month London InterBank Offered Rate for yen.
Issue and reoffer price 99.85.
The second tranche, also totaling 15 billion yen, pays interest quarterly at three-month LIBOR plus 20 basis points.
Maximum coupon 4.7%.
Issue and reoffer price 99.70.
Fees 0.20 point.
General Electric Credit Corp. (U.S.) -- $100 million of floating-rate notes maturing March 1997 at issue and re-offer price 100 via Kidder Peabody Securities Co.
Principal and interest linked to the Mexican peso.
Interest paid quarterly, at 20 basis points over 91-day Cetes rate.
Fees 1/4 point.
GE Credit is a unit of General Electric Co.
Oesterreichische Postsparkasse (Austria) -- $100 million of corridor floating-rate Euronotes due Feb. 25, 1995 at issue price par via Creditanstalt-Bankverein (London Branch).
Notes linked to London InterBank Offered Rate for Italian lira.
Interest paid semi-annually at 4.5% over three-month lira LIBOR, subject to LIBOR falling within following six-month LIBOR ranges.
Range one: 7.5% to 9%, Range two: 6.5% to 8.5% Guarantor Republic of Austria.
Fees 0.15 points.
Queens Centre Funding Corp. (U.S.) -- $70 million of mortgage-backed bonds two tranches with scheduled maturity of Jan. 1, 1999, via Lehman Brothers Internatrional.
Bonds secured on commercial mortgages, originator undisclosed.
Stated maturity Jan. 1, 2004.
Tranche A is for $60 million with interest paid quarterly at 80 basis points over three-month LIBOR.
Bonds issued and reoffered at 99.874.
Tranche B is for $10.2 million with interest paid quarterly at three-month LIBOR plus 215 basis points.
Issue and reoffer price 99.867.
Fees undisclosed.
Sudwest LB Capital Markets PLC (German parent;) -- 150 billion Italian lire of 8% Eurobonds due Feb. 24, 2004, at issue price 101.80 via Banca di Roma SpA.
At co-manager's breakeven price 99.80 bonds yield 8.03%.
Guarantor Sudwestdeutsche Landesbank AG.
Fees 2 points.

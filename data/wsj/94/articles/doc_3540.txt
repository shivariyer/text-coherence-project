Waban Inc. took a charge of $60.2 million to close or relocate part of its operations and reported a loss of $44.9 million, or $1.36 a share, for the fourth quarter ended Jan. 29.
That compares with net income of $14.1 million, or 42 cents a share, a year ago.
Waban, which operates two warehouse-style discount chains -- HomeBase and BJ's Wholesale Club -- had indicated in November that it planned to close or relocate about 16 of its older HomeBase stores.
The fourth-quarter results also include a $1.3 million charge to cover the cost of relocating the firm's BJ's Wholesale Club in Syracuse, N.Y., the company said.
Sales declined 5.1% to $890.3 million from $937.6 million.
For the year, Waban had a loss of $17.8 million, or 56 cents a share, compared with profit of $44.2 million, or $1.33 a share.
Sales climbed 6.9% to $3.59 billion.

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Eaton Corp. -- $200 million of debentures priced in two parts by underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first tranche, $100 million of notes due April 1, 1999, was priced as 6.375s at 99.641 to yield 6.46%.
The debentures were priced at a spread of 35 basis points above Treasurys.
The second tranche, $100 million of debentures due April 1, 2024, was priced as 7.625s at 99.554 to yield 7.663%.
The debentures were priced at a spread of 57 basis points above Treasurys.
Both parts are noncallable and rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $200 million of notes due April 5, 1999, priced initially at par to yield 6.39%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 30 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Houghton Mifflin Co. -- $100 million of notes due April 1, 2004, priced as 7.125s at 99.4 to yield 7.21%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 60 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $400 million of 10-year, noncallable debentures due April 5, 2004, priced to yield 6.865%.
The 10-year debentures were priced at 99.890265 with a 6.85% coupon.
First Alert Inc. -- initial offering of 3.6 million common shares priced at $17 each through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc. and Morgan Stanley & Co.
Reptron Electronics Inc. -- initial offering of 2.2 million common shares priced at $13 each through underwriters led by Prudential Securities and Raymond James & Associates.
New York City Educational Construction Fund -- $138.6 million of Series 1994 revenue bonds priced by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group.
The offering includes the following: $69.2 million of Series A senior revenue bonds priced to yield from 3.05% in October to 5.7% in 2007; $19.1 million of Series B senior subordinated revenue bonds priced to yield 5.95% in 2011 and 6% in 2013; and $50.3 million of Series C junior subordinated revenue bonds priced to yield from 3.05% in October to 5.9% in 2009.
The bonds are insured by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from Moody's and S&P.
Global Mark International Ltd. (Indonesia) -- $500 million of mandatory exchangeable step-up coupon Eurobonds due April 6, 1997, at issue and reoffer price par via UBS Ltd.
Bonds mandatorily exchangeable into common shares of PT Indofood Sukses Makmur from initial public offering until maturity date.
Bonds exchangeable at 7,692 Indonesian rupiahs a share, equal to 23 times forecast 1994 earnings per share of 334.47 rupiahs.
If IPO doesn't occur, bonds callable at price to yield, on 1995 anniversary, 150 basis points over three-year U.S. Treasury.
Call price adjusted to yield 175 over Treasurys on 1996 anniversary and 200 over Treasurys at maturity.
Fees three, less 0.25 for co-lead managers.
Rabobank Nederland (Netherlands) -- 150 million Dutch guilders of 6.25% Eurobonds due July 22, 1998, at issue price 102.075 for own account.
Bonds will trade interchangeably with 500 million guilders of identical bonds outstanding.
Reoffered at 101.275 to yield 5.89%, a margin of eight basis points over comparable Dutch state loan of July 1998.
Other terms unchanged.
Fees one.

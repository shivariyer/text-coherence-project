Bank of San Francisco Co.
Holding Co. said it plans a private placement of securities to raise capital.
The parent of Bank of San Francisco said it hopes to sell units that include its Class A common shares.
It didn't disclose what else the units might include, or how much money it hopes to raise.
It did say that the units would likely be priced "significantly below" the recent trading range of its Class A common stock, which closed Friday on the American Stock Exchange at $1.50, down 12.5 cents, or 7.7%.
Bank of San Francisco posted a 1993 net loss of $10.2 million, or $1.15 a share.

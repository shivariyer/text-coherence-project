Economically sensitive stocks and oil-related issues lifted the market after more reassuring news on inflation.
Stocks followed bonds back and forth early in the day but managed to remain higher in the afternoon even as the debt market faded.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 16.99, or 0.44%, to 3865.14 -- its highest close since Feb. 23.
The Treasury's 30-year bond lost 3/8 point, pushing up its yield to 6.82%.
But analysts played down the importance of the divergence and attributed a large part of the stock market optimism to hopes that interest rates have begun to stabilize after the big increases last month and early in March.
An economic reading from the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia helped reassure that belief.
It included indications that product prices have moderated in the Philadelphia region.
Broad-based indexes that track mostly New York Stock Exchange issues also rallied, but they couldn't keep pace with the advance on the Nasdaq Stock Market.
Strength in technology and telecommunications shares helped carry the Nasdaq Composite Index up 4.86, or 0.61%, to 803.85 -- above its former record high of 800.47, which had stood since Jan. 31.
Big gainers on Nasdaq included Tele-Communications Class A, which rose 1 1/4 to 24 1/4; Oracle, up 1 1/4 to 34 3/4; and Lotus Development, up 5 3/4 to 85 1/2 after it disclosed a marketing agreement with American Telephone & Telegraph.
AT&T was up 3/4 to 53 1/2.
A rally in cyclical stocks gained strength in the afternoon, helping the stock market to outperform bonds.
The economically sensitive group benefited in part from news that Caterpillar is adding workers to keep up with customer demand.
After a trading halt, Caterpillar reopened sharply higher in the last minutes of trading to lift the industrial average to its session high.
The stock rose 3 1/4 to 119 1/2.
Among other cyclicals, Aluminum Co. of America rose 1 3/4 to 75 1/2 and International Paper advanced 7/8 to 69 1/8.
Energy stocks, meanwhile, were lifted by positive comments from analysts at several major brokerage firms, including Bear Stearns, which raised investment ratings on several major producers.
Chevron rallied 1 3/4 to 91 1/2, Amoco 3/4 to 55 3/8, Atlantic Richfield 2 3/4 to 101 7/8 and Kerr-McGee 1 1/8 to 46 3/4.
The Standard & Poor's 500 Stock Index gained 1.48, or 0.32%, to 470.90, and the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index rose 0.77, or 0.30%, to 261.11.
The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index added 0.81, or 0.17%, to 470.63.
U.S. Surgical rallied for a second straight day, gaining 1 5/8 to 20.
The stock was up 2 1/4 Wednesday after the medical device company said its restructuring efforts were nearly complete, and predicted that its deficit for the first quarter will be narrower than the 26 cent-a-share loss from operations that it expects to report for the fourth quarter.
The company released more details on 1993 results yesterday morning.
Northwest Airlines sold 20 million shares in an initial public offering that was priced at $13 a share.
As reported in this newspaper yesterday, the airline company was forced to slash the asking price of its IPO to between $13 and $14 a share from an earlier range of between $19 and $21, people familiar with the deal said.
King World Productions jumped 1 5/8 to 36 7/8 after CNBC cable television reported rumors that the company might be acquired by Capital Cities/ABC or another TV network.
Spokesmen for King World and Capital/Cities declined to comment on the talk.
Shares of Capital Cities rose 1/8 to 705.
Compuware rallied 6 1/8 to 42 3/4 on Nasdaq following news that the company agreed to buy Uniface Holding, a closely held Dutch company.
Compuware will pay about 6.7 million common shares.
Airborne Freight lost 1 1/4 to 36 3/4 after Japan's Mitsui & Co. sold a 1.4 million-share block of the Seattle package and document-delivery company's stock.
A spokesman for Airborne said he didn't know the identity of the buyer.
Healthtrust jumped 1 1/2 to 31 1/8.
The hospital company reported earnings of 56 cents a share for its second quarter, ended Feb. 28.
That was up from 45 cents a year ago, and above the 53 cent mean estimate of analysts surveyed by Institutional Brokers Estimate System.
Centex advanced 1 3/4 to 35 7/8.
The home building concern Wednesday made its initial sales presentation tied to its planned initial public offering for its construction products unit.
Talbots increased 1 1/8 to 32 7/8 after it reported fourth-quarter earnings of 18 cents a share.
A year ago, the apparel store operator had a profit of 12 cents a share.

STAR SEMICONDUCTOR Inc., a computer chip concern based in San Jose, Calif., filed on March 3 for an initial public offering of 750,000 common shares.
The initial price of the shares is expected to be $12 to $14 a share.
The offering is expected to take place in mid-April.
Proceeds would be used to hire important management personnel and expand all areas of the company, including sales and marketing and production capacity.
Star, started in founder Jeffrey Robinson's garage in 1988, makes and markets digital-signal processors used in computers and telecommunications products.
Customers include Hewlett-Packard Inc. and Hughes Aircraft Co.
The company moved to its current headquarters from Warren, N.J., last fall.

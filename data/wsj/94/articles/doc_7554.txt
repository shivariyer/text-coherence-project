Interactive Network Inc. said it will launch a test to determine if its subscribers are interested in game competitions over video-game maker Sega Enterprises Ltd.'s cable-television channel.
As part of the test, libraries of video games will be delivered to home subscribers over cable.
Shares in Interactive Network, which offers a subscription service that enables viewers to play along with TV game shows and sports events, rose $1.125 to $10.50 in over-the-counter trading yesterday.

Houston Industries Inc. said its Kblcom Inc. unit agreed to acquire three cable companies serving about 47,000 customers in the Minneapolis area for a gross purchase price of $87 million.
In a news release, the company, in Houston, said that after reduction for liabilities, it will pay the purchase price in common stock.
Houston Industries said the three cable companies, Nortel, Amzak Cable Midwest and Minnesota Cable Properties, are under common ownership.
About 40,000 of the customers served by the properties are in the Minnesota communities of Bloomington, St. Louis Park, Fridley, Chaska and Shakopee, with the remaining customers located in small communities south and west of the Minneapolis metropolitan area.

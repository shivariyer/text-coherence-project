Riggs National Corp. reported a small profit for the fourth quarter compared with a large year-earlier loss, reflecting aggressive moves by the company to clean up its portfolio of nonperforming loans.
Riggs said net income for the quarter was $3.1 million, or nine cents a share, compared with a loss of $24.1 million, or 97 cents a share, a year earlier.
The bank holding company, based in Washington, D.C., said its nonperforming assets fell $60 million for the quarter to the lowest level in more than three years.
The bank had total assets of $4.78 billion on Dec. 31.
The loan loss provision fell to $2.1 million from $27.3 million.
Net interest income rose to $35.2 million from $32.1 million.
For 1993, Riggs had a loss of 94.2 million, or $3.65 a share, compared with a 1992 loss of $21.1 million, or 87 cents a share.
The full-year loan loss provision was $69.3 million, up from $52.1 million.

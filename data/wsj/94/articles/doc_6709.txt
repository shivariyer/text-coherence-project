General Instrument Corp. said fourth-quarter net income leaped amid booming sales for cable-television equipment.
Net jumped to $39.5 million, or 64 cents a share, from $7.2 million, or 12 cents a share, a year earlier.
Sales climbed 37% to $408.4 million from $298.1 million.
Chicago-based General Instrument reported gains in all business areas, including digital compression technology, cable TV set-top boxes and other gear for video distribution and transmission.
For the full year, net income was $90.6 million, or $1.48 a share, against a loss of $53 million, or $1.08 a share, in 1992.
The 1992 results include a charge of $11.6 million, or 24 cents a share, related to early extinguishment of debt.
Sales for the year rose 30% to $1.39 billion from $1.07 billion.
In composite New York Stock Exchange trading, General Instrument closed at $59.875, up 50 cents.

General Instrument Corp., Chicago, said it entered a cross-licensing agreement for digital technologies with Zenith Electronics Corp.
Terms weren't disclosed.
General Instrument will license its DigiCipher brand access control, digital compression and transmission technologies to Zenith.
DigiCipher converts analog television signals into digital signals, then squeezes the signals to as little as onetenth their original size, enabling the user to send or receive many more signals over less space.
Zenith will license to General Instrument its VSB transmission technology, which expands the capabilities of digital cable-TV systems beyond 500 channels to 1,000.
General Instrument develops and makes broad-band transmission, distribution and access-control technologies for cable, satellite and terrestrial broadcasting applications.
Zenith, based in Glenview, Ill., is a consumer-electronics, cable and networking-products company.

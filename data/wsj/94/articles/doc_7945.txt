Quaker State Corp., citing improvement in its vehicle-light manufacturing and Fast Lube units, swung to a profit in the fourth quarter, compared with a loss in the year-ago period.
The Oil City, Pa.-based marketer of motor oil and lubricants posted net income of $1.9 million, or seven cents a share, including pretax charges of $3.1 million for a plant closing, severance costs related to a corporate reorganization, and a writedown at its credit life and indemnity unit.
Revenue was $191.9 million.
That compares with a year-ago loss of $35.8 million, or $1.32 a share, which included a loss from discontinued coal operations of $37.4 million, or $1.38 a share.
Revenue was $174 million.

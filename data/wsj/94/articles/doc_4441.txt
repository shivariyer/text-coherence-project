Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms generally rose a bit in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month, small-denomination "savings" CDs crept up to 2.83% from 2.82%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs remained at 4.54%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month, broker-sold CDs edged up to 3.21% from 3.17%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 4.52% from 4.44%, the survey said.
On large-denomination, "jumbo," CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on six-month certificates inched up to 2.97% from 2.94%.
On five-year jumbos, the average rose to 4.84% from 4.81%, according to Banxquote.

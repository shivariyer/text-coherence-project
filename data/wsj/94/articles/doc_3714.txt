GTE Corp., Stamford, Conn., said it will supply Air Canada with a telecommunications system enabling airline passengers to make and receive calls from the air, as well as transmitting data.
Terms of the pact weren't disclosed.
The pact was signed by GTE Airfone, a unit of GTE, and the unit's Canadian partner, Skytel Communications, and covers Air Canada's entire domestic and international fleet of 109 aircraft.
GTE said its digital GenStar system will allow passengers to plug in laptop computers and portable facsimile machines.
Skytel, a unit of Canadian telephone company BCE Ltd., provides air-to-ground communications service to airlines.
GTE, a large regional phone company, said it has agreements to supply the GenStar system for about 1,700 aircraft at several major airlines.

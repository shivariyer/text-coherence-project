The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $97 million of medium-term notes due March 10, 1999, priced at par to yield 6.05%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 22 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of debentures due March 15, 1999, priced initially at par to yield 6.06%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 25 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of debentures due March 11, 2004, priced at par to yield 6.81%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue, noncallable for five years, was priced at a spread of 44 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
PHC Inc. -- 1.2 million units priced at $5 each through Americorp Securities Inc., according to the company known also as Pioneer Healthcare.
Each unit consists of one Class A common share and one Class A warrant to purchase one Class A common share at $7.50.
New South Africa Fund Inc. -- initial offering of 4.7 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.
Credit Commercial de France (France) -- 150 million Swiss francs of public floating-rate bonds due March 31, 1999, priced at 100.5 via Credit Suisse.
Payable semiannually at 0.0625 point below London Interbank Offered Rate for Swiss francs.
Coupon 4% first 180 days.
Fees undisclosed.
Kaufhof Finance BV Amsterdam (German parent) -- 100 million marks of Eurobonds due March 30, 2004, at issue price 101.78 via Commerzbank AG.
Coupon 7% first year.
Years two to six, semiannual coupon of 12 basis points over six-month mark Libor.
Thereafter coupon 7%.
Guarantor Kaufhof Holdings AG.
Fees 2.5, less 0.125 for lead manager.

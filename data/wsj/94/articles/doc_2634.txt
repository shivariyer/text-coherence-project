LEAR SEATING Corp., Southfield, Mich., a maker of car and truck seats, said it plans an initial public offer of 9.375 million common shares, priced between $15 and $17.
In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, Lear said the company will only receive as much as $106 million from the sale of about 6.25 million of its shares.
The remaining 3.125 million shares, which could sell for as much as $53.13 million, will be sold by FIMA Finance Management Inc., a British Virgin Islands company controlled by Ifint SA of Luxembourg, an international investment holding company.

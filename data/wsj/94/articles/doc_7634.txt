Gannett Co., Arlington, Va., citing growth in newspaper and broadcast advertising revenue, said net income jumped 13% in the fiscal fourth quarter.
For the quarter ended Dec. 26, the media concern reported earnings of $129 million, or 88 cents a share, compared with $114.2 million, or 79 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue increased 5.1% to $982.6 million from $935.1 million.
For the year, net nearly doubled to $397.8 million, or $2.72 a share, from $199.7 million, or $1.39 a share.
Year-earlier results included a $180 million charge for retirement benefits and a $34 million credit related to income taxes.
Revenue rose 5% to $3.64 billion from $3.47 billion.
Gannett said the results reflect "significant" recovery in the broadcast segment and profit from flagship newspaper USA Today.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Gannett shares fell 87.5 cents to $56.875.

In regard to Dorothy Rabinowitz, Jan. 20 editorial-page essay "Inman's False Cry of McCarthyism": In purportedly clarifying the definition of the word "McCarthyism," so flagrantly overused in the 1990s, Ms. Rabinowitz doesn't complete the original definition of the word when it was newly minted in 1950.
Several victims of the Wisconsin wild man's outrageous lies -- notably, scholar Owen Lattimore -- challenged the senator to repeat his charges off the Senate floor, where he could be sued for slander.
The gutless bully always ducked these challenges.
"McCarthyism" thus implies cowardice as well as reckless mendacity.
Fortunately, no other congressman before or since has exploited congressional immunity like the late unlamented Sen. McCarthy.

Physicians Health Services Inc., Trumbull, Conn., reported a 35% increase in fourth-quarter net income largely because of lower medical costs.
The managed-care company's net rose to $3.3 million, or 35 cents a share, from $2.4 million, or 39 cents a share, a year earlier.
Per-share results reflected a 48% increase in the weighted average number of shares outstanding, a result of an initial public offering in January 1993.
Premium revenue declined 1.6% to $66.2 million.
For the full year, earnings increased 39% to $11.9 million, or $1.31 a share, from $8.6 million, or $1.36 a share.
The number of weighted average shares outstanding increased 45% over the year.
Premium revenue rose 3.5% to $274.8 million.
Physicians Health attributed its results to its lower Medical Loss Ratio, which decreased to 82.9% in 1993 from 84.4% in 1992.
Hospital-bed days declined 10.7% to 360 per 1,000 members in 1993 from 403 per 1,000 members in 1992.
Physicians Health shares fell 75 cents to $21.50 in the Nasdaq Stock Market.

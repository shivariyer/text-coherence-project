Heilig-Meyers Co., a Richmond, Va., home-furnishings retailer, said net income rose 49% in its fiscal fourth quarter.
For the quarter ended Feb. 28, net was $14.3 million, or 28 cents a share, compared with net income of $9.6 million, 21 cents a share, a year ago.
Revenue for the quarter increased 33% to $241.9 million.
Last year, the company expanded its region through the acquisition of McMahan's Furniture Co., a 92-store chain in western markets.
Heilig-Meyers, which operates 570 stores in 22 states, said it plans to open 50 to 60 stores this year.
Heilig-Meyers also said it improved operations, including targeting direct-mail advertising and implementing a sales analysis and inventory control system.
In composite New York Stock Exchange trading, its shares fell 75 cents to $35.
For the year, net income was $55 million, or $1.12 a share, up 55% from $38 million, or 84 cents a share, a year ago.
Revenue increased 31% to $863.8 million.

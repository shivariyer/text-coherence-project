Boosted by strong holiday sales, Burlington Coat Factory Warehouse Corp. topped analysts' expectations and reported a 28% rise in fiscal second-quarter profit.
The Burlington, N.J., clothing retailer reported profit for the quarter ended Jan. 1 of $56.1 million, or $1.38 a share, well ahead of analysts' estimates of about $1.25 a share.
The company had profit of $43.7 million, or $1.07 a share, in the year-ago quarter.
Revenue rose 25% to $628.7 million from $607.8 million.
Burlington Coat Factory Chief Financial Officer Robert LaPenta said in an interview that stronger-than-expected holiday sales, tighter cost controls and lower cost of merchandise contributed to the strong quarter.
In composite New York Stock Exchange trading, the company's stock rose $1.25 to $23.25.

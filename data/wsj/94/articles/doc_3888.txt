H&R Block Inc. reported a profit for its fiscal third quarter after including proceeds from the sale of its Interim Services Inc. unit.
Before the gain, H&R Block had a loss from continuing operations of $11.5 million.
Block, of Kansas City, Mo., the world's largest tax-preparation concern, said earnings totaled $12.5 million, or 12 cents a share, on revenue of $229.4 million, compared with net of $8.6 million, or eight cents a share, on revenue of $190.7 million previously.
The latest earnings included the gain of $27.3 million from the initial public offering of Interim.
For the nine months ended Jan. 31, losses from continuing operations totaled $30.4 million, compared with a year-ago loss of $10.7 million.

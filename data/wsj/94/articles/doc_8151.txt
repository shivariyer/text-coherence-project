The Dow Jones Industrial Average ended lower after pushing far into record territory.
Bond prices fell, and the dollar was mixed.
The Dow industrials slipped 1.69 points to 3912.79.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index fell 2.75 to 471.97, and the Nasdaq Composite Index dropped 3.63 to 790.65.
The industrial average climbed nearly 20 points before reversing course.
Analysts noted that even as the industrials were rallying, declining stocks outnumbered advancers and other major indexes were slumping.
A similar scenario occurred Friday when the Dow industrials soared more than 22 points and smashed through the 3900 level.
"It was a Dow phenomenon," said Joseph McAlinden, market strategist at Dillon, Read & Co.
"The broad market wasn't participating very much."
Two stocks saved the industrial average from an even bigger decline.
Caterpillar, which jumped 1 5/8 to 100 1/4 after reporting stronger-than-expected earnings Friday, and International Business Machines, which surged 3 3/8 to 58 5/8.
IBM, which reports its fourth-quarter earnings today, got a boost from news that Fidelity is buying the stock.
Analysts expect the company to earn as much as 80 cents a share, the first quarterly profit in more than a year.
World-wide, stock prices fell in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index lost 1.57 to 112.99.
Stock prices fell.
Volume totaled 296.9 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,206 issues fell and 934 rose.
Bond prices slipped.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue fell more than 1/4 point, or more than $2.50 for each $1,000 face amount, to yield 6.30%.
The dollar was mixed.
In late New York trading the currency was quoted at 1.7508 marks and 111.70 yen compared with 1.7540 marks and 111.60 yen Friday.

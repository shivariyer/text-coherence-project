AMPHENOL Corp., a Wallingford, Conn., maker of connectors and cables, said it registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission for an offering of Class A common stock, including about four million new shares, and about 3.7 million shares by about a dozen top managers.
Amphenol said it will use its proceeds to prepay debt.
The executives are all selling less than 10% of their holdings, the company said.
In New York Stock Exchange trading, Amphenol shares fell $1.125 a share, or 6.6%, to close at $16.

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Bally's Park Place Funding Inc. -- $425 million of first-mortgage notes due March 15, 2004, priced as 9.25s at 97.618 to yield 9.625%.
The notes are noncallable for five years.
Rated single-B-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Bally's Funding is a unit of Bally Manufacturing Corp.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of debentures due March 3, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.17%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 13 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $150 million of notes due March 8, 1999, priced initially at par to yield 5.94%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 24 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Republic National Bank of New York -- $1 billion of notes due March 8, 1995, priced initially at par to yield 4.3%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 10 basis points above one-year Treasury bills.
Rated double-A-1 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Republic National Bank of New York is a unit of Republic New York Corp.
Quality Dining Inc. -- initial offering of 2.5 million common shares priced at $11.50 each through underwriters led by Morgan Keegan & Co. and McDonald & Co.
Georgia -- $215.9 million of Series 1994B general-obligation improvement bonds apparently won by a Merrill Lynch & Co. group.
Yields range from 3.6% in 1996 to 5.45% in 2013 and 2014.
The $20 million Series 1994A improvement bonds are believed to have been won by a Prudential Securities Inc. group.
Yields range from 2.9% in 1995 to 4.3% in 1999.
Georgia's bonds have a triple-A rating from Moody's and a double-A-plus rating from Standard & Poor's Corp.
New York State -- $224.1 million of general-obligation improvement bonds apparently won by a PaineWebber Inc. group.
Yields range from 3.65% in 1996 to 5.75% in 2015-24.
The bonds have a single-A rating from Moody's and a single-A-minus rating from S&P.
Norfolk, Va. -- $182.7 million of Series 1994 improvement and refunding bonds apparently won by a Merrill Lynch & Co.
Yields for reoffered bonds range from 3% in 1995 to 5.55% in 2012 and 2013.
Moody's and S&P rated the bonds double-A.
Honda Motor Co. -- $404.2 million of certificates priced through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
Honda Auto Receivables 1994-A Grantor Trust, was priced as 4.8s at 99.859375 to yield 4.95%, a yield spread of 40 basis points more than the Treasury's 3.875% notes of August 1995.
The certificates were rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P based on 7.25% subordinate certificates retained by the issuer and a reserve account.
Western Financial Savings Bank -- $200 million of auto asset-backed certificates priced by sole manager Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The certificates were sold through Western Financial Auto Loan Grantor Trust 1994-1, a special-purpose vehicle.
The $184 million of senior certificates were priced as 5.1s at 99.8504 to yield 5.22%, a spread of 41 basis points above the Treasury's 4.625% notes of February 1996.
The certificates have an average life of 1.85 years.
Details on the $16 million subordinate class weren't available immediately.
The issue was rated triple-A by S&P based on a 100% insurance wrap provided by the Financial Security Assurance Inc.
Western Financial is a unit of Westcorp.

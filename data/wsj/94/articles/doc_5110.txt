A strong stock-market rally fizzled out, leaving prices modestly higher.
Bond prices and the dollar ended slightly lower.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 9 points to 3937.27.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index rose 0.27 to 472.79, and the Nasdaq Composite Index jumped 2.50 to 792.62.
Stock prices started the day on a strong note, with the industrial average shooting up 21 points by midmorning.
But profit-taking soon swept in.
"Traders were all over the lot buying and selling in hit-and-run type actions," said Alan R. Ackerman, market strategist at Reich & Co.
"It makes the market more volatile than one would like."
Utility stocks slumped.
The Dow Jones Utility Average, often an indicator of future interest-rate trends, fell 2.70 to 212.76, continuing its recent decline.
Since Jan. 31, the average has fallen nearly 6%.
"The market is clearly prepared for somewhat higher interest rates, which is understandable given that the economy is growing," said Charles Clough, chief investment strategist at Merrill Lynch.
World-wide, stock prices rose in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index gained 0.21 to 116.39.
Stock prices rose.
Volume totaled 295.4 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,183 issues rose and 965 fell.
Bond prices fell.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue lost less than an eighth of a point, or less than $1.25 for each $1,000 face amount, to yield 6.46%.
The dollar slipped.
In late New York trading it was quoted at 1.7233 marks and 103.80 yen, compared with 1.7305 marks and 103.70 yen Tuesday.

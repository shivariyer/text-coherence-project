MBNA Corp. said fourth-quarter net income rose 22%, and directors approved a 3-for-2 stock split and a 12.5% increase in the quarterly dividend.
The Newark, Del., bank credit-card concern said net income rose to $63.3 million, or 63 cents a share, from $51.8 million, or 52 cents a share, a year earlier.
The stock split is effective Feb. 14 to shares of record Feb. 2.
The company declared a quarterly dividend of 18 cents a share, payable April 1, to stock of record March 18.
The dividend is equal to 27 cents on a pre-split basis; the company previously paid 24 cents quarterly.
MBNA said it added 3.4 million credit-card customers during the year, boosting the total to more than 10 million.
Credit-card charge volume during the year rose 23% to $17.9 billion.
Total assets at year-end stood at $6.95 billion, up from $5.98 billion at the end of 1992.
Managed loans, which include loans packaged and sold as securities, rose 25% to $12.4 billion.

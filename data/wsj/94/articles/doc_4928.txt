Orange & Rockland Utilities Inc. posted sharply lower fourth-quarter profit because of expenses related to criminal investigations of three former employees.
The utility's net income fell 39% to $5.8 million, or 36 cents a share, from $9.5 million, or 63 cents a share.
Meanwhile, revenue rose 11% to $254.7 million from $229.1 million.
Orange & Rockland, based in Pearl River, N.Y., said investigatory and litigation expenses totaled $6.1 million for all of 1993, with about $5 million incurred in the fourth quarter.
A former vice president pleaded guilty to making illegal campaign contributions, receiving commercial bribes and grand larceny.
Two other former employees pleaded guilty to stealing from the utility through a phone billing scheme.
Orange & Rockland said that investigation expenses of between $3 million and $6 million are estimated for this year.
Orange stock fell 62.5 cents, or 1.6%, to close at $37.625 a share yesterday in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.

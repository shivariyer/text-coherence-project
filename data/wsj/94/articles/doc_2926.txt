LDDS Communications Inc. said it agreed with EmtelCuba to provide direct long-distance telephone service to Cuba, subject to approval by the Federal Communications Commission and the Treasury Department.
LDDS, a long-distance carrier, said it would begin providing the service to Cuba as soon as the FCC completes its review, which is expected to take 60 days.
LDDA the new service should help alleviate the shortage of circuits to the island.
The application, according to LDDS, follows State Department guidelines, under which U.S. companies can be licensed to provide new services to Cuba.
LDDS characterized the agreement as the first of "several" accords that it expects to make in international markets.

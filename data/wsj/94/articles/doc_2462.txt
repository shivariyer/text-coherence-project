LTX Corp. said it reduced its worldwide work force by about 10%, or more than 100 people, and initiated a program to eliminate excess facilities as part of a major restructuring.
The semiconductor test equipment company said in Westwood, Mass., that it took a restructuring charge of $14.4 million in its second quarter, ended Jan. 31.
The company also took a charge of $3.5 million for excess inventories.
For the second quarter, LTX reported a net loss of $24 million, or 96 cents a share, including the charges, on sales of $38.1 million.
A year ago, the company reported a loss of $1.7 million, or eight cents a share, on sales of $39.3 million.

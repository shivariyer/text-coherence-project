American International Group said its fourth-quarter net income rose 11%.
The results, in line with analysts' expectations, pushed the big New York insurance company's profit to a record for any quarter of $510.2 million, or $1.61 a share, compared with $458.9 million, or $1.44 a share, during the same period of 1992.
Revenue rose 10% to $5.36 billion from $4.86 billion.
All three of AIG's major businesses -- property and casualty, life insurance and financial services -- reported higher levels of operating profit, which excludes capital gains.
In comments to stock analysts, AIG said that it expects to lose $50 million pretax on the Los Angeles earthquake.
In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, AIG closed at $87.375, down $1.25.

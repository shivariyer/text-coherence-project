Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were mixed in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month smalldenomination "savings" CDs inched up to 2.82% from 2.77%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs also rose a bit to 4.54% from 4.49%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs remained at 3.17%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield fell to 4.44% from 4.64%, the survey said.
On large-denomination, "jumbo," CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate climbed to 2.94% from 2.88%.
On five-year jumbos, the average rose to 4.81% from 4.75%, according to Banxquote.

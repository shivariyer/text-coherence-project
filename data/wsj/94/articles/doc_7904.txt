Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms changed little in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs slipped to 2.72% from 2.73%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs eased to 4.44% from 4.45%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs edged down to 3.10% from 3.12%, according to Banxquote.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield remained at 4.48%, the survey said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate slid to 2.76% from 2.78%.
On five-year jumbos, the average remained at 4.63%, Banxquote reported.

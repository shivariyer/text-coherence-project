Shares of Applied Innovation Inc. jumped 7.8% after the company announced a 2-for-1 stock split and said it expects to post stronger earnings for 1993.
The company, which makes network-management products for telecommunications companies, expects to report net income of $3.6 million for the fourth quarter, up from $1.5 million a year ago.
The Worthington, Ohio, company's shares rose $3.74, to a new 52-week high of $51.75 on the Nasdaq market.
The 2-for-1 stock split is payable March 2 to stock of record Feb. 16.
The company had 3.6 million shares outstanding at the end of December.

Petroleum Development Corp. said Fidelity Low Priced Stock Fund purchased 948,000 shares, or 9.1%, of the company's common stock outstanding.
In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the fund, managed by Fidelity Investments, Boston, said its purchase of the shares is for investment purposes only.
The company's largest shareholder, PNC Bank Corp., holds 1.2 million shares.
Petroleum Development, a Bridgeport, W.Va., independent oil and gas concern, has the option to repurchase the shares during the next four years.
Dale Rettinger, Petroleum Development executive vice president, said the company was "pleased to have Fidelity as a major shareholder."
Petroleum Development has about $24 million in annual revenue.
Petroleum Development shares rose 6.25 cents to $1.75 in Nasdaq Stock Market trading yesterday.

Federal regulators gave final approval to Viacom Inc.'s planned acquisition of Paramount Communications Inc., clearing Viacom's plan to take ownership of Paramount's seven television licenses.
With the Federal Communications Commission's clearance, Viacom can acquire the broadcast licenses for three Fox network affiliates in Detroit, Raleigh-Durham, N.C., and San Antonio, and four independent stations in Philadelphia, Washington, Dallas and Houston.
Separately, Paramount reported a loss for the third quarter ended Jan. 31.
The media concern had a net loss of $36.6 million, or 31 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $66.8 million, or 56 cents a share, the year earlier.
Revenue increased 7.1% to $1.01 billion from $943.7 million.
In American Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, Viacom fell 25 cents to close at $32.50.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Paramount rose 37.5 cents to $46.50.

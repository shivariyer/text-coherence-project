The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Capital Re Limited Life Co. -- $75 million of cumulative monthly income preferred shares priced with a 7.65% dividend yield.
The three million shares were priced at $25 each, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The preferred shares, which are noncallable for five years, are rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Capital Re Limited Life Co. is a unit of Capital Re Corp.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $150 million of notes due Jan. 25, 1999, priced at par to yield 5.34%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 23 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Paging Network Inc. -- $300 million of senior subordinated notes due Feb. 15, 2006, priced at par to yield 8.875%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for five years, are rated single-B-2 by Moody's and single-B-1 by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
W.R. Berkley Corp. -- $150 million of cumulative, perpetual, preferred stock priced with a 7.375% dividend yield.
The shares were prices at $25 each, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
Noncallable for five years, the issue is rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-1 by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Lincoln Snacks Co. -- initial offering of 2.2 million common shares priced at $4.50 each through underwriters led by Allen & Co.
Papa John's International Inc. -- 1.2 million common shares priced at $26.25 each, according to the company.
The lead underwriter is Montgomery Securities.
JP Realty Inc. -- initial offering of 11.3 million common shares priced at $17.50 each through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $400 million of realestate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by PaineWebber Inc.
The offering, Series 1678, is backed by the agency's 15-year, 6% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 6.6% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 179 months.
Goldlion Capital Ltd. (Hong Kong parent) -- $50 million of convertible Eurobonds due Feb. 1, 1999, at issue price par with coupon indicated between 4.75% and 5.25% via CS First Boston.
Terms fixed on or before Jan. 20.
Convertible into ordinary shares of Goldlion Holdings at indicated premium 15% to 18% and conversion period runs from March 1 to Jan. 15, 1999.
Callable from second year at 103, declining 1% per annum subject to share price being at least 150% of conversion price.
Fees 2.5.
Interest payable semiannually.
Sweden (sovereign) -- 500 million Dutch guilders of 5.75% Eurobonds due Feb. 11, 2004, at issue price 100.25 via Rabobank Nederland.
Reoffered at 99.55 to yield 5.811%, a margin of 20 basis points above comparable Dutch state loan.
Fees one.
Toyobo Co. (Japan) -- five billion yen of floating-rate Euronotes due Jan. 20, 1998, at issue price par via Mitsubishi Finance International PLC.
Interest payable quarterly and floats at 27.5 basis points over three-month yen London Interbank Offered Rate.
Fees 0.2.

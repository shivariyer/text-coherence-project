Media General Inc. said fiscal fourth-quarter net income jumped 20% on continued improvement in newspaper and broadcast-television operations.
For the quarter ended Dec. 26, the Richmond, Va., media company reported earnings of $9.1 million, or 34 cents a share, compared with $7.6 million, or 29 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose 5.1% to $156.5 million from $148.9 million.
J. Stewart Bryan, chairman and chief executive officer, said that except for newsprint pricing, which continues a downward trend, economic prospects appear more favorable for 1994 than for the past several years.
For the year, net income rose to $25.7 million, or 98 cents a share, up 35% from $19 million, or 73 cents a share, in the year-earlier period.
Revenue was up 4% to $600.8 million.
In composite American Stock Exchange trading Media General shares were unchanged at $27.625.

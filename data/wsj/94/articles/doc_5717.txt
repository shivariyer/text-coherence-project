Hasbro Inc. said fourth-quarter net income rose 8%, even after a restructuring charge, on a 12% revenue increase.
The nation's biggest toy maker, which is based in Pawtucket, R.I., said net income rose to $70.7 million or 78 cents a share from $65.6 million or 73 cents a share.
Net in the latest period included a previously reported charge of $15.5 million related to costs of closing a facility in the Netherlands and laying off 162 employees there.
The company said profit would have risen 22% without the charge.
Revenue rose to $932.2 million from $831.3 million.
In American Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Hasbro shares rose 25 cents to $34.125.

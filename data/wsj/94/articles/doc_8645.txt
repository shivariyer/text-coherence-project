Keystone Financial Inc. said it signed a definitive agreement to acquire Frankford Corp., a southeastern Pennsylvania banking company with about $661 million in assets.
Keystone, a bank holding company based in Harrisburg, Pa., will pay about $131.5 million in stock, or 1.333 Keystone shares for each Frankford share.
Keystone said the acquisition would complement its suburban Philadelphia operations.
The acquisition, together with another pending purchase, will boost Keystone's assets to about $4.5 billion.
In Nasdaq Stock Market trading, Keystone shares closed at $29.75, down 25 cents, while Frankford shares slipped 37.5 cents to $37.125.

Comsat Corp. reported a 7% increase in fourth-quarter profit, reflecting growth in its video-on-demand and satellite-based wireless services.
Net income totaled $18.1 million, or 44 cents a share, up from $16.9 million, or 43 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose to $167.5 million from $156.5 million.
Comsat provides voice, video and data services and is the largest owner of the global Intelsat and Inmarsat communications satellite networks.
For all of 1993, revenue rose to $640.4 million from $563.6 million, and the company said it is "on track" to report revenue of $1 billion by 1996.
Revenue for satellite-based wireless services for mobile customers rose 20% to $190 million due to traffic growth, while revenue increased 22% to $95.8 million in the video-on-demand unit, reflecting higher demand by the hotel industry.
Revenue in the international communications segment, which relates to satellite-based phone and video services between countries, were flat with 1992 results of $250 million.
Net income for 1993 totaled $75.3 million, or $1.85 a share, compared with $42.9 million, or $1.09 a share, in 1992.
Separately, Comsat and Radiation Systems Inc. said two class-action lawsuits by purported shareholders have been filed in Nevada over the previously announced merger involving the two companies.
Radiation Systems makes a broad range of communication systems and components.
The suit seeks, among other things, to enjoin the merger, Comsat said.
Comsat and Radiation Systems said that lawsuits are without merit.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Comsat fell 37.5 cents to $26.625.

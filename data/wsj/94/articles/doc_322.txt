Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms changed little in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month, small-denomination "savings" CDs remained at 3%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs crept up to 4.74% from 4.72%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month brokersold CDs remained at 3.46%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 5.01% from 4.93%, the survey said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate inched up to 3.21% from 3.19%.
On five-year jumbos, the average yield rose to 5.14% from 5.10%, according to Banxquote.

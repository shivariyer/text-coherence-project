Ingersoll-Rand Co. said it signed a letter of intent to acquire the sales and service arm of Ecoair, a subsidiary of Oberhausen, Germany-based MAN Gutehoffnungshutte AG.
The German company makes airends, a key component in industrial air compressors.
Ingersoll-Rand, based in Woodcliff Lake, N.J., said it also will form a 50-50 joint venture with MAN Gutehoffnungshutte to develop and make rotary-screw airends.
The new company, based in Oberhausen, will market airends to compressor-packaging manufacturers, including Ingersoll-Rand.
Terms of the agreement weren't disclosed.
Ingersoll-Rand is a maker of industrial equipment, bearings, door hardware and power tools, with annual revenue of $4 billion.

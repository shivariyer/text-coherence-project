The Texas Economic Index, a weekly leading indicator, fell to 111.5 in the latest week from 111.7 in the prior week, as the help wanted index tumbled and Texas refinery output fell.
On the positive side, initial unemployment claims were down from week to week.
On a year-over-year basis, the growth rate of the index fell to 2.52% from a revised 2.57% a week earlier.
Leading Index, which is national, and the Consumer Confidence Index, which tracks Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana and Arkansas U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, American Petroleum Institute, The Conference Board, CIBCR at Columbia University

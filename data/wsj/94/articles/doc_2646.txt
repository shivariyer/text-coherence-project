Petroleum Heat & Power Co. said reduced costs and marketing expenses helped push fourth-quarter earnings 74% higher.
For the quarter, the Stamford, Conn., heating-oil distributor reported net income of $8.9 million, or 41 cents a share, compared with net income of $5.1 million, or 22 cents a share.
Revenue declined 6% to $161.1 million from $171.5 million.
The company said fourth quarter 1993 winter weather in its northeastern markets was actually warmer than 1992.
The brutal winter weather didn't affect the company's markets until mid-December.
In Nasdaq Stock Market trading, the company's Class A shares rose 12.5 cents to $8.50.
For the year, its net loss widened to $8.4 million, or 57 cents a share, from a loss of $4.4 million, or 81 cents a share, in 1992.
Revenue rose 5.1% to $538.5 million.

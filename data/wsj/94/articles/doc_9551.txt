Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were little changed in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs remained at 2.74%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs also remained steady at 4.46%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs fell to 3.12% from 3.17%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 4.48% from 4.44%, the survey said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 or more, the average yield on a six-month certificate fell to 2.79% from 2.8%.
On five-year jumbos, the average fell to 4.66% from 4.68%, Banxquote said.

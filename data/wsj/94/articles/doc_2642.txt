Mesa Airlines said it offered to acquire another regional airline, CCAIR Inc., in a stock transaction valued at about $32 million.
Under terms of the offer, Mesa said it would exchange about 1.5 million Mesa shares for the roughly seven million CCAIR shares outstanding, valuing them at about $4.50 a share.
CCAIR, of Charlotte, N.C., said its board was studying Mesa's offer.
Jonathan Ornstein, a Mesa executive vice president, said he hopes the directors will view the offer "favorably."
CCAIR flies in the Southeast while Mesa flies under a number of names in various regions.
In late Nasdaq Stock Market trading, Mesa shares were trading at $21.25, down 50 cents.
CCAIR stock was up 12.5 cents at $4.375.

Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp., or Freddie Mac, said that fourth-quarter net income jumped 32% to $204 million, or $1.04 a share.
That compares with net income of $155 million, or 80 cents a share, a year earlier.
The government-sponsored concern said revenue rose 24% to $521 million from $421 million a year earlier.
The company's profit comes primarily from fees earned from buying mortgages and packaging them as securities, and interest collected on the loans it keeps as investment.
In the quarter, Freddie Mac's return on its retained mortgage portfolio increased 41% to $324 million, compared with $230 million a year earlier.
In New York Stock Exchange trading yesterday, Freddie Mac shares closed at $54.75, up $1.375.
For the year, net grew 26% to $786 million, or $4.07 a share, from $622 million, or $3.29 a share, the year before.
Revenue rose 19% to $2.01 billion from $1.69 billion.

Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms rose in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs increased to 2.99% from 2.93%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs rose to 4.70% from 4.63%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs jumped to 3.46% from 3.23%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 4.84% from 4.74%, the survey said.
On large-denomination, "jumbo," CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate crept to 3.13% from 3.11%.
On five-year jumbos, the average edged up to 5.04% from 4.99%, according to Banxquote.

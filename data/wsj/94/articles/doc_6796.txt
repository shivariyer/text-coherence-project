Canadian wheat stocks totaled 1.02 billion bushels at Dec. 31, a 2% increase from one billion bushels a year earlier, Statistics Canada, a government agency, said.
The stocks on farms and at elevators and other commercial locations included 137.1 million bushels of durum wheat, a 1.3% decline from 138.9 million a year earlier.
Durum wheat is used to make pasta products.
Rapeseed stocks at Dec. 31 totaled 150.8 million bushels, a 28% increase from 118.1 million a year earlier.
Rapeseed, also known as canola, is Canada's biggest oil seed crop.

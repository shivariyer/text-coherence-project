Yields on taxable money-market funds were little changed in the latest week.
IBC/Donoghue's average seven-day compound yield on taxable funds remained at 2.72% in the week ended Tuesday, according to Money Fund Report, an Ashland, Mass., newsletter.
Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.
The seven-day simple yield on taxable funds slipped to 2.68% from 2.69%, while the 30-day simple yield eased to 2.71% from 2.72%.
The 30-day compound yield edged down to 2.74% from 2.75%.
Assets of 637 taxable funds rose by $4.34 billion, to $498.26 billion, the newsletter said.
Institutional investors added $6.06 billion to taxable assets, while individual investors withdrew $1.73 billion.
Average maturity of the taxable funds' investments, which include commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and Treasury bills, lengthened by one day to 60 days.
For tax-exempt funds, the average seven-day compound yield rose to 1.77% from 1.68% in the latest week, according to Money Fund Report.
The latest yield is equivalent to a taxable 2.77% for someone paying 36% in taxes and to 2.93% for those who pay 39.6%.
Assets of 340 tax-exempt funds fell by $1.23 billion, to $110.45 billion, the newsletter said.
The tax-free funds' average portfolio maturity remained at 58 days.
Money Fund Report is published by IBC/Donoghue Inc., a subsidiary of IBC USA (Publications) Inc.

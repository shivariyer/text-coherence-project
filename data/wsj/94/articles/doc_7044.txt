Oil-services giant Schlumberger Ltd. said it plans to invest $7 billion over five years on equipment, new-product development and streamlining.
Speaking to financial analysts, Chairman Euan Baird also said the company expects to keep about $1 billion liquid in order to sustain its investments and "to react quickly when a new opportunity to enhance our business arises."
Mr. Baird said the New York-based company will spend $4 billion on new oil-field equipment; $1.5 billion on oil-field-product development, software and communications; and $1.5 billion on streamlining some products and on acquisitions.
In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Schlumberger closed at $59 a share, down $0.375.

Canada's gross domestic product rose 0.3% in November from October, said Statistics Canada, a government agency.
The November rise followed GDP increases of 0.2% in October and 0.5% in September.
GDP is the total value of goods and services produced in a nation.
Manufacturing, a major part of GDP, rose 0.6% in November, led by a 2.1% increase in output of electrical and electronic products.

Coca-Cola Enterprises Inc., Atlanta, reported a fourth-quarter profit, against a year-earlier loss, as the company benefited from gains in volume and lower packaging costs.
The large Coke bottler said net income totaled $4 million, or three cents a share, compared with a loss of $31 million, or 24 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose 3% to $1.32 billion from $1.28 billion.
The stock closed at $17.875, up $1.375, in composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange.
The company, which is 43%-owned by Coca-Cola Co., also in Atlanta, said that sales of bottled and canned soft drinks rose about 3% in the fourth quarter, excluding the effect of acquisitions.
Fountain gallon volume, again excluding the effect of acquisitions, increased about 6%.
For the year, Coca-Cola Enterprises had a net loss of $15 million, or 11 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $186 million, or $1.45 a share, a year earlier.
The 1993 results include a charge of $40 million, or 31 cents a share, for the restatement of deferred tax liability.

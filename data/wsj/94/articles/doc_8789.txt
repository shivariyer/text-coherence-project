Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms were generally flat in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs eased to 2.73% from 2.74%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs slid to 4.45% from 4.46%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs remained at 3.12%, while the average yield on five-year broker-sold CDs stayed at 4.48%, the survey said.
On large-denomination, or "jumbo," CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate slipped to 2.78% from 2.79%.
On five-year jumbos, the average inched down to 4.63% from 4.66%, according to Banxquote.

Society Corp. of Cleveland and KeyCorp, Albany, N.Y., said the Federal Reserve Board approved the two companies' application to merge to create the 10th largest U.S. banking organization.
Shareholders of both bank holding companies will vote on the plan in separate meetings Feb. 16.
The transaction is expected to be completed March 1.
Under the agreement, KeyCorp shareholders will receive 1.205 common shares of the new company for each of their KeyCorp shares.
Shareholders of Society will retain their existing number of shares.
The merged company will be called Key Bancshares Inc. and will be based in Cleveland, with nearly $60 billion in assets and banking affiliates in 13 states.

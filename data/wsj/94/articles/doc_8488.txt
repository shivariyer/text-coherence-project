First Data Corp. said fourth-quarter profit jumped 22% because of continued growth in base businesses.
The Hackensack, N.J., information processing company said net income for the quarter rose to $52.9 million, or 48 cents a share, from $43.4 million, or 39 cents a share, a year earlier.
The 1993 quarter included a $12.4 million gain from sale of its WATS Marketing of America Inc. unit and a nonrecurring charge of $13.8 million for consolidation of data-processing operations.
Revenue for the quarter rose nearly 19% to $383.4 million.
The company said that it expects revenue and earnings to grow 20% this year.
Net income for 1993 rose 22% to $173 million, or $1.56 a share, from $141.4 million, or $1.30 a share, in 1992.
Revenue grew 24% to $1.49 billion.

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Bayou Steel Corp. -- $75 million of first-mortgage notes due March 1, 2001, priced at par to yield 10.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes are noncallable for four years and are rated single-B-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-B by Standard & Poor's Corp.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Chemical Securities Inc.
Dairy Mart Convenience Stores Inc. -- $75 million of senior subordinated notes due March 15, 2004, priced at par to yield 10.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes are noncallable for five years and are rated single-B-3 by Moody's and single-B by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.
Doman Industries Ltd. -- $425 million of senior notes due March 15, 2004, priced at par to yield 8.75%.
The issue is noncallable for five years.
Rated Ba3 by Moody's and double-B-minus by S&P, the issue was sold through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of notes due March 3, 1997, priced initially at par to yield 5.19%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 13 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $150 million of medium-term notes due March 7, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.31%.
The notes, noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 33 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Niagara Mohawk Power Corp. -- $210 million of first-mortgage bonds due March 1, 2001, priced as 6.875 at 99.65 to yield 6.939%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 95 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Baa2 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Reebok International Ltd. -- three million common shares priced at $33.125 each through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Total Containment Inc. -- initial offering of 1.6 million common shares priced at $9.50 each through underwriters led by Pennsylvania Merchant Group Ltd.
Delaware Group Global Dividend & Income Fund Inc. -- 6.7 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Southern Africa Fund Inc. -- initial offering of six million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co. and Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $500 million of realestate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by CS First Boston Inc.
The offering, Series 1711, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.6% and weighted average maturity of 359 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $720 million of Remic securities offered by Bear, Stearns & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-50, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 6.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.125% and weighted average maturity of 358 months.
Abbey National First Capital BV (U.K. parent) -- 100 million Canadian dollars of collared, floating-rate Euronotes due March 25, 2004, at issue and reoffer price 99.5 via Wood Gundy Inc.
Payable semiannually at 30 basis points below six-month bankers' acceptance rate for Canadian dollars.
Minimum coupon 6.375%, maximum 9%.
Guarantor Abbey National PLC.
Fees 0.5.
Hydroelectrica Alicura (Argentina) -- $175 million of 8.375% Eurobonds due March 15, 1999, at issue and reoffer price 99.578 via book-runner Lehman Brothers International.
Bonds yield 8.48%, a margin of 285 basis points over when issued five-year U.S. Treasury.
Fees 1.125.
Payable semiannually.
Ispat Mexicana SA de CV (Mexico) -- $175 million of 10.375% Eurobonds due March 15, 2001, at issue and reoffer price par via CS First Boston Ltd.
Bonds yield margin of 443 basis points over comparable U.S. Treasury.
Bonds callable on March 15, 1999, at 103.458 and on March 15, 2000 at 101.79.
Issuer must repurchase 30% of face amount at 110 if it decides on or before March 15, 1997, to undertake initial public offering of its shares.
Fees 2.5.
Payable semiannually.

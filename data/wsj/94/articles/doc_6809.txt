When California voters were sold a sales-tax increase last year, they were promised the money would go to "public safety."
But in Riverside County, near Los Angeles, 5% of the funds will be going to "midnight basketball" and other social-service programs designed to divert youngsters from trouble, supervisors there have decided.
Maybe after-hours hoops deters crime better than expensive cops, but that wasn't what Clint Eastwood told the voters in the TV commercials that swung the election and made the public sector's day.

Flow International Corp. estimated a $1.8 million net loss for the fiscal third quarter ended Dec. 31, citing problems at a construction project, among other things.
The Kent, Wash., maker of high-pressure water jets and related products said the loss will equal 13 cents a share.
Analysts had been expecting the company to earn nine to 10 cents a share.
Flow didn't specify which construction project had caused its problems.
It also said its business had been hurt by cold winter weather and the Los Angeles earthquake.
However, the company said "longterm prospects are solid" and predicted a strong fiscal fourth quarter.
In Nasdaq Stock Market trading, Flow rose 6.25 cents to $8.

Canadian production of market pulp rose 8% in January from a year earlier, while shipments rose 26%.
The Canadian Pulp and Paper Association, an industry group, said Canadian mills produced 677,000 metric tons of market pulp, up from 624,000 metric tons.
A metric ton is equal to 2,204.62 pounds.
The operating rate was 94%, up from 91%.
Total market shipments in January rose to 643,000 tons from 511,000 tons.
Shipments to the U.S. rose 1%, to 203,000 tons.
Canadian producer inventories at the end of January were 578,000 tons, a 27-day supply, compared with 544,000 tons in December.
Market pulp is wood pulp sold on the open market to producers of paper and other products.

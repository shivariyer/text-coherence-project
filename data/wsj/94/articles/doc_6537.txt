The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Del Webb Corp. -- $100 million of senior subordinated debentures due Feb. 15, 2006, priced at par to yield 9%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures are not callable for five years.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Alantec Corp. -- initial offering of 2.5 million common shares priced at $13 each through underwriters led by Hambrecht & Quist and Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Morgan Stanley Africa Investment Fund Inc. -- initial offering of 15.4 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Prudential Securities Inc.
The offering, Series 1994-34, is backed by the agency's 15-year, 6% mortgage securities.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic securities offered by PaineWebber Inc.
The offering, Series 1994-36, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 6.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.1% and weighted average maturity of 358 months.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $400 million of Remic securities offered by Nomura Securities International Inc.
The offering, Series 1697, is backed by the agency's 15-year, 6% mortgage securities.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $85 million of notes due Feb. 14, 1996, priced at par to yield 4.22%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced flat to Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.

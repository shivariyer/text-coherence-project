Ecogen Inc. reported a narrower fourth-quarter net loss, reflecting increased revenue and a large charge in the year-earlier quarter.
The Langhorne, Pa., biotechnology company said its net loss was $3 million, or 17 cents a share, down from a loss of $9.3 million, or 54 cents a share.
The latest period included a special charge of $350,000, or two cents a share, for technology purchases, the company said.
The 1992 fourth quarter included special charges of $5.3 million, or 31 cents a share.
Revenue more than doubled to $4.8 million from $2.1 million.
The net loss for the year fell to $11.9 million, or 68 cents a share, from $21.4 million, or $1.26 a share.
Revenue soared to $19.7 million from $9 million.
"Our 1993 results reflect a trend of product revenue growth and product margin improvement that we expect will continue throughout 1994," Ecogen said.
It said it is committed to "achieving our goal of an operating profit by the end of 1994."
On the Nasdaq Stock Market, Ecogen closed at $7.25, unchanged.

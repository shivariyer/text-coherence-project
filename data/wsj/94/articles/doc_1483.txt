The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Delaware Management Holding Inc. -- $180 million of senior secured notes due March 15, 2004, priced at par to yield 10.25%.
The issue is noncallable for five years.
Rated Ba3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc. in the 144a private-placement market.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of index notes due March 25, 1999, priced with a 5.619% coupon at par.
The issue pays monthly and has a 5.685% yield, according to a NationsBanc official.
The notes are noncallable for one year, then will amortize based on prepayment speeds on Federal National Mortgage Association 7% mortgage-backed securities.
The notes were priced at a spread of 65 basis points above Treasurys and will be sold through NationsBanc Capital Markets Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $75 million of step-up notes due April 12, 1999, priced at par to yield 5.55%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The yield on the notes rises annually after the first year to 5.7%, 6.1%, 7.1% and 8.1%.
The notes are noncallable after the first year and were priced at a spread of 45 basis points above the five-year when-issued Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of notes due March 25, 2004, priced initially at par to yield 6.95%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, which are noncallable for five years, were priced at a spread of 47 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Greyhound Financial Corp. -- $150 million of notes due March 25, 1999, priced as 6.75s at 99.916 to yield 6.77%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 80 basis points above the Treasury when-issued five-year note.
Rated Baa2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Greyhound Financial is a unit of Greyhound Dial Corp.
Smith Barney Shearson Holdings Inc. -- $200 million of notes due March 15, 1997, priced as 6s at 99.957 to yield 6.015%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 65 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Shearson Inc.
The issuer is a unit of Travelers Inc.
Wilrig AS -- $110 million of senior secured notes due March 15, 2004, priced at par to yield 11.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, which are noncallable for five years, are rated single-B-2 by Moody's and single-B by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Argentina Fund Inc. -- 3.4 million common shares priced at $15.50 apiece through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Flextronics International -- initial offering of 2.5 million common shares priced at $14 apiece through underwriters led by Montgomery Securities and Cowen & Co.
Metricom Inc. -- 2.2 million common shares priced at $30.25 apiece through underwriters led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. and Cowen & Co.
Minnesota Educational Computing Corp. -- initial offering of 1,638,000 common shares priced at $11.50 apiece through underwriters William Blair & Co., Piper Jaffray Cos. and Salomon Brothers Inc.
The company said it is offering 1,170,000 of the shares, and a selling shareholder is offering 468,000.
Petco Animal Supplies Inc. -- initial public offering of three million common shares priced at $15.50 apiece through managing underwriters PaineWebber Inc. and CS First Boston Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $114.9 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by CS First Boston Inc.
The offering, Series 1718, is backed by the agency's three previously issued Remic classes, which in turn are backed by 30-year mortgage securities.
Green Tree Securitized Net Interest Margin Trust 1994 -- A $508 million of securitized net interest margin certificates priced as 6.9s at 99 30/32 to yield 7.02%, according to market officials.
The securities have an expected final maturity of Oct. 15, 1999, a legal final maturity of Feb. 15, 2004, and a 2.47-year average life.
Cash flow is backed by excess spread from previously issued Green Tree Financial Corp. manufactured-housing asset-backed securities.
The securities were priced 183 basis points above the 4.375% Treasurys maturing in August 1996.
Credit supports include a $142.9 million subordinated tranche, which will be retained, and a reserve fund totaling $88 million.
The securities are rated Baa3 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by Fitch Investors Service Inc. and will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.

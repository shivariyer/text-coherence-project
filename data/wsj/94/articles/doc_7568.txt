Ingersoll-Rand Co., citing improvement from restructuring its pump venture with Dresser Industries Inc., said fourth-quarter net income more than doubled.
Earnings totaled $67.8 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with $32.4 million, or 31 cents a share, a year earlier.
The year-earlier quarter included a $70 million pretax charge for restructuring of the 51%-owned Ingersoll-Dresser Pump Co. venture.
Sales were flat at $1.09 billion.
For the year, the company had net of $142.5 million, or $1.36 a share, countering a year-earlier loss of $234.4 million, or $2.25 a share.
Sales grew 6.3% to $4.02 billion from $3.78 billion.
Ingersoll-Rand, Woodcliff Lake, N.J., makes bearings, door hardware, tools, construction equipment and other machinery.
Dresser Industries, Dallas, is an energy-services concern.
In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Ingersoll-Rand closed at $39.50, up $2.25.

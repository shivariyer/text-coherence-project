CS First Boston Inc., New York, said net income soared 87% last year to a record $328 million.
The brokerage firm reported that its equity capitalization, or book value, rose 23% to $1.32 billion from $1.07 billion, and that long-term capitalization grew 54% to $4.65 billion from $3.02 billion.
The unit of CS Holding doesn't report quarterly results or give a detailed breakdown of how its various businesses performed.
The biggest contributor to profit was the trading and sales of fixed-income and derivative securities.
The company also reported strong results from underwriting new debt and equity securities.

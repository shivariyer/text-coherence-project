Discount-retailing giant Wal-Mart Stores Inc., extending its streak of rapid growth, said its profit surged 16% in its fiscal fourth quarter on a 19% revenue gain.
Net income in the period, which ended Jan. 31, totaled $868 million, or 38 cents a share, up from $749.6 million, or 33 cents a share, the year earlier.
Revenue rose to $20.54 billion from $17.27 billion.
Results of the Bentonville, Ark., company generally were in line with the expectations of analysts, many of whom predict Wal-Mart will continue its impressive growth.
Yesterday, in New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Wal-Mart's stock price rose 50 cents to close at $28.25.
For all of fiscal 1994, Wal-Mart's profit rose 17% to $2.33 billion, or $1.02 a share, from $1.99 billion, or 87 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue increased 21% to $67.99 billion from $55.98 billion.

Honda Motor Co. of Japan said it plans to increase its production in the U.S. and Canada by 18.4% this year to a record 597,000 vehicles.
Brisk demand is expected in the U.S. market.
And, "We will produce cars at places where we can sell," a Honda spokesman said.
Honda expects to market about 790,000 cars in the U.S. this year, up 10.3% from 1993.
It plans to build 494,000 cars, mostly Accord models, in the U.S. in 1994, up 22.3% from last year, and 103,000 cars in Canada, up 2.4 %.
Meanwhile, Honda's domestic production will be reduced by 5.1 % from a year earlier to 1.09 million cars this year, the spokesman said.
In 1993, Honda sold 579,127 cars including mini-cars in Japan.
Honda has a market share of 8.5% in Japan, making it fourth, behind Toyota Motor Corp., Nissan Motor Co. and Mitsubishi Motors Corp.

Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks changed little in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs eased to 2.71% from 2.72%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs slipped to 4.42% from 4.44%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate inched up to 2.77% from 2.76%, according to the survey.
On five-year jumbos, the average declined to 4.61% from 4.63%, Banxquote reported.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs fell to 3.02% from 3.10%, according to the Banxquote survey.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield remained at 4.48%, the survey said.

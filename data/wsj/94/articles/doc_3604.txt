Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms generally rose in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination, "savings" CDs edged up to 2.87% from 2.83%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs rose to 4.58% from 4.54%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs increased to 3.28% from 3.21%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield jumped to 4.66% from 4.52%, the survey said.
On large-denomination, "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate inched up to 3.02% from 2.97%.
On five-year jumbos, the average rose to 4.89% from 4.84%, according to Banxquote.

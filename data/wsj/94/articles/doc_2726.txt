Yields on certificates of deposit at major banks and brokerage firms mostly rose a bit in the week ended yesterday.
The average yield on six-month small-denomination "savings" CDs rose to 2.93% from 2.87%, according to a survey by Banxquote Money Markets.
The average yield on five-year savings CDs inched up to 4.63% from 4.58%, the Wilmington, Del., information service said.
The average yield on six-month broker-sold CDs slipped to 3.23% from 3.28%.
On five-year broker-sold CDs, the average yield rose to 4.74% from 4.66%, the survey said.
On large-denomination "jumbo" CDs, which typically require deposits of $95,000 and more, the average yield on a six-month certificate increased to 3.11% from 3.02%.
On five-year jumbos, the average rose to 4.99% from 4.89%, according to Banxquote.

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
American General Finance -- $150 million of senior notes due April 1, 1997, priced at par to yield 5.8%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 45 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
American General Finance is a unit of American General Corp.
Cold Metal Products Inc. -- initial public offering of 2,770,000 common shares priced at $10 apiece through lead underwriters McDonald & Co.
Securities Inc., NatWest Securities Ltd. and Fieldstone FPCG Services Limited Partnership.
Of the total, the issuer offered 2,078,000 shares and the company's principal shareholder, Aarque Management Corp. offered the remaining 692,000 shares.
Illinois -- $175 million March 1994 general-obligation bonds apparently won by a Morgan Stanley & Co. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 5.7048%.
Yields range from 4.3% in 1997 to 5.95% in 2019.
Moody's has rated the bonds double-A, and S&P has assigned a double-A-minus rating.
New York City -- $685.7 million fixed-rate, tax-exempt Fiscal 1994 bonds priced by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. group.
The $670.1 million Series H, Subseries H-1 bonds have yields that range from 4.45% in 1996 to 6.3% in 2011.
Yields for the $15.6 million Series I bonds range from 4.45% in 1996 to 6.3% in 2019-23.
The bonds are all refunding securities.
Moody's has rated the issue Baa1, and both S&P and Fitch Investors Service Inc. have assigned a single-A-minus ratings.
The bonds sold are part of an issue that is seen totaling about $1.1 billion.
A concurrent offering of $379.2 million of adjustable-rate bonds is expected.
Philadelphia School District -- $160 million Series A of 1994 general-obligation bonds priced by a Merrill Lynch & Co. group.
Yields range from 3.65% in 1995 to 5.85% in 2009.
The bonds are insured by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
Washoe County, Nev., School District -- $121.4 million general-obligation bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
Goldman Sachs is believed to have won the $103.9 million school-building bonds with a bid setting the true interest cost at 5.755%.
Yields range from 4.4% in 1997 to 5.95% in 2014.
The $17.6 million refunding bonds were apparently won by Goldman with a true-interest-cost bid of 5.5981%.
Those bonds have yields that range from 5.1% in 2002 to 5.7% in 2008.
The securities are insured by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
The offering, Series G031, is backed by 30-year 8% Government National Mortgage Association mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 8.5% and weighted average maturity of 339 months.
Chevy Chase Bank FSB -- $200 million issue of floating-rate, asset-backed certificates priced in two parts by underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
The certificates are backed by Visa and MasterCard receivables and were sold through Chevy Chase Master Credit Card Trust 1994-1, a special-purpose vehicle.
The $170 million in senior Class A certificates were priced at par with a coupon that floats 20 basis points above the one-month London Interbank Offered Rate.
The coupon is capped at a 9% interest rate.
The certificates have an average life of three years and were rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P.
The $30 million in subordinate Class B certificates were priced at par with a coupon that floats 40 basis points above one-month Libor.
The coupon is also capped at 9%.
The certificates have an average life of 3.79 years and were rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P.
South Australian Government Financing Authority (Australian agency) -- $500 million of floating-rate Euronotes due April 1996 at issue price par via Swiss Bank Corp.
Payable quarterly flat at three-month Libor for dollars.
Guarantor Treasurer of South Australia.
The borrower's senior debt outstanding is rated double-A-2 by Moody's and double-A by S&P.
The bonds are listed in Hong Kong and Luxembourg and are denominated in $10,000, $100,000 and $1 million.
Fees 0.1.

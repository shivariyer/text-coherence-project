Alcan Aluminium Ltd. said it will close its Richmond, British Columbia, fabricating plant, idled since last October.
The Montreal aluminum maker said 92 unionized and 20 nonunion employees will be affected.
The unionized workers had been on strike since last July; staff employees continued operating the plant until October.
The plant has an annual capacity of 28,000 metric tons of electrical rod and 6,000 metric tons of extrusions.
A metric ton is 2,204.62 pounds.
Alcan said it will offer a severance package and re-employment assistance to all affected employees.
It declined to estimate the cost of the closure.
Alcan has 44,000 employees world-wide and 46 fabricating mills in North America.

Southern Co. increased its quarterly dividend to 59 cents a share from 57 cents and declared a 2-for-1 stock split.
The Atlanta-based utility also reported that fourth-quarter earnings fell to $133 million, or 41 cents a share, from $141 million, or 45 cents a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose to $1.95 billion from $1.87 billion.
Discussing the dividend increase and the stock split, the company noted that its share price has more than doubled in the past five years.
In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, Southern closed at $42.875 a share, up 12.5 cents on volume of 255,500 shares.
The increased dividend is payable March 5 to stock of record Feb. 7.
The stock split is scheduled to take effect for stock of record at the close of business Feb. 7.
Currently, Southern has about 320 million common shares outstanding.
For all of 1993, the company reported earnings of $1 billion, or $3.14 a share, compared with $953 million, or $3.02 a share, a year earlier.
Revenue rose to $8.49 billion from $8.07 billion.

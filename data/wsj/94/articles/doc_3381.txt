Program trading for the week ended Feb. 25 accounted for 7.9%, or an average 23.7 million daily shares, of New York Stock Exchange volume.
Brokerage firms executed an additional 8.1 million daily shares of program trading away from the Big Board, mostly on foreign markets.
Program trading is the simultaneous purchase or sale of at least 15 different stocks with a total value of $1 million or more.
Of the program total on the Big Board, 45.3% involved stock-index arbitrage, up from 43.1% the prior week.
In this strategy, traders dart between stocks and stock-index options and futures to capture fleeting price differences.
Some 65.1% of program trading was executed by firms for their own accounts, or principal trading, while 29.8% was for their customers.
An additional 5.1% was designated as customer facilitation, in which firms use principal positions to facilitate customer trades.
Of the five most-active firms, Cooper Neff, UBS Securities, Nomura Securities and Kidder Peabody executed all or most of their program trading for their own accounts, while Morgan Stanley did most of its program trading for its customers.

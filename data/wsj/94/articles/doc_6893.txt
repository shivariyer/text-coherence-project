Brock Control Systems Inc., in a surging market for productivity aids, expects its business to grow 40% or slightly more by 1997, its chief executive officer said.
The Atlanta company, which provides software for automating sales, marketing and customer-service operations, also said it is "very comfortable" with analysts' fourth-quarter estimates, which call for net income of $1.4 million, or 31 cents a share, on revenue of $8 million.
Year-earlier net was $971,000, or 30 cents a share, on revenue of $5.8 million.
Rodger Johnson, president and CEO, said he is also comfortable with analysts' estimates for the full year, which call for earnings of 62 cents a share, up from a year-earlier 51 cents a share.
He said Brock anticipates growth in line with or "moderately" faster than the industry's.
Analysts expect the $550 million annual market to grow 40% by 1997.

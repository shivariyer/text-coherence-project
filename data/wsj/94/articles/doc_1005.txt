J.P. Morgan & Co.
Chairman Dennis Weatherstone earned nearly $6.6 million last year, up 45% from 1992, according to the company's proxy statement.
Net income at the blue-chip bank jumped 52% to a record $1.72 billion in 1993, up from $1.13 billion in 1992.
Mr. Weatherstone, 63 years old, had a base salary of $700,000 in 1993 and a bonus of roughly $2.3 million.
The pay package included restricted stock awards of about $3.5 million.
In addition to the $6.6 million in compensation, Mr. Weatherstone was granted options to purchase 70,000 shares at an exercise price of $71 each.
The company placed a potential value on the grants at roughly $3.1 million to $7.9 million, based on stock-appreciation rates of 5% and 10%.
Other Morgan executives saw a similar rise in their compensation.
President Douglas A. Warner III earned about $5.8 million last year, compared with nearly $4 million in 1992.
Morgan Vice Chairmen Roberto G. Mendoza and Kurt F. Viermetz each earned about $5.4 million in 1993, up from $3.7 million a year earlier.

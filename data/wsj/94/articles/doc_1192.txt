Coming out of a prolonged slump in demand for its trading cards, Topps Co. reported a fiscal fourth-quarter profit compared with a year-ago loss.
For the quarter ended Feb. 26, Topps, a marketer of collectible cards, posted net income of $4.6 million, or 10 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $21.4 million, or 45 cents a share, for the 1993 period.
Topps's revenue jumped 59% for the quarter to $64.1 million from $40.4 million.
The company said improvements in the sales of its sports-related cards helped results.
In the last couple of years, the company said the trading-card market had been saturated, squeezing profits.
In Nasdaq Stock Market trading, the stock rose 25 cents to $7.125.
For the year, the company posted net income of $26.6 million, or 57 cents a share, a 40% increase from $19 million, or 40 cents a share, last year.

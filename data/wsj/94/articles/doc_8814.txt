Earnings news took the spotlight yesterday as investors bid up small-company stock prices, pushing two key indexes to new highs.
The Russell 2000 Index gained 0.44 point, or 0.17%, to end at 262.4, setting its fifth straight closing record.
The index measures the performance of small-capitalization stocks traded on the Nasdaq Stock Market and the major exchanges.
After early weakness, the Nasdaq Composite Index set a new high of its own at 793.02, gaining 0.84 point, or 0.11%, from Monday and outperforming Friday's record of 792.31.
Nasdaq advancers trailed decliners, 1,466 to 1,492.
Nasdaq National Market volume was 274.2 million shares, up from Monday's 203.5 million.
Overall volume was 319.4 million shares, up from 243.5 million.
In earnings news, Borden Chemicals & Plastics rose 1 5/8 to 11 5/8 on the New York Stock Exchange.
The Columbus, Ohio, company's fourth-quarter net income was 12 cents a unit, up sharply from a year-earlier two cents a unit.
Analysts were looking for earnings of one cent a unit.
Shares of TransTechnology rose 7/8, or 6.9%, to a new high of 13 5/8 in Big Board trading, boosted by the company's Monday report of third-quarter per-share earnings from continuing operations of 39 cents a share.
In the year-earlier quarter, the Union, N.J., developer of technical products had a per-share net from continuing operations of 29 cents.
Telxon advanced 2 to 12 1/4 after reporting that its fiscal third-quarter loss narrowed to four cents a share from a year-earlier 63 cents a share.
Wall Street expected the Akron, Ohio, microprocessor designer to post a per-share loss of five cents.
Fiscal second-quarter net income of 28 cents a share, up from a year-earlier 10 cents a share, sent Cannon Express up 2 to 14 3/4.
Cannon is a Springdale, Ark., trucking concern.
Gupta, Menlo Park, Calif., climbed 3 1/2 to 24 1/8.
After Monday's close, the software company said fourth-quarter per-share earnings were 23 cents, above last year's nine cents and analysts' expectations of 20 cents.
Also late Monday, Powersoft, Burlington, Mass., reported fourth-quarter operating earnings of 25 cents a share, up from a year-earlier 19 cents a share.
The news, and a rating upgrade yesterday morning from Prudential Securities, lifted the software developer's stock 4 to 48.
But Jenny Craig, Del Mar, Calif., fell 1 1/4 to 7 1/2 after sliding 1 5/8 on Monday before trading was halted.
After Monday's close of markets, the provider of weightloss services said it earned four cents a share in its fiscal second quarter, down from the year-earlier period's 25 cents a share.
Innovex slipped 1 1/8 to 10 1/2 after reporting that fiscal first-quarter earnings from continuing operations fell to 13 cents a share from a year-earlier 19 cents a share.
The Hopkins, Minn., maker of electromagnetic products said second-quarter earnings will likely fall below last year's 23 cents a share for the period.
Market watchers said a cut in an earnings estimate for OshKosh B'Gosh by a Cleary Gull Reiland & McDevitt analyst may have pressured the stock, causing a 2 3/8 slide to 16 1/4.
A Dain Bosworth trader said Cleary Gull's estimates were high compared with others on Wall Street.
The Cleary Gull analyst was unavailable for comment.
Circa Pharmaceuticals climbed 2 1/4 to 12 7/8 on the American Stock Exchange.
Bear Stearns put an initial "buy" rating on the Copiague, N.Y., company and set a 12-month to 18-month price target of $20.
Among new issues, Peoples Bancorp of Indianapolis rose to 26 7/8 from yesterday morning's offering price of 25 1/2.
The holding company for Peoples Bank & Trust said proceeds are expected to be used for general corporate purposes.
In other action, Mark Controls, Skokie, Ill., jumped 2 5/8 to 13 7/8 following news that Big Board-traded Crane plans to acquire it for $13 a share.
Crane owns 13% of Mark, a maker of valves and other industrial and commercial products.
An initial "buy" rating from Oppenheimer pushed SafeCard Services, Cheyenne, Wyo., up 1 7/8 to 20 1/8 on the Big Board.
Ned Davis, a senior vice president at the brokerage firm, said he believes Paul G. Kahn's appointment Dec. 7 as SafeCard's chairman and chief executive officer will strengthen the company's management.
Nasdaq National Market stocks that hit new highs for the year to date totaled 127, while 22 hit new lows.

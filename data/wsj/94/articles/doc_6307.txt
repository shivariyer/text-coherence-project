Citing a prolonged price war in the market for computer keyboards, Key Tronic Corp. reported a second-quarter loss and said it expects to post losses for the rest of its fiscal year.
The computer keyboard maker, based in Spokane, Wash., said it posted a net loss of $2.3 million, or 28 cents a share, for the quarter ended Jan. 1.
That compares with year-earlier net income of $1.4 million, or 16 cents a share.
Sales rose 27% to $41.3 million from $32.6 million a year earlier.
For the six months, Key Tronic posted a loss of $3.9 million, or 48 cents a share, on sales of $77.3 million.
A gain from an accounting change resulted in first-half net income of $4.8 million, or 55 cents a share.
In the year-earlier period, the company posted net income of $2.3 million, or 27 cents a share, on sales of $64.2 million.

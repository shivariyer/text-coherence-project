Charles Schwab & Co. reported record trading activity for January and said client equity exceeded $100 billion.
In January, the San Francisco discount brokerage firm executed an average 51,700 trades a day, compared with 38,600 trades a day in December and 34,600 trades a day in January 1993.
Combined New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq Stock Market average daily volume increased to 634 million shares in January from 546 million shares in December and 525 million shares in January 1993.
Schwab said its clients' equity increased from $95.8 billion in December to $100.8 billion in January.
Schwab's client equity was $68.6 billion in January 1993.

Small-company stocks climbed higher but remained below their morning peaks before bonds pulled back from a modest advance.
The Russell 2000 Index of small-capitalization stocks rose 1.43, or 0.54%, to end at 265.96.
The Nasdaq Composite Index added 2.50, or 0.32%, to 792.62.
Advancing issues pushed ahead of Nasdaq decliners, 1,750 to 1,367, while Nasdaq National Market volume rose to 278,631,100 shares from 246,778,200 Tuesday.
Bond prices started the day slightly firmer but took a downward turn in the afternoon.
The Treasury's 30-year bond yield rose to 6.46%.
Choppy action on the Big Board also took a toll on smaller issues.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average bounced up and down in about a 25-point range, finishing up nine points at 3937.27.
Some smaller semiconductor and software stocks rose on the heels of good earnings from Applied Materials, which gained 2 to 49 1/2.
The Santa Clara, Calif., chip maker posted fiscal first-quarter operating earnings of 45 cents a share, up from 18 cents a year earlier.
Also, KLA Instruments Corp., San Jose, Calif., climbed 2 1/4 to 36 3/4; Bolt Beranek & Newman Inc., Cambridge, Mass., added 1/2 to 14 7/8 on the Big Board; and Cypress Semiconductor Corp., San Jose, Calif., rose 1/2 to 17 7/8, also on the New York Stock Exchange.
In IPO action, Martin Marietta Materials' offering was priced after the close of trading at $23 a share, or $2 above its original $19 to $21 a share.
Analysts have said the company should be a strong performer among popular small-cap cyclical stocks this year.
Coventry, a Nashville, Tenn., health maintenance organization, declined 3/4 to 52 1/8 following a Smith Barney Shearson downgrade to "outperform" from "buy."
Maxicare Health Plans, Los Angeles, rose 7/8 to 13 1/2 after a report on CNBC that said Physician Corp. of America has acquired nearly 5% of Maxicare and might try to take over the company.
Physician, of Miami, eased 1/2 to 28 1/4.
Optical Data Systems gained 1 1/2 to 14 on improved fourth-quarter earnings of 23 cents a share.
In the year-earlier quarter, the Richardson, Texas, computer-networking concern posted profit of 21 cents.
"There's still a lot of money around and very good demand," said Larry Kinney, head of over-the-counter trading at SoundView Financial Group.
He said investors held back to see if stocks would become cheaper, and then did some last-minute buying before the close.
Some observers said market players also grew hesitant because of another slide in the Dow Jones Utility Average, which fell 2.70 points to 212.76, its lowest level since July 1, 1992.
Many analysts say declines in the utility index augur a broader market pullback.
Greg Kuhn, who heads Kuhn Asset Management, said the fact that Wall Street is concerned about losses in the utility average is a good sign, since it breeds caution, always a positive for stocks.
He said a more troublesome sign would be if "we get to a point where everybody says it doesn't matter, and they ignore its warning."
According to Mr. Kuhn, yesterday's seesaw session is the market's way of "building a launching pad for the next move up."
Since Feb. 4, when the Federal Reserve said it would tighten monetary policy and equities tumbled, a normal "backing and filling" has occurred, he said.
SciClone Pharmaceuticals fell 1 to 19 5/8.
The San Mateo, Calif., company cited a delay in its bid to obtain marketing approval for Thymosin alpha 1 in China.
The drug is used to treat hepatitis B.
Neutrogena fell 1 1/2 to 19 3/4 after posting fiscal first-quarter earnings of 18 cents a share, below analysts' forecasts of about 22 cents.
In the year-earlier period, the Los Angeles maker of skin and hair-care products posted operating earnings of 17 cents.
Calgene gained 3/4 to 12 1/2.
The Davis, Calif., developer of genetically engineered plants and plant products said the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Animal, Plant Health Inspection Service approved its petition for its BXN bromoxynil resistant cotton varieties.
Checkers Drive-In Restaurants, Clearwater, Fla., dropped 1 1/2 to 8 3/4 following its release of fourth-quarter earnings of seven cents a share on charges that matched the net income a year earlier.
A disappointing earnings outlook pushed Handleman 1 3/4 lower to 11 1/2 on the Big Board.
The Troy, Mich., distributor of prerecorded music said it expects to report fiscal third-quarter net of 29 cents to 31 cents a share, down from 43 cents a year earlier.
A Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette downgrade to "neutral" from "moderately attractive" lopped 1 3/4 from D.R. Horton's stock price, sending it down to 17.
The Arlington, Texas, company constructs and sells single-family homes.
Today, the release of January consumer prices will be the focal point.
"There's a certain level of anxiety heading into these reports.
All the Fed's waiting for is to confirm their pre-emptive strike on raising interest rates," Mr. Kuhn said.

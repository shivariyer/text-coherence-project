Kansas City Southern Industries Inc. shares climbed sharply after the company said it is studying "strategic options" that might increase stockholder value.
The Kansas City, Mo., holding company said its board has retained outside consultants and an investment banker to help it analyze a "wide range of alternatives," ranging from "operational improvements to asset redeployments."
The company didn't disclose the identity of the consultants, who an official said have been instructed that "nothing is sacred."
Trading in Kansas City Southern shares was halted briefly in midsession pending the company's disclosure.
In composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, the company's shares closed at $48.875, up $3.50, or 7.7%.

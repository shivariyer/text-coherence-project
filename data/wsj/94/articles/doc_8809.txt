It wasn't the most exciting day but the stock market wasn't nearly as dull as depicted by the Dow Jones averages.
In a Wall Street rarity, both the Dow Jones Industrial Average and the Dow Jones Transportation Average closed unchanged: the industrials at 3,870.29 and the transportation index at 1,822.58.
It was the first time since July 27, 1967 that the industrial and transportation averages finished flat, according to Ned Davis Research Inc.
Yesterday's double unchanged session was only the second since 1926, the first year for which Ned Davis has data.
Elsewhere, most broad-based indexes managed modest gains and the Nasdaq Composite Index crept up 0.84, or 0.11%, to 793.02, a new high, topping a record that was set Friday.
Advancing stocks led decliners about 1,210 to 892 on the New York Stock Exchange.
Analysts tied much of the optimism to recent encouraging earnings reports, and to the modest strength in bonds.
The Treasury's 30-year issue added 1/4 point, pushing its yield down to 6.26%.
Low interest rates have been one of the market's most important props.
Big Board volume totaled 308.6 million shares, compared with 226.4 million shares Monday.
The Big Board Composite Index was 262.76, up 0.45.
The average price per share rose six cents.
Meanwhile, a surprisingly strong profit reading from Chrysler, and a series of solid reports from some of the largest U.S. banking companies helped set the positive tone yesterday.
The market was higher much of the day but prices were locked in a narrow range.
Chrysler's report sent its shares up 3/4 to 62 1/2 and inspired buying in shares of the other big auto makers, traders said.
Ford Motor increased 1/4 to 68 5/8 and General Motors climbed 1 3/8 to 62 1/8.
Chrysler posted earnings of $2.11 a share for the fourth quarter.
Analysts surveyed by Institutional Brokers Estimate System had forecast a profit of just $1.60.
A year ago, Chrysler had net income of $1.12.
Among banks, Citicorp edged up 1/8 to 41 1/4, Wells Fargo slipped 2 1/2 to 133 5/8, Chase Manhattan added 1/4 to 35 3/4 and NationsBank lost 1/2 to 49 1/4.
Each of those companies released improved earnings.
The Standard & Poor's 500 Stock Index added 0.95, or 0.20%, to 474.25.
The American Stock Exchange Market Value Index gained 0.16, or 0.03%, to 481.94.
Paramount Communications climbed 1 1/4 to 80 after Viacom sweetened its bid for the New York media company.
Viacom's new offer was valued at about $9.7 billion.
Viacom's Class B stock fell 1 3/8 to 37 3/4 on the Amex.
Rival bidder QVC Network eased 1/2 to 43 on the Nasdaq Stock Market.
On the Big Board, Blockbuster Entertainment slipped 1/4 to 27 7/8.
That company has agreed to merge with Viacom.
Cott fell 2 1/8 to 24 7/8 on Nasdaq.
Mabon Securities analyst Jennifer Solomon cut her opinion on the stock to "sell" from "buy."
She cut her estimate of the company's fourth-quarter earnings, and said she was concerned about increased price competition from other Canadian beverage companies.
BMC Software slumped 2 3/8 to 60 7/8 on Nasdaq.
Late Monday, the Sugar Land, Texas, company said it acquired Patrol Software, of Redwood Shores, Calif., and its Australian affiliate for $33.7 million in cash.
Santa Fe Pacific added 1 1/8 to 26 1/8.
The Schaumburg, Ill., company's Santa Fe Pacific Gold unit had 14.1 million ounces of proven and probable gold reserves at the end of last month.
That is up 121% from the level of so-called contained ounces it had at the end of 1992.
Penn Central slumped 1 1/2 to 31 1/2.
Salomon Brothers analyst Michael Frinquelli downgraded the Cincinnati company's stock to "hold" from "buy," citing increasing competition in the automobile insurance market.
He also downgraded his rating on Horace Mann Educators, Springfield, Ill.
That stock eased 1/4 to 23 1/8.
Sara Lee lost 7/8 to 23 3/8 on heavy volume of two million shares.
The company said it reacquired Kiwi Brands, a shoe-care products company in South Africa, that it had sold in 1986 because of economic sanctions against that country.
Terms weren't disclosed.
Sara Lee in Chicago, said it didn't know of any reason for the weakness in its stock.

The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
American Telephone & Telegraph Co. -- $400 million of notes due April 1, 2004, priced as 6.75s at 99.265 to yield 6.853%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 44 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
BankAmerica Corp. -- $300 million of subordinated notes due April 15, 2006, priced as 7.2s at 99.592 to yield 7.251%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 82 basis points above 10-year Treasurys.
Rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Farm Credit Banks -- $2.7 billion of consolidated systemwide bonds priced through Federal Farm Credit Banks Funding Corp.
Issues priced include $1.19 billion of bonds due July 1, priced at 3.65%, a spread of 11 basis points above comparable Treasurys; $730 million of bonds due Oct. 3, priced at 4%, a spread of 10 basis points above comparable Treasurys; and $780 million of bonds due April 3, 1995, priced at 4.43%, a spread of 14 basis points above comparable Treasurys.
Career Horizons Inc. -- initial offering of three million common shares priced at $17.50 each through underwriters led by Bear, Stearns & Co. and Robert W. Baird & Co.
Consumers Power Co. -- $200 million of cumulative, perpetual, preferred stock priced with a 8.32% dividend yield.
The eight million shares were priced at $25 each, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue is noncallable for five years and is rated Ba-1 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Consumers Power is a unit of CMS Energy Corp.
Dakotah Inc. -- initial public offering of one million shares priced at $5.125 each, according to the company.
Underwriters include Kennedy, Mathews, Landis, Healy & Pecora Inc.
Integrated Micro Products PLC -- initial offering of 1.8 million American depositary shares priced at $10 each through underwriters led by S.G. Warburg & Co. and Volpe Welty & Co.
Each ADR represents one ordinary share.
Super Vision International Inc. -- initial offering of 1.2 million units priced at $5 each through underwriters led by D.H. Blair Investment Banking Corp.
Each unit consists of one Class A common share, one Class A warrant and one Class B warrant.
Each Class A warrant is redeemable for one Class A common share and one Class B warrant at $7.50 thourgh March 21, 1999; each Class B warrant is redeemable for one Class A common share at $10.50 through March 21, 1999.
Highlander Income Fund Inc. -- initial offering of two million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Piper Jaffray Inc.
Anaheim, Calif. -- $95 million of 1994 taxable notes won by PaineWebber Inc.
The notes have a 5% coupon and are priced to yield 4.68% in April 1995.
Moody's has rated the issue MIG-1, and S&P has assigned an SP1-plus rating.
California Public Works Board -- $84 million of 1994 Series A lease-revenue bonds priced by a Bear, Stearns & Co. Inc. group.
Yields range from 4.25% in 1996 to 6.15% in 2014.
The bonds are rated a conditional single-A-1 by Moody's and a provisional single-A by S&P.
Phoenix -- $70.6 million of Series 1994 various-purpose general-obligation bonds won by a Prudential Securities Inc. group.
Yields range from 3.9% in 1996 to 5.6% in 2010 and 2011.
The bonds have been rated double-A by Moody's and double-Aplus by S&P.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $600 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Kidder, Peabody & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-72, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 6% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 6.6% and weighted average maturity of 355 months.
Beneficial California Inc. -- $757.5 million of home-equity-loan, asset-backed certificates priced in two parts by underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
The issuer is a unit of Beneficial Corp. of Wilmington, Del.
The $657.1 million of senior Class A-1 certificates was priced at par with a coupon that floats 24 basis points above the one-month London Interbank Offered Rate.
The certificates have an average life of 2.44 years at a conditional prepayment rate of 25%.
The certificates were rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.
The $100.4 million of subordinate Class B certificates was priced at par and have a coupon that floats 60 basis points above one-month Libor.
The certificates have an average life of 2.82 years at 25% conditional prepayment rate.
They were rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P.
The subordinate class is supported by a 1.5% reserve fund.
USAA Federal Savings Bank -- $310.5 million of auto-loan asset-backed certificates priced as 5s at 99.859375 to yield 5.165%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The certificates have an average life of 1.34 years and are rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.
They were sold through USAA Auto Loan Grantor Trust 1994-1, a special-purpose vehicle.
The certificates are supported by a 5% cash collateral account.
The collateral consists of auto and light-truck installment loans.

Issue split into 15% offered to retail French investors and 85% offered internationally.
Each bond convertible from April 8 to maturity into one Alcatel ordinary share at 11.27% premium over yesterday's opening share price of 719 francs.
Bonds redeemable at maturity at 1,098 francs, 37.25% above nominal value.
Yield to maturity 5.5%.
Bonds callable from Jan. 1, 1998, at price equivalent to 5.5% yield to maturity.
Fees 2.6.

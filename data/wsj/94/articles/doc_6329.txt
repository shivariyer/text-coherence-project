Cincinnati Bell Inc. reported a $106.8 million fourth-quarter loss and repeated previous forecasts that it expects lower operating earnings in the first quarter of 1994.
The Cincinnati-based telecommunications company reported a net loss equal to $1.69 a share for the fourth quarter, compared with a year-earlier loss of $1.4 million, or one cent a share.
Revenue rose 4% to $287.2 million from $276 million.
Pretax charges of $102 million for restructuring and expected sale at a loss of CBIS Federal, an information services business, had the most significant impact on fourth-quarter results.
The CBIS charges reduced results in the quarter by $88 million, or $1.39 a share.
Cincinnati Bell said in the fourth quarter its main operating unit Cincinnati Bell Telephone had operating profit of $18.3 million, up 17% from $15.6 million.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading, Cincinnati Bell shares fell 12.5 cents to close at $17.875 a share.

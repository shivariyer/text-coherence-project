The company said its chairman, E. Theodore Stolberg, will leave his post when his term expires Feb. 24.
The maker of phosphate fertilizer products said Craig Harlen, the company's chief executive officer and president, is expected to succeed Mr. Stolberg as chairman.
Mr. Harlen is 44 years old.
Peter B. Pfister was named to succeed Mr. Stolberg as a director.
Mr. Pfister, 34, is a partner in Weiss, Peck & Greer, an investment firm that owns a majority stake in Nu-West.
In an interview, Mr. Stolberg, who is 44 and has been chairman of Nu-West since the company was founded in 1987, said he is leaving to devote more time to Stolberg Partners, a New York investment firm that he started in March.

Wyman-Gordon Co., North Grafton, Mass., said it agreed to buy Cooper Industries Inc.'s forging division in a cash and stock deal with an indicated value of $87.5 million.
Under terms, Wyman-Gordon will issue 16.5 million new common shares to Cooper and pay the Houston electrical products and industrial equipment maker $5 million in cash.
Following the proposed acquisition, which is subject to shareholder and government regulatory approval, Cooper will hold about 48% of Wyman-Gordon's shares outstanding.
In Nasdaq Stock Market trading yesterday, Wyman-Gordon rose 50 cents, or 10%, to $5.375.
In New York Stock Exchange trading, Cooper fell 25 cents to $50.625.

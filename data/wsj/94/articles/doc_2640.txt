Citing new marketing and merchandising programs, Toys "R" Us Inc. reported record results for its fourth quarter and fiscal year.
The Paramus, N.J., retail toy company said earnings for the quarter, ended Jan. 29, rose 10% to $374.5 million, or $1.27 a share, from $339.7 million, or $1.14 a share, a year ago.
Sales were $3.89 billion, up 15% from $3.4 billion.
For the year, sales rose 11% to $7.95 billion from $7.17 billion.
Profit rose 10% to $483 million, or $1.63 a share, from $437.5 million, or $1.47 a share.
The company attributed the positive results in part to several new marketing and merchandising initiatives, including the wider distribution of its Holiday Toy catalog, expansion of specialty boutiques within its stores and customer service initiatives.
The company has plans to open 45 new toy stores in the U.S. and 70 stores internationally, as well as 10 new Kids "R" Us children's clothing stores.

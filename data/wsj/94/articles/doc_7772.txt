T. Rowe Price Associates Inc. said the mutual fund boom, particularly in the international arena, helped lift fourth-quarter net income 53% to $14.7 million, or 48 cents a share, from $9.6 million, or 32 cents a share, a year ago.
Revenue rose 38% to $89.9 million from $65.4 million.
The Baltimore-based company, which serves as investment adviser to mutual funds and other clients, said net income for the full year rose 36% to $48.5 million, or $1.59 a share, from $35.8 million, or $1.19 a share.
The 1993 results included a charge of one cent a share from accounting changes.
Revenue for the year grew 27% to $310 million from $245.1 million.
The results exceeded analysts' estimates.
In a statement, the company said it is "optimistic" about its future prospects, citing its strength in overseas markets.
T. Rowe Price stock closed at $29.75, up $1, in Nasdaq Stock Market trading.

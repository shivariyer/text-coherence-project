The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
California Energy Co. -- $400 million of senior discount notes due Jan. 15, 2004, priced Friday with a zero coupon at 75.523 to yield 10.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The coupon will rise to 10.25% on July 15, 1997.
The notes, which are noncallable for five years, are rated Ba-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $112 million of notes due March 28, 1996, priced initially at par to yield 5.185%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of seven basis points above the Treasury's when- issued two-year note.
The issue will be sold through underwrit ers led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $150 million of debentures due March 28, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.78%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, which are noncallable for two years, were priced at a spread of 46 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through under writers led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $200 million of medium-term notes due April 1, 2004, priced at par to yield 7.09%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, which are noncallable for three years, were priced at a spread of 57 basis points above Treasurys and will be sold through under writers led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
Digital Equipment Corp. -- $400 million of cumulative perpetual preferred stock priced at par for a 8.875% dividend yield.
The 16 million shares were priced at $25 apiece, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The preferred stock, which is noncallable for five years, is rated ba-1 by Moody's and triple-B-minus by S&P.
The dividend will be paid quarterly on Jan. 15, April 15, July 15 and Oct. 15 each year.
The first dividend, payable April 15, will be for less than a full quarter and will be paid to holders of record on March 28.
The stock isn't convertible.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Gasonics International Corp. -- initial offering of 2.2 million common shares priced at $13 apiece through underwriters led by Montgomery Securities.
JDN Realty Corp. -- initial offering of 5.9 million common shares priced at $22 apiece through underwriters led by Alex. Brown & Sons Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $150 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Bear, Stearns & Co.
The offering, Remic Trust 1994-66, is backed by various Fannie Mae Remic tranches and the agency's Strip classes.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $100 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Prudential Securities Inc.
The offering, Remic Trust 1994-64, is backed by Fannie Mae's Remic tranches and the agency's Strip classes.
Barings BV (United Kingdom parent) -- $150 million of floating-rate Euronotes due March 2001 at issue and reoffer price 99.44 via Baring Brothers & Co. and CS First Boston Inc.
Payable quarterly at three-month dollar London Interbank Offered Rate plus 0.375 point.
Call and put option exercisable quarterly from March 1999 at par.
Guarantor Barings PLC.
Fees 0.25.
Fujita Corp. (Japan) -- 40 billion yen of bonds offered in Japan through lead manager Nomura Securities Co., including 20 billion yen of 3.9% four-year bonds at issue price par and 20 billion yen of 4.5% six-year bonds at par.
The chief trustee for the four-year issue is Tokai Bank and for the six-year issue is Sakura Bank.
Payment for the bonds is due March 30.
The Japan Bond Research Institute rates the new bonds single-A-minus.
Toyota Motor Credit Corp. (Japanese parent) -- 840 million French francs of Eurobonds due April 14, 1995, at issue and reoffer price par via Banque Paribas.
Coupon 7.63%.
Interest accrues only on days when three-month Paris Interbank Offered Rate within ranges 6.35% to 5.65% for upper limit and 5.6% to 4.25% for lower limit.
Fees undisclosed.

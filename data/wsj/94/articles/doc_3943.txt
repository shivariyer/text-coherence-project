Mark M. Little was named vice president, power-generation engineering, at the GE Industrial & Power Systems unit.
Mr. Little, 41 years old, succeeds Thomas C. Paul, who becomes vice president, technology, at the unit.
Mr. Little was previously product general manager, gas-turbine-combined cycle, for the unit.
In addition, Charles Castine, 44, was named vice president, consumer service, at the GE Appliances unit.
He succeeds David Cote, who was recently named vice president and general manager of GE Silicones.
And Andreas M. Georghiou, 44, was named vice president, commercial business services, at GE American Communications Inc., a new position.
Mr. Georghiou was formerly director, broadcast services, at the unit.

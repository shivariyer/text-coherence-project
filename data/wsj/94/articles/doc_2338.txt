Standard & Poor's Corp. said it will remove Litton Industries Inc. from the S&P 500 stock index after the close of trading Thursday and replace it with its spinoff company.
Litton is spinning off to shareholders its oil-field information services and industrial automation businesses.
The new company, called Western Atlas Inc., will have an estimated market value of $1.8 billion.
Western Atlas will replace Litton in the index.
After the spinoff, Litton will consist of defense electronics and shipbuilding operations and will have an expected market value of $1.4 billion.

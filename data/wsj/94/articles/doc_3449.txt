Yields on taxable money-market funds rose in the week ended Tuesday.
IBC/Donoghue's average seven-day compound yield on taxable funds inched up to 2.83% from 2.81%, according to Money Fund Report, an Ashland, Mass., newsletter.
Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.
The seven-day simple yield on taxable funds rose to 2.79% from 2.77%, while the 30-day simple yield increased to 2.75% from 2.73%.
The 30-day compound yield edged up to 2.79% from 2.76%.
Assets of 645 taxable funds (one more than the previous week) fell by $6.10 billion, to $472.66 billion, the newsletter said.
Institutional investors took out $6.47 billion, while individual investors put in $370.8 million.
Average maturity of the taxable funds' investments, which include commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and Treasury bills, was shortened by one day to 53 days.
The average tax-exempt seven-day compound yield inched up to 1.99% from 1.98%, in the latest week.
The latest taxfree yield is equivalent to a taxable 3.11% for someone paying 36% in taxes and to 3.29% for those who pay 39.6%.
Assets of 346 tax-exempt funds (one more than the previous week) fell by $128.9 million, to $112.66 billion, according to the newsletter.
The tax-free funds' average portfolio maturity remained at 55 days.
Money Fund Report is published by IBC/Donoghue Inc., a subsidiary of IBC USA (Publications) Inc.

Manpower Inc., Milwaukee, showing strong profit gains in its U.S. temporary-help business, reported fourth-quarter net income of $4.2 million, or six cents a share, against a year-earlier net loss of $9 million, or 12 cents a share.
Both periods included sizable nonrecurring charges tied to amortizing goodwill costs.
Such charges were $8.6 million in the latest quarter and $19.8 million in the year-ago quarter.
Manpower said that its U.S. operations showed a 71% gain in operating profit in the quarter from the previous year.
Revenue rose 4.8% to $826.3 million.
For all of 1993, Manpower had a net loss of $48.9 million, or 66 cents a share, including nonrecurring charges of $71.5 million and a $20 million restructuring charge, on revenue of $3.18 billion.
That net loss was wider than the year-earlier $46.7 million, or 64 cents a share, on revenue of $3.19 billion.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, the stock closed at $18.25, up 62.5 cents.

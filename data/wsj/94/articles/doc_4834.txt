Kaiser Aluminum Corp. said it has completed a $1.1 billion refinancing and recapitalization program that increases liquidity, reduces interest rates and extends debt maturities.
The aluminum producer, 61% of which is held by Maxxam Inc., said it completed the sale of eight million shares of 8.255% preferred redeemable increased dividend equity securities for about $94 million in proceeds, the sale of $225 million in 9 7/8% senior notes and the signing of a five-year $250 million revolving credit agreement.
Previously, Kaiser had completed the sale of $400 million in 12 3/4% senior subordinated notes and a public offering of 17.2 million 65 cent depositary shares for $125 million.

U.S. Banknote Corp. said it received an $83.7 million, 20-month contract to print food stamps for the U.S. Department of Agriculture, including a one-year renewal option.
Under terms of the contract, which runs through September 1995, U.S. Banknote in New York will design, develop and test the new food stamp watermark for the federal agency.
In 1993, U.S. Banknote, which also prints securities documents, travelers checks and other high-security documents, derived $40 million, or 20%, of its annual revenue from its food stamp contract.
In New York Stock Exchange late trading, U.S. Banknote gained 25 cents, or 5.7%, to close at $4.625 a share.

Melville Corp., Rye, N.Y., reported fourth-quarter net income soared, compared with year-earlier results that included a strategic realignment charge of $222 million, or $2.13 a share.
The specialty retailer said earnings rose to $237.4 million, or $2.22 a share, from $37.2 million, or 32 cents a share, the previous year.
Without the realignment charge, 1992 fourth-quarter earnings were $259.2 million, or $2.45 a share.
Sales edged up to $3.51 billion, from $3.49 billion.
The latest quarter had one more selling day than the year-ago quarter.
Same-store sales in the period decreased 0.3%.
For the year, earnings jumped to $331.8 million, or $3 a share, from $133.4 million, or $1.13 a share.
Besides the realignment charge a year ago, Melville recorded a charge of $22.6 million or 21 cents a share, for accounting changes in retirement benefits.
Sales for the year, which had one less selling day than the previous year, were about flat at $10.44 billion.
In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, the stock closed at $39.25, up $1.125.

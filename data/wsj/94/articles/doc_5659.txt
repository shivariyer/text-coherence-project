Investors' nervousness about rising interest rates knocked stock prices lower.
Bond prices and the dollar fell.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average slid 36.58 points to 3895.34.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index dropped 3.84 to 468.93, and the Nasdaq Composite Index lost 3.11 to 783.42.
The market was weakened at midday when Elaine Garzarelli, a well-known analyst at Lehman Brothers, said a 4% to 7% correction is possible, although she remains bullish.
A partial recovery gave way to a late round of selling that left the industrials near the day's low.
Weakening bond prices in the final hour of stock trading sparked the sell-off, analysts said.
Stock-market investors moved in to pocket profits on many high-flying economically sensitive stocks.
Auto and heavy-machinery manufacturers were among the stocks hit the worst.
"Those are the cyclicals that have been the biggest winners -- not just over the past few months but the past few years," said James Solloway, director of research at Argus Research.
Interest rates rose across the board even as the Treasury's auction of $11 billion in 30-year bonds was largely successful.
But stock and bond investors remain jittery about today's release of January wholesale price data.
A larger-than-expected jump could batter both markets.
Mr. Solloway said another small rise in short-term rates is possible soon, but added he doesn't think "the Federal Reserve will push them up that far or fast in the next nine to 12 months."
World-wide, stock prices fell in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index lost 0.16 to 115.80.
Stock prices declined.
Volume totaled 325,760,000 shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,311 issues fell and 811 rose.
Bond prices fell.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue lost nearly 1/2 point, or nearly $5 for each $1,000 face amount, to yield 6.44%.
The dollar weakened.
In late New York trading, the currency was quoted at 1.7533 marks and 108.15 yen compared with 1.7585 marks and 108.35 yen Wednesday.

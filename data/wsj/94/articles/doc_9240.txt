State Street Boston Corp. said its fourth-quarter net income rose 9.3% to $47.7 million, or 62 cents a share, from $43.6 million, or 56 cents a share.
The Boston bank makes most of its money from fees for servicing mutual funds and institutional money management.
It said that higher operating expenses for salaries, computers and product development offset a 21% increase in fee revenue to $222.7 million.
For the year, State Street said net rose 12% to $179.8 million, or $2.36 a share, from $160.4 million, or $2.10 a share.
During the year, assets under custody rose 21% to $1.6 trillion.

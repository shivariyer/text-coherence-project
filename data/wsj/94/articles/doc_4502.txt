Black & Decker Corp., Towson, Md., helped by a gain in sales of consumer products, reported fourth-quarter earnings of $42.3 million, or 47 cents a share, compared with a year-earlier loss.
Revenue for the quarter climbed 3% to $1.44 billion from $1.39 billion.
In the year-earlier quarter, the company reported a loss of $122.8 million, or $1.51 a share, including a $135 million restructuring charge and a $21 million charge for the early extinguishment of debt.
For 1993 the company reported earnings of $66 million, or 65 cents a share, compared with a net loss of $333.6 million, or $4.52 a share, in 1992.
Without special items, profit for 1993 was $95 million, or $1 a share, up 55% from $61 million, or 65 cents a share, a year earlier.
The maker of tools and home products and supplier of information systems and services said it had double-digit sales gains in some products.
Going forward, it said operating-income margins will "reflect the tapering off of costs associated with introducing new products and improving sales and marketing support."

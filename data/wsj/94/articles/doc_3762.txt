Greyhound Lines Inc. posted a 6.3% decline in its January bus traffic, blaming bad weather and the earthquake in California.
The company said revenue passenger miles declined to 417,587 from 445,635.
A revenue passenger mile is one paying passenger carried one mile.
The bad weather cited by Greyhound included ice storms in the Northeast and Midwest and heavy rains in California.
The company said continued poor weather hurt its February traffic.

Yields on taxable money-market funds rose, while individual investors added to assets in the week ended Tuesday.
IBC/Donoghue's average seven-day compound yield on taxable funds inched up to 2.90% from 2.87%, according to Money Fund Report, an Ashland, Mass., newsletter.
Compound yields assume reinvestment of dividends.
The seven-day simple yield on taxable funds rose to 2.86% from 2.83%, while the 30-day simple yield crept to 2.82% from 2.80%.
The 30-day compound yield rose to 2.86% from 2.84%.
Assets of 646 taxable funds (one more than the previous week) rose by $2.06 billion to $480.85 billion, the newsletter said.
Individual investors accounted for $1.31 billion of the inflow, while institutional investors put in the remaining $749.2 million.
Average maturity of the taxable funds' investments, which include commercial paper (short-term corporate IOUs) and Treasury bills, shortened by one day to 49 days.
The average tax-exempt seven-day compound yield slid to 1.82% from 1.89% in the latest week.
The latest tax-free yield is equivalent to a taxable 2.84% for someone paying 36% in taxes and to 3.01% for those who pay 39.6%.
Assets of 348 tax-exempt funds (two more than last week) rose by $25.1 million, to $116.50 billion, according to the newsletter.
The tax-free funds' average portfolio maturity shortened by one day to 53 days.
Money Fund Report is published by IBC/Donoghue Inc., a subsidiary of IBC USA (Publications) Inc.

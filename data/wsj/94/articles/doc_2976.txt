The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $150 million of notes due Sept. 15, 1995, priced at par to yield 4.7%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of five basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Hoechst Celanese Corp. -- $100 million of medium-term notes due March 15, 2009, priced as 7.125s at 99.835 to yield 7.143%.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 83 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through undewriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Hoechst Celanese is a unit of Hoechst AG.
Bethlehem Steel Corp. -- 15 million common shares priced at $21.375 each through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
New Jersey Housing & Mortgage Finance Agency -- $105.6 million of 1994 Series I and Series J home-buyer revenue bonds priced by a Smith Barney Shearson Inc. group.
The $41.65 million Series I bonds are priced to yield from 3.55% in 1996 to 5.6% in 2017.
The $63.95 million Series J bonds, subject to the federal alternative-minimum tax, are priced to yield 5.2% in 2018 and 6.2% in 2025.
Both series of bonds are insured by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from Moody's and S&P.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $300 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Chemical Securities Inc.
The offering, Series 1994-60, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.65% and weighted average maturity of 357 months.
Cie. Financiere des Nouvelles Galleries (France) -- one billion French francs of 6.875% Eurobonds due April 6, 2004, at issue price 100.555 via Credit Commercial de France.
Reoffered at 98.98 to yield 7.02%, a margin of 78 basis points over comparable French Treasury.
Fees two.
Furukawa Electric Co. (Japan) -- 10 billion yen of floatingrate Euronotes due March 24, 1998, at issue price 100.15 via Daiwa Europe Ltd.
Reoffered at 100.05.
Payable quarterly at 0.25 point above three-month London Interbank Offered Rate for yen.
Fees 0.25.
Furukawa Electric Co. (Japan) -- 10 billion yen of floatingrate Euronotes due June 24, 1998, at issue and reoffer price 100.15 via DKB International PLC.
Payable quarterly at 0.25 point above three-month yen Libor.
Fees 0.25.
PSA Peugeot Citroen SA (France) -- 3.96 billion French francs of 2% convertible bonds due Jan. 1, 2001, at nominal price 990 francs via Societe Generale.
Bonds convertible from March 18 to maturity at 10% premium over Monday opening share price of 900 francs.
Bonds redeemable at 1,224 francs to yield 5% at maturity.
Bonds callable from Jan. 1, 2000, to Dec. 1, 2000, at 1,224 francs if share price exceeds redemption price.
Fees 2.55.
Skopbank (Finland) -- $75 million of floating-rate Euronotes due April 1999 at issue and reoffer price par via CS First Boston Inc.
Payable semiannually flat at six-month dollar Libor.
Fees 0.25.

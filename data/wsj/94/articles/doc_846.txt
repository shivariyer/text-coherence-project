The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $130 million of notes due April 5, 2004, priced initially at par to yield 6.75%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 21 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of multiple step-up notes due April 7, 1997, priced at par to yield 5% for the first year, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
Thereafter, the coupon rises annually to 5.65%, then to 6.7%.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 32 basis points above Treasurys and will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Hertz Corp. -- $150 million of senior notes due April 15, 2001, priced as 7s at 99.777 to yield 7.04%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 70 basis points above seven-year Treasurys.
Rated Baa-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Hertz is a unit of Ford Motor Co.
International Lease Finance Corp. -- $100 million of notes due April 1, 1999, priced as 6.625s at 99.809 to yield 6.67%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 60 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
International Lease Finance is a unit of American International Group Inc.
Pitney Bowes Credit Corp. -- $200 million of notes due Feb. 15, 1997, priced as 5.625s at 99.663 to yield 5.751%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 30 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Pitney Bowes Credit is a unit of Pitney Bowes Inc.
Crystallume -- one million common shares priced Wednesday at $5.40 each and one million redeemable warrants priced at 10 cents each, managed by Dickinson & Co., according to the company.
Gulf South Medical Supply Inc. -- initial offering of 1.9 million common shares priced at $16 each through underwriters William Blair & Co. and Montgomery Securities.
Alabama Higher Education Loan Corp. -- $97.8 million of student-loan revenue refunding bonds priced by a Lehman Brothers Inc. group.
The $8.77 million of Series 1994-A bonds yield from 3.1% in 1994 to 5.25% in 2000.
These bonds aren't subject to the alternative-minimum tax.
The $19.6 million of Series B-1994 bonds, which are subject to the alternative-minimum tax, yield from 3.3% in 1994 to 5.65% in 2002.
The $34.4 million of Series 1994-C bonds, also subject to the alternative-minimum tax, yield from 3.3% in 1994 to 5.45% and 5.85% in 2004, 6% in 2007 and 6.1% in 2009.
Call features for this series are at 2004 and 2006.
The $35 million of Series 1994-D bonds, subject to the alternative-minimum tax as well, yield from 3.3% in 1994 to 5.45% in 2000, with term bonds at 5.85% in 2004, 6% in 2007 and 6.15% in 2010.
Its call features are also at 2004 and 2006.
All bonds are rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.
Michigan Trunk Line Fund -- $92 million of refunding bonds, Series 1994-B, priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group.
Yields range from 2.75% in 1994 to 5.1% in 2002.
Bonds due in 2001 and 1998, carry a rating of single-A from Moody's and double-A-minus from S&P.
The remainder of the bonds are insured by the Financial Guaranty Insurance Co. and are rated triple-A by Moody's and S&P.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $325 million of stripped, mortgage-backed securities priced through undewriter Kidder, Peabody & Co.
Strips Trust 261 is backed by the agency's 30-year, 8.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 9.12% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 303 months.
European Investment Bank (supranational) -- one billion marks of 6.5% Eurobonds due April 21, 2004, at issue price 101.65 via Deutsche Bank AG.
Bonds yield 6.51% after fees of 1.75, a margin of 15 basis points over German Treuhand of March 1999.
General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S. parent) -- 100 million Eurpean Currency Units of 6.125% Eurobonds due April 13, 1999, at issue price 101.215 via Credit Commercial de France and Merrill Lynch International Ltd.

Fees 1.875.

Bonds yield 8.65% after full fees of 1.875.

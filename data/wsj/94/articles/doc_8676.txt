Symantec Corp. reported a narrowed loss of $9.2 million, or 31 cents a share, for its fiscal third quarter, including one-time charges of $21.5 million, or 49 cents a share, for acquisitions and litigation.
The Cupertino, Calif., software concern had a loss of $11.5 million, or 40 cents a share, in the year-ago period.
Revenue for the period ended Dec. 31 was $67 million, up 9% from $61.6 million a year ago.
Separately, the company announced a $19 million tentative settlement of three shareholder lawsuits filed against it in San Jose federal court in December 1992.
The suits alleged that the company provided false and misleading financial data in violation of federal-securities laws between June 1991 and October 1992.
Symantec has denied any wrongdoing.
The settlement, all but $6.5 million of which will be paid by the company's insurers, is included in the quarterly results.
Also included is a charge of $15 million for the purchase of software maker Fifth Generation Systems Inc.

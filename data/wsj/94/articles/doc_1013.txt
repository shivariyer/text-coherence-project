Standard Federal Bank said it agreed to acquire Mackinac Financial Corp. for a minimum cash purchase price of $21.3 million.
In a news release, Standard said the final purchase price will be increased if the aftertax value of Mackinac's mortgage-banking operation exceeds $3.7 million as of a date to be agreed upon by the parties.
The acquisition is subject to approval by the shareholders of Mackinac and the Office of Thrift Supervision.
The closing will be scheduled within 60 days after receipt of OTS approval.
Mackinac, a Troy, Mich.-based savings and loan holding company, had assets of $221.1 million and stockholders' equity of $11.3 million at Dec. 31, 1993.

The following were among Friday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Apple Computer Inc. -- $300 million of notes priced at 6.5s at 99.925 to yield 6.51% in 2004.
The offering was managed by Goldman, Sachs & Co., CitiCorp Securities Inc., CS First Boston Inc. and Morgan Stanley & Co.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $100 million of debentures due Feb. 22, 1999, priced at par to yield 5.57%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The debentures, callable after one year, were priced at a spread of 21 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Freeport-McMoRan Resource Partners L.P. -- $150 million of 8.75% senior subordinated notes due Feb. 15, 2004, according to the partnership.
Interest will be paid semiannually Feb. 15 and Aug. 15, starting Aug. 15.
Goldman, Sachs & Co. is the underwriter.
Western National Corp. -- $150 million of senior notes due Feb. 15, 2004, priced as 7.125s at 99.589 to yield 7.183%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 130 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Ba-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-B by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
DSP Group Inc. -- initial offering of two million common shares priced at $14 each through underwriters led by Oppenheimer & Co. and Cowen & Co.
Kaiser Aluminum Corp. -- eight million convertible, preferred shares priced at $11.75 each, according to the company.
Each share bears a cumulative annual dividend of $0.97.
The shares are subject to mandatory conversion into Kaiser common stock no later than Dec. 31, 1997.
Underwriters are Merrill Lynch & Co.; Bear, Stearns & Co.; Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.; PaineWebber Inc.; and Salomon Brothers Inc.
Softdesk Inc. -- initial public offering of two million common shares priced at $11.50 each, according to the company.
Hambrecht & Quist Inc. and Wessels, Arnold & Henderson are the managing underwriters.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500.5 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-41, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.5% and weighted average maturity of 357 months.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic securities offered by CS First Boston Inc.
The offering, Series 1994-39, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 6.5% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7% and weighted average maturity of 357 months.
Deutsche Bau und Boden Bank AG (Germany) -- 200 million marks of floating-rate Euronotes due Mar. 18, 1999, at issue price 100.08 via Deutsche Genossenschaftsbank.
Payable semiannually at 0.0625 point over six-month Frankfurt Interbank Offered Rate.
Fees 0.2, less 0.02 for lead manager.
Dresdner Bank AG (Germany) -- $100 million of subordinated collared floating-rate Euronotes due March 2004 at issue and reoffer price 99.9 for own account with Lehman Brothers International.
Payable semiannually at six-month U.S. London Interbank Offered Rate less 0.25 point.
Minimum coupon 5.125%, maximum 8.125%.
Fees 0.5.
Hamburg, Germany (municipality) -- 50 million marks of bonds due Nov. 29, 2018, at issue price 99.6 via Merrill Lynch Bank AG.
Bonds will trade interchangeably with 50 million marks of identical bonds outstanding.
Terms unchanged.
Coupon 6.08% first 10 years, thereafter 6.85%.
Put option exercisable annually at par after year 10.
Fees undisclosed.
Lloyds Bank PLC (U.K.) -- #400 million of 7.375% surbordinated Eurobonds due Mar. 11, 2004, at issue price #101.213 via Salomon Brothers International Ltd.
Reoffered at 99.663 to yield 7.424%, a margin of 72 basis points over comparable U.K. gilt.
Fees two.
Petroleos Mexicanos SA (Mexico) -- $300 million of floating-rate Euronotes due March 8, 1999, at issue and reoffer price 99.34 via J.P. Morgan Securities Ltd.
Payable quarterly at three-month dollar Libor plus one point to yield Libor plus 1.15 point.
Fees 0.5.

Polaroid Corp. formally launched its ProCam instant camera in the U.S. at a retail price of $199 to $250.
ProCam, Polaroid's first camera designed for business users, has automatic focus and extended flash capabilities, as well as rubber handles and a heavy strap to withstand high-volume use, Polaroid said.
The camera concern said ProCam had "strong" initial sales in Europe and Japan, where it was introduced in 1992 and 1993, respectively.
The company declined to disclose sales levels.end.

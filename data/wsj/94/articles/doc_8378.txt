Great Eagle Holdings Ltd., a Hong Kong property developer, agreed to buy a 500-room hotel for 2.03 billion Hong Kong dollars ($262.8 million) from an unidentified seller.
Great Eagle will operate the Hong Kong Renaissance Hotel upon completion of the purchase, expected Aug. 1.
The Kowloon hotel opened in 1988 and had pretax income of HK$65 million in the eight months ended in November, Great Eagle said.
According to data supplied by the seller, Great Eagle said, the hotel generated net income of HK$72 million and HK$78 million in the years ended March 31, 1992, and 1993, respectively.
Great Eagle noted that it paid HK$350 million upon signing the purchase agreement and will pay a further HK$250 million April 7, with the balance due in August.

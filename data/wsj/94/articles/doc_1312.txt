Spain's merchandise trade deficit on a customs basis shrank 47% to 89.69 billion pesetas ($644.6 million) in January from 168.91 billion pesetas in January 1993, the Finance Ministry said.
The January trade deficit was 60% lower than the December shortfall of 221.80 billion pesetas.
Imports in January climbed 24% to 776.38 billion pesetas from 624.32 billion pesetas in January 1993, while exports surged 51% to 686.69 billion pesetas from 455.41 billion pesetas in the same period.

South Korea's trade balance registered a deficit of $14.7 billion in January, ending the past four straight months of surpluses, as a result of a hefty rise in imports, the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy said.
In a preliminary report, the ministry said that the nation's exports rose 14% to $6.09 billion in January from $5.33 billion a year earlier.
Imports gained a hefty 20% to $7.56 billion from $6.30 billion the year earlier.

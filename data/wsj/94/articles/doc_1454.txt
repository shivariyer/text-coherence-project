The American Stock Exchange announced three disciplinary actions for violations of exchange rules.
Wayne Tyrrell, Sarasota, Fla., a former employee of Dean Witter, Discover & Co.'s Dean Witter Reynolds Inc., was barred for three years, with two years deemed served.
The exchange said he recommended unsuitable option transactions in a customer account, made unsuitable short-term Treasury bond trades and churned a customer's account.
Mr. Tyrrell settled the charges without admitting or denying the allegations.
Peter Dwan, Syracuse, N.Y., a former branch-office manager of Prudential Securities Inc., a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America, was barred for two years from acting in any supervisory capacity, with six months deemed served.
The exchange said he failed to supervise diligently the activities of two brokers.
He didn't admit or deny allegations.
Matthew Charlton, New London, N.H., was disciplined for permitting option trades to be entered from off the floor of the exchange into his registered options-trader account, enabling the trades to receive favorable margin treatment.
He was fined $3,000.
Mr. Charlton didn't admit or deny allegations.

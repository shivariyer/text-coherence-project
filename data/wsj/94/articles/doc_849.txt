Norwegian energy, chemicals and metals group Norsk Hydro AS said it signed a letter of intent with Cargill Fertilizer Inc. of the U.S. on the sale of Cargill's assets at its Port Sutton terminal in Tampa, Fla.
The sale includes two ammonia storage tanks with a total capacity of 49,000 tons and Cargill's 50% stake in Ammtrans, a joint venture with CF Industries Inc., that leases an ammonia pipeline from Tampa Bay Pipeline Co.
Financial details weren't disclosed by the companies.

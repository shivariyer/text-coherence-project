Sprint Corp. said it agreed with R.P. Telekom SA to develop a fiber-optic network for local telephone and cable television services in Poland's Pila and Katowice districts.
Sprint, a telecommunications company based in Kansas City, Mo., said it will invest about $15 million to own a 25% equity interest in the new company.
R.P. Telekom, a joint venture of Polish and Western investors that develops telecommunications projects in Poland, will also invest in the company and contribute its majority-ownership interest in two local operating companies, Telekom Pila and Telekom Silesia.
The remaining ownership will be held by investors and local interests.
Sprint said total investment is about $120 million.

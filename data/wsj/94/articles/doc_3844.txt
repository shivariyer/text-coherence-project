The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
CIT Group Holdings Inc. -- $100 million of subordinated, medium-term notes due March 1, 2004, priced at par to yield 6.98%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for three years, were priced at a spread of 81 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Chase Securities Inc.
CIT is a unit of Chemical Banking Corp. and Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank Ltd.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $250 million of floating-rate notes due March 8, 1996, priced initially at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue will pay and float quarterly at two basis points above three-month Treasury bills.
The coupon will be set March 8.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $150 million of step-up notes due March 14, 1997, priced at par to yield 4.4%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 35 basis points above Treasurys.
The coupon will rise to 5.75% in March 1995.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $150 million of notes due March 7, 2001, priced at par to yield 6.35%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 40 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $150 million of medium-term notes due March 10, 2004, priced initially at par to yield 6.72%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, noncallable for three years, were priced at a spread of 56 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
InfoSoft International Inc. -- initial offering of three million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc. and Adams Harkness.
Millicom International Cellular SA -- 4.2 million common shares priced at $24.25 each through underwriters led by S.G. Warburg and Morgan Stanley & Co.
Network Long Distance Inc. -- initial offering of 1.3 million common shares priced at $5 each through underwriters led by Barron Chase Securities.
Norton McNaughton Inc. -- initial offering of 4.7 million common shares priced at $14 each through underwriters led by Mabon Securities Corp. and Wheat First Butcher & Singer Capital Markets.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Morgan Stanley & Co.
The offering, Series 1994-55, is backed by the agency's 30-year, 7% mortgage securities.
The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.6% and weighted average maturity of 358 months.

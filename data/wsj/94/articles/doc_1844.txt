The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Citicorp -- $200 million of subordinated notes due March 15, 2004, priced as 7.125s at 99.195 to yield 7.24%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 83 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated Baa2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc.
Federal Express 1994 Pass-Through Trusts -- $377 million of pass-through certificates sold in three parts through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first part, $125 million of pass-through certificates due Sept. 23, 2006, was priced at par to yield 7.53%.
The debt has a seven-year average life.
The issue was priced at a spread of 135 basis points above the 7.75% Treasury maturing in 2001.
The second tranche, $175 million of passthrough certificates maturing Sept. 23, 2008, was priced at par to yield 7.89%.
The debt has a 10-year average life.
The issue was priced at a spread of 149 basis points above 10-year Treasurys.
The third part, $77 million of pass-through certificates maturing March 23, 2010, was priced at par to yield 8.4%.
The debt has a 15-year average life.
The issue was priced at a spread of 200 basis points above 10-year Treasurys.
Rated Baa2 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, all three tranches are noncallable.
The issuer is an independent facility from which Federal Express Corp. leases airplanes.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $200 million of notes due March 23, 1999, priced initially at par to yield 6.19%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The notes, which are noncallable for one year, were priced at a spread of 31 basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.
Ford Motor Credit Co. -- $500 million of notes due March 3, 1997, priced as 5.625s at 99.675 to yield 5.748%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 42 basis points above Treasurys.
Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Ford Motor Credit is a unit of Ford Motor Co.
Pennsylvania Power & Light Co. -- $250 million of debt priced in two parts through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first tranche, $150 million of first mortgage bonds, due March 1, 2004, was priced as 6.875s at 99.391 to yield 6.96%.
The notes were priced at a spread of 55 basis points above Treasurys.
Gross spread is $6.50, selling concession is $4 and reallowance is $2.50.
The second tranche, $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due March 1, 2014, was priced as 7.375s at 99.22 to yield 7.45%.
They were priced at a spread of 50 basis points above Treasurys.
Gross spread is $8.75, selling concession is $5 and reallowance is $2.50.
Both parts are noncallable and rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P.

New York City Municipal Water Finance Authority -- $250 million bond anticipation notes largely won by a J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. group.
The 1994 Series A water and sewer-system notes are due Dec. 15.
J.P. Morgan won $100 million of the notes with a bid setting the net interest cost at 2.848% and $50 million with a net-interest-cost bid of 2.886%.
The notes are 3.75s priced to yield 2.8% at their maturity.
Smith Barney Shearson won $50 million with net-interest-cost bids of 2.869% and 2.82%.
Those notes are also 3.75s priced to yield 2.8%.
The remaining $50 million were apparently won by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Moody's has rated the notes MIG-1, and S&P has assigned an SP-1 rating.
Pennsylvania -- $491.1 million First Series of 1994 improvement unlimited-tax bonds apparently won by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 5.3623%.
Yields for reoffered bonds range from 3.05% in 1995 to 5.75% in 2013 and 2014.
Bonds due in 2007-09 are insured by Ambac Indemnity Corp. and carry triple-A ratings from both Moody's and S&P.
The balance of the bonds have a single-A-1 rating from Moody's and a double-A-minus rating from S&P.

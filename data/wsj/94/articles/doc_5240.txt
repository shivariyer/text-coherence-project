Former Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld and three business executives were named directors, expanding the size of the board to nine from five.
Mr. Rumsfeld, 61 years old, served as White House chief of staff in the Nixon administration and as defense secretary under President Ford.
The other new members are Robert S. Cline, 56, chairman and chief executive of the Airborne Express unit of Airborne Freight Corp., Seattle; Mike Slade, 36, president and chief executive of closely held Starwave Corp., a Bellevue, Wash., supplier of multimedia software; and Robert M. Smelick, 51, managing principal and director of closely held Sterling Payot Co., an investment banking firm based in San Francisco.
Metricom is a supplier of wireless data communication networks.

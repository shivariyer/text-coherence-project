Sturdy bonds and merger news punctuated yesterday's trading activity, enabling small-company stocks to extend a two-session rally.
Market watchers said stable bonds were the main driving force behind yesterday's buying, quelling, at least for the meantime, last week's fears that the Federal Reserve could move to tighten credit.
The Russell 2000 Index of small-capitalization stocks jumped 1.98, or 0.75%, to 266.69.
The Nasdaq Composite Index rose 4.50, or 0.57%, to 795.05.
The Russell 2000 lies just 0.93, or 0.35%, from its record, set Feb. 2.
The Nasdaq Composite is 5.42, or 0.68%, shy of its closing high set Jan. 31.
Advancing Nasdaq issues topped decliners, 1,650 to 1,427, on total volume of 303,236,000 shares down from volume of 318,175,000 Friday.
Yesterday, merger news attracted buyers to all segments of the market and might help contribute to another bout of "takeover fever" among smaller stocks, said Robert Antolini, of Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette.
Grumman soared 14 1/4 to 54 1/8 after agreeing to be acquired by Martin Marietta, which rose 1 1/4 to 46.
Both are traded on the New York Stock Exchange.
In other merger news, ValueVision International Inc., Minneapolis, rose 1 3/8 to 9 1/8, and National Media, Philadelphia, added 7/8 to 11 1/8 on the NYSE.
The two companies signed a merger agreement under which ValueVision increased its tender offer by $1 a share to $11.50 for National Media's stock.
The transaction is valued at more than $150 million.
An acquisition proposal from Westcott Communications drove Sandy's stock up 1 1/2 to 7 3/8 on the American Stock Exchange.
Westcott, based in Carrollton, Texas, which fell 5/8 to 23 3/8, has made an unsolicited proposal to merge the smaller Troy, Mich., communications company into a Westcott subsidiary.
OrNda Healthcorp, Nashville, Tenn., gained 1 to 18 3/4.
The company said it will proceed with its merger with American Healthcare Management and Summit Health.
As reported, OrNda's purchase of the two will create a company with $1.6 billion in revenue and 46 acute-care hospitals in 17 states.
American Healthcare Management, King of Prussia, Pa., added 1/4 to 10 5/8 on the Big Board, and Summit Health, Burbank, Calif., gained 5/16 to 9 1/8.
An initial "buy" recommendation from Rauscher Pierce Refsnes lifted Brite Voice Systems's stock 1 1/4 to 14 3/4.
The Wichita, Kan., company makes voice-activated processing systems and provides information in audio form.
The day's big decliner was IIS Intelligent Information Systems, an Israeli communication and networking company, which tumbled 7 7/8, or 45%, to 9 5/8.
A 95% decrease in fourth-quarter net income and forecasts that disappointing results might plague the next two quarters were cited.
Positive comments from the publisher of Overpriced Stock Service, Michael Murphy, in this week's Barron's gave several issues a boost.
NetFrame Systems, a Milpitas, Calif., computer systems developer gained 1 to 16 1/4; Parallan Computer, Mountain View, Calif., 7/8 to 11; Auxpex Systems, Santa Clara, Calif., 7/8 to 7 5/8; and Vical, a San Diego pharmaceuticals company, 1 1/2 to 13 1/2.

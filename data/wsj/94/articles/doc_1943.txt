German retail sales rose a real, or inflation-adjusted, 1% and a nominal 3% in January, from a year earlier, the Federal Statistics Office said in a preliminary report.
In western Germany alone, retail sales rose a real 1% and a nominal 2% from a year earlier.
German retail sales for the month rose a real 3% from December, and a nominal 2% on a seasonally adjusted basis.
The statistics office declined to release index levels, and said the percentages are rounded.

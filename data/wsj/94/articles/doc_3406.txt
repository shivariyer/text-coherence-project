The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $250 million of floating-rate notes due March 14, 1996, priced at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable notes will float quarterly and pay quarterly at nine basis points above three-month Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $169 million of notes due March 11, 1996, priced at par to yield 4.89%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of five basis points above Treasurys.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $300 million of twoyear debentures priced through Freddie Mac at par to yield 4.87%.
BioCryst Pharmaceuticals Inc. -- initial offering of 2.2 million common shares priced at $6.50 each through underwriters led by Dillon, Read & Co. and Needham & Co.
Global Privatization Fund Inc. -- initial offering of 70 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by Kidder Peabody & Co.
Jardine Fleming India Fund Inc. -- initial offering of 10.7 million common shares priced at $15 each through underwriters led by PaineWebber Inc. and Robert Fleming Inc.
Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corp. -- $800 million of realestate mortgage-investment conduit securities offered by Merrill Lynch & Co.
The offering, Series 1702, is backed by the agency's seven-year, 6.5% balloon mortgage securities and 30-year, 7% securities.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $217.7 million of Remic securities offered by Daiwa Securities America Inc.
The offering, Multifamily Remic 1994-M2, is backed by the agency's Multifamily mortgage-backed securities.
Advanta Mortgage Corp. -- $174.5 million of home-equity loan asset-backed certificates priced in two parts by underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The issue, Advanta Mortgage Loan Trust 1994-1, was rated triple-A by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and Standard & Poor's Corp., based on an insurance wrap provided by the Municipal Bond Investors Assurance Corp.
The $144 million of Class A-1 certificates was priced as 6.3s at 100.03125 to yield 6.24%, an 85-basis-point spread above the interpolated U.S. Treasury.
The certificates have an average life of 3.6 years.
The $30.5 million of Class A-2 certificates was priced as 6.3s at 100.34375 to yield 6.19%, an 89-basis-point spread above the interpolated Treasury.
The certificates have an average life of 3.3 years.
Advanta Morgtage is a unit of Advanta Corp.
General Electric Capital Corp. (U.S. parent) -- 250 million Dutch guilders of 5.5% Eurobonds due March 25, 1997, at issue price 100.69 via Abn Amro Bank NV.
Reoffered at 99.84 to yield 5.56%, a margin of 15 basis points over interpolated Dutch state loan yield curve between February 1997 and July 1998.
Fees one.
Credito Fondiario E Industriale SpA (Italy) -- 120 billion Italian lire of floating-rate Euronotes due March 10, 2004, at issue price par via Monte dei Paschi di Siena and Chase Investment Bank Ltd.
Payable semiannually at six-month London Interbank Offered Rate for lire plus 100 basis points.
Callable annually from May 31, 1997, at par.
Fees undisclosed.

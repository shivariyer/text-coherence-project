The Dow Jones Industrial Average plowed to yet another high despite a flurry of downbeat profit reports that kept stocks lower most of the day.
Bond prices fell and the dollar was mixed.
Hitting its seventh record of the year, the industrial average climbed 14.08 points to 3884.37.
Standard & Poor's 500-stock index edged up 0.05 to 474.30 but the Nasdaq Composite Index, hurt by heavy selling in the technology arena, slumped 3.74 to 789.28.
"You had a couple of bad earnings reports in the drug and technology sectors and that sparked some concerns about profits for the quarter," said William Dodge, chairman of the investment policy committee at Dean Witter Reynolds.
Stocks started the day lower as investors, unimpressed by financial results from Intel and Digital Equipment, began hammering away at the stocks.
Digital fell 3 to 32 and Intel sank 4 to 62 despite reporting a 38% jump in fourth-quarter profits late Tuesday.
Adding to stock market woes was news that Pfizer, considered by many to be the strongest drug company, had a discouraging fourth-quarter.
Pfizer tumbled 5 to 62 7/8.
Other drug stocks were pummeled.
Schering-Plough lost 1 7/8 to 64 3/8, Eli Lilly 1 5/8 to 59 7/8 and Merck 3/4 to 35 7/8.
But despite the barrage of bad news, stock investors hardly flinched.
"I think people were encouraged by the fact that the market didn't crack," says Laszlo Birinyi, who heads a financial services company that bears his name.
"Even though you had a bond market, which was a liability and a number of key names which traded poorly, the market didn't break."
Meanwhile, in Europe, signs of renewed economic growth and expectations of lower interest rates propelled stock markets in London, Zurich, Amsterdam, Madrid, Stockholm, Oslo and Helsinki to record highs.
British, French and German stock markets each rose 1% to 1.2%.
Wild gyrations continued to play havoc with stock markets in Asia.
Manila soared 5.2%, Hong Kong climbed 3.1% and Tokyo rose 2.8%; but stocks in Kuala Lumpur fell 2.6% and those in Bangkok were off 1.2%.
World-wide, stock prices rose in dollar terms.
The Dow Jones World Stock Index gained 0.77 to 114.21.
Stock prices were mixed.
Volume totaled 310.8 million shares on the New York Stock Exchange, where 1,144 issues rose and 983 fell.
Bond prices fell.
The Treasury's benchmark 30-year issue lost 3/8 point, or $3.75 for each $1,000 face amount, to yield 6.29%.
The dollar was mixed.
In late New York trading the currency was at 1.7460 marks and 111.35 yen, compared with 1.7465 marks and 110.75 yen late Tuesday.

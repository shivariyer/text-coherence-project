Leucadia National Corp., New York, said it and Banco Mercantil, a private Argentine banking concern led by the Wertheim family, successfully bid to acquire a 60% interest in Argentina's Caja de Ahorro y Seguro SA, a holding company being privatized by the Argentine government.
The holding company has assets in excess of $500 million and is Argentina's largest insurer and also has banking operations with 44 retail branches.
Leucadia is an insurance and financial holding company.
The group offered to pay approximately $85 million for the 60% interest.
The bid, subject to approval by the Argentine government, is expected to close during the first half of 1994.

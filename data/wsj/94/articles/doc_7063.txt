Northrop Corp. reported a fourth-quarter loss because of a larger-than-expected loss provision of $107 million, or $2.18 a share, for the classified Tri-Service Standoff Attack Missile.
The Los Angeles company recorded a fourth-quarter loss of $35 million, or 73 cents a share, compared with earnings of $55 million, or $1.17 a share, in the fourth quarter of 1992.
The pretax loss provision was $164 million.
Northrop's sales for the 1993 quarter fell 17% to $1.26 billion from $1.51 billion a year earlier.
The company attributed the latest loss provision for the fixed-price missile program to rising costs of development.
In all, the company has made loss provisions of $603 million for the program.
Northrop stock rose $1, or 2.6%, to close at $39.875 a share in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.

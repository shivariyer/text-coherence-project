Centerior Energy Corp., Cleveland, said its operating utility subsidiaries Cleveland Electric Illuminating Co. and Toledo Edison Co. will file to merge into a single operating entity.
Under the terms of the agreement, shareholders of Toledo Edison will exchange their shares for preferred shares of the successor company while Cleveland Electric preferred shares will automatically become shares of the successor.
Debt holders of the two utilities will become debt holders of the successor corporation, which hasn't yet been named, the company said.
The merger is expected to be effective late this year, the company said.

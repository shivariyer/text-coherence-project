Weldotron Corp., Piscataway, N.J., said its chairman and chief executive officer, Martin Siegel, sold 250,000 shares of his Weldotron common stock to Lyford Corp., a subsidiary of investment company Mentmore Holdings Corp., Washington, at $4 a share.
The sale gives Lyford, which previously purchased 200,000 shares directly from Weldotron, a 19.56% share of the company's shares outstanding.
Mr. Siegel, founder of the packaging and electronic-systems manufacturer, will remain in his position and will hold a 7.35% stock position, the company said.
Weldotron stock closed at $2.1875, up 31.25 cents, in composite American Stock Exchange trading Friday.

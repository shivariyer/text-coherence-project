The Federal National Mortgage Association, known as Fannie Mae, said fourth-quarter net income climbed 16% to $493.6 million, or $1.80 a share, on strong growth in its mortgage portfolio.
That compares with net income of $426.4 million, or $1.55 a share, a year earlier.
The government-sponsored concern, which is based in Washington, said its net interest income on the loans it keeps for investment grew 17% to $635.3 million from $544.2 million a year earlier.
The latest quarter included a $38.8 million charge for the repurchase of debt.
For the year, net income rose 15% to $1.87 billion, or $6.82 a share, from $1.62 billion, or $5.91 a share.

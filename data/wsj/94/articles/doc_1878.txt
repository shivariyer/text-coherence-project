Pharmhouse Corp., a health and beauty care discount retailer, said it signed a letter of intent to acquire the All For A Dollar Inc. close-out retail chain in a stock transaction valued at about $12.7 million.
According to the agreement announced after the market's close, each share of All For A Dollar will be exchanged for 0.4173 share of Pharmhouse.
The Springfield, Mass., company has about 6,951,090 shares outstanding.
In Nasdaq trading, All For A Dollar closed at $2.125 a share, up 12.5 cents, and Pharmhouse closed at $4.375, up 12.5 cents.
Pharmhouse, which operates 14 stores in the Mid-Atlantic area, reported a net loss of $2.1 million, or 98 cents a share, on revenue of $72 million for the nine months ended Oct. 30.
All For A Dollar operates 168 retail close-out stores where items are sold for $1 each in the Northeast.
It reported a loss of $1 million, or 15 cents a share, on sales of $44.8 million for the nine months ended Oct. 2.
The proposed transaction, expected to be completed by summer of 1994, is conditioned upon a number of factors including the continued due diligence of both companies, the execution of mutually acceptable agreements, and approval by both companies' boards and shareholders.

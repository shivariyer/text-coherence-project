LARIZZA INDUSTRIES Inc., Troy, Mich., said its lenders converted about $56.3 million of senior debt into 8,283,040 of the company's common shares.
Larizza, a maker of automotive components, said it won't receive any proceeds from the sale of the lenders' shares.
The company said it will grant to the underwriters an over-allotment option to purchase an additional 1.2 million shares.
The offering is being managed by PaineWebber Inc., McDonald & Co.
Securities Inc. and Roney & Co.

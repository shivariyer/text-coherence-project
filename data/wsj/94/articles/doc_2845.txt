The Texas Economic Index, a weekly leading indicator, rose to 111.8 in the latest week from 111.7 the prior week.
On a year-over-year basis, the index grew 2.80%, down from the 2.88% growth rate a week earlier.
Several components of the index showed little change between this period and last.
However, consumer confidence continued to grow at a healthy pace.
Leading Index, which is national, and the Consumer Confidence Index, which tracks Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana and Arkansas U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, American Petroleum Institute, The Conference Board, CIBCR at Columbia University

TSX Corp., a El Paso, Texas, maker of electronic equipment for the cable-TV industry, said it sold a 49% stake to cable giant Tele-Communications Inc. for $12.4 million.
TSX said TCI, based in Englewood, Colo., also gave it a $4.6 million unsecured short-term loan.
The Texas company said it used $16.5 million of the money from TCI to pay off its long-term noteholders.
TSX said it agreed to put two TCI representatives on its board.
The acquisition by TCI of 2.1 million newly issued TSX shares, announced after the stock market closed, works out to $5.88 a share.
In American Stock Exchange trading, TSX closed at $6, up 12.5 cents.

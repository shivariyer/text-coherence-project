The following were among yesterday's offerings and pricings in U.S. and non-U.S. capital markets, with terms and syndicate manager, as compiled by Dow Jones Capital Markets Report:
Aluminum Co. of America -- $250 million of notes due Feb. 1, 2001, priced as 5.75s at 99.601 to yield 5.82%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.

Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by CS First Boston Inc.
Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of floating-rate notes due Feb. 10, 1997, priced at par, according to MCM CorporateWatch.

The first coupon will be set Monday .
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Lehman Brothers Inc.
Federal National Mortgage Association -- $100 million of step-up, medium-term notes due Feb. 16, 1999, priced at par to yield 4.8%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.

The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Dean Witter Reynolds Inc.
GTE California Inc. -- $300 million of debentures priced through a competitive bid won by Morgan Stanley & Co.
The debentures, due Feb. 1, 2001, were priced as 5.625s at 99.1184 to yield 5.78%, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 36 basis points above Treasurys.
The debentures are rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P. GTE California is a unit of GTE Corp.
Morgan Stanley Finance PLC -- $200 million of capital units priced with a 7.8% dividend yield, according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The company sold eight million units priced at $25 apiece.
The units, which are noncallable for five years, are rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.
The units mature in 20 years, if they aren't converted into preferred stock.
The units can convert into preferred anytime after the first year, at the company's option.
The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.
Morgan Stanley Finance is a unit of Morgan Stanley Group Inc.
Property Trust of America -- $200 million, two-part issue of notes priced by underwriters led by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch.
The first tranche, $100 million of notes due Feb. 15, 2008, was priced as 6.875s at 99.691 to yield 6.917%.
The notes were priced at a spread of 120 basis points above Treasurys, and the average life is 10.5 years.
The second tranche, $100 million of notes due Feb. 15, 2014, was priced as 7.5s at 98.902 to yield 7.613%.
The notes were priced at a spread of 120 basis points above Treasurys, and the average life is 18 years.
Both parts are rated Baa2 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, and both amortize.

Amdahl Corp., Sunnyvale, Calif., posted a net loss of $40.9 million, or 36 cents a share, as projected by the struggling maker of mainframe computers.
Amdahl's fourth-quarter loss compared with net income of $2.5 million, or two cents a share, a year earlier.
Sales plunged 41% to $442.9 million from $745 million.
For the year, the company had a much-widened loss of $580 million, or $5.09 a share, compared with a loss of $7 million, or six cents a share.
Sales were $1.68 billion, off 33% from $2.52 billion.
Results for 1993 include after-tax restructuring charges of $394.3 million, or $3.46 a share, and 1992's results include a charge for layoffs of $12.5 million, or 11 cents a share.
Amdahl is attempting to shift its product mix as companies are trading mainframes for desktop networks.

KN Energy Inc. said it signed a definitive agreement to acquire American Oil & Gas Corp. in a stock swap with an indicated value of about $285 million.
Under terms of the proposed transaction, a tax-free exchange of common shares, 0.47 shares of KN Energy will be swapped for each share of American Oil & Gas.
Thus, about 12.2 million KN shares would be involved.
In composite trading yesterday on the New York Stock Exchange, KN closed at $23.375, down 12.5 cents, while American Oil & Gas closed at $10.50, down 12.5 cents.
If completed, American Oil & Gas's current holders would own about 45% of the surviving company.
American Oil & Gas, based in Houston, reported 1993 net income of $6.6 million, or 25 cents a share, including nonrecurring items.
Revenue was $539.3 million.
KN Energy had 1993 net income of $24.3 million, or $1.57 a share, on revenue of $493.3 million.
The proposed transaction is subject to shareholder, regulatory and lender approval.
